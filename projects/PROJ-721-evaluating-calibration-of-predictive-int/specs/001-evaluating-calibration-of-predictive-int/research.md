# Research: Evaluating Calibration of Predictive Intervals in Predictive Intervals in Time Series Forecasting

## Overview

This research evaluates the calibration of predictive intervals generated by four standard time series forecasting models (ARIMA, ETS, Prophet, LightGBM) on the M4 competition dataset. The primary metric is empirical coverage rate at nominal [deferred] and [deferred] levels. The study also investigates systematic mis-calibration across series characteristics (seasonality, trend strength) and explores the efficacy of adaptive conformal prediction for recalibration.

## Methodology

### Models
1. **ARIMA**: `statsmodels.tsa.arima.model.ARIMA`. Auto-selected order (p,d,q) via AIC.
2. **ETS**: `statsmodels.tsa.exponential_smoothing.ets.ETSModel`. Auto-selected error, trend, seasonality components.
3. **Prophet**: `prophet.Prophet`. Default parameters; seasonalities added based on `frequency`.
4. **LightGBM**: `lightgbm.LGBMRegressor`.
 * **Objective**: `quantile` with `alpha=0.1` (for 80% PI) and `alpha=0.05` (for 95% PI).
 * **Features**: Lag features, rolling statistics, time-based features (month, day of week).

### Prediction Intervals
* **ARIMA/ETS**: Asymptotic intervals based on model residuals (assuming normality).
* **Prophet**: Quantile-based intervals from posterior samples (default 1000 samples).
* **LightGBM**: Direct quantile regression (no distributional assumption).
* **Nominal Levels**: [deferred] and [deferred] (FR-003).

### Evaluation Metrics
* **Empirical Coverage**: Proportion of test points falling within the predicted interval.
 * Formula: $Coverage = \frac{1}{N} \sum_{i=1}^{N} \mathbb{I}(y_i \in [L_i, U_i])$
* **Interval Score**: A proper scoring rule combining coverage and width (optional, for robustness).
* **Calibration Deviation**: $|Empirical Coverage - Nominal Coverage|$. Target: ≤2% (SC-002).

### Stratification
* **Seasonality**: Group by `seasonality` (Yes/No) from metadata.
* **Trend Strength**: Derived via STL decomposition on training data.
 * Variance Ratio: $V_{trend} / V_{total}$.
 * Threshold: 'High' if ratio > 0.5, 'Low' otherwise (FR-005).

### Recalibration
* **Method**: Adaptive Conformal Prediction (ACP).
 * Adjusts interval width based on past coverage errors.
 * Applied if initial deviation > 2% (Principle VII).
* **Validation**: Paired bootstrap test with **10,000 resamples** (T039) to compare baseline vs. recalibrated coverage.

### Statistical Significance
* **Multiplicity Correction**: Benjamini-Hochberg FDR (FR-007).
 * Applied to p-values from comparisons across multiple models × 12 horizons (multiple tests).
 * Justification: Standard in forecasting literature (M4/M5 papers); robust to positive dependence.
* **Causal Framing**: All results are associational (FR-009). No claims of "superiority" are made, only "better calibration on this dataset".

## Decision Rationale

* **Subset Size ([deferred] series)**: The full M dataset would exceed 6h runtime on 2 CPU. A subset of [deferred] is statistically sufficient to detect calibration gaps >2% with high power, while maintaining frequency distribution (SC-005).
* **LightGBM Quantile Regression**: Chosen over bootstrap-based quantiles for computational efficiency on CPU. Direct quantile regression is valid and faster.
* **STL for Trend**: Chosen because it is robust to seasonality and computationally efficient.
* **FDR vs. Bonferroni**: FDR is less conservative and more appropriate for 48 simultaneous tests, reducing Type II errors while controlling false discoveries.

## Assumptions & Limitations

* **Assumption**: M4 metadata is complete for `seasonality` and `frequency`.
* **Assumption**: STL decomposition accurately captures trend strength for all series.
* **Limitation**: Power may be limited for detecting small calibration deviations (<1%).
* **Limitation**: ARIMA/ETS intervals assume normality of residuals; if residuals are heavy-tailed, coverage may be poor.
* **Limitation**: LightGBM requires careful feature engineering to avoid overfitting on short series.
