## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon‑vs‑method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question investigates whether visual‑aesthetic attributes of a webpage influence users’ credibility judgments, a substantive phenomenon in social and cognitive psychology. It does not hinge on the performance of any particular analytical method or technology.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictor variables (color scheme, typography, image quality, layout simplicity) are instantiated via independently created HTML/CSS templates, while the predicted variable (perceived credibility) is collected from participants’ Likert ratings. These data sources are distinct, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a significant positive effect and a null effect would be publishable. A positive finding would reinforce the role of superficial design cues in misinformation susceptibility; a null finding would suggest that content dominates credibility assessments, contrary to many anecdotal claims.

### Question‑narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question names a domain relationship (“visual‑aesthetic qualities … affect perceived credibility”) rather than imposing constraints on a particular methodological implementation.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
