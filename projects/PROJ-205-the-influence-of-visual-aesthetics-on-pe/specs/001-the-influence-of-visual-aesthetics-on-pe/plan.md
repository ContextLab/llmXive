# Implementation Plan: The Influence of Visual Aesthetics on Perceived Credibility of Online Information

**Branch**: `001-visual-aesthetics-credibility` | **Date**: 2024-01-15 | **Spec**: `specs/001-visual-aesthetics-credibility/spec.md`
**Input**: Feature specification from `/specs/001-visual-aesthetics-credibility/spec.md`

## Summary

This feature implements a within-subjects randomized experiment to determine if visual aesthetic qualities (Professional, Minimalist, Low-Quality, Neutral) systematically affect perceived credibility of online information, controlling for textual content. The system delivers multiple stimuli in a Latin square randomized order, collects Likert ratings for credibility and professionalism, and executes a statistical analysis pipeline (Repeated-Measures ANOVA + Bonferroni-corrected pairwise t-tests + Mixed-Effects robustness checks) on CPU-only infrastructure.

**Scale/Scope**: N=250 participants (target), stimuli per participant, multiple ratings per participant

## Complexity Tracking

| Violation | Why Needed | Simpler Alternative Rejected Because |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mixed-Effects Models** | Required for US-3 (Robustness) to control for age/education covariates and test interactions. | Simple ANOVA cannot account for participant-level heterogeneity or test moderation effects without violating statistical assumptions. |
| **Latin Square Design** | Required for FR-001 to control order effects in a within-subjects design. | Simple randomization risks order bias (e.g., fatigue or learning effects) which could confound the aesthetic effect. |
| **Streamlit** | Required for rapid, browser-based deployment of the survey without custom frontend code. | Building a custom React/Vue frontend would increase complexity and maintenance burden without adding research value. |
