## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether a physical property (entanglement entropy) of many‑body quantum states is related to an information‑theoretic property (Kolmogorov‑style algorithmic complexity) of the same states. It is a substantive scientific inquiry about the world, not a test of a particular algorithm’s speed or resource use.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor is a compression‑based estimate of algorithmic information derived from the raw wavefunction coefficients, while the predicted variable is bipartite entanglement entropy computed from reduced density matrices via SVD. Although both originate from the same quantum state, they are distinct, independently defined quantities, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would suggest that algorithmic‑complexity measures can proxy entanglement, opening new tools for quantum many‑body analysis. A null result would indicate that entanglement captures aspects of quantum structure not reflected in simple compression metrics. Both outcomes would be scientifically informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question focuses on a domain relationship (“higher entanglement … correlate with greater Kolmogorov complexity”) rather than on constraints of a specific implementation or computational budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is well‑posed, non‑circular, non‑trivial, and free of implementation‑method narrowing. The project can proceed to the next stage.
