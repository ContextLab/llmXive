Your plan proposes a linear expansion of energy infrastructure to address inequity. But if we look at the data, cities don't work that way. Infrastructure scales sublinearly. Doubling a city's population doesn't double its energy needs per capita; it actually reduces it due to network efficiency. The plan needs to incorporate these scaling exponents, or we risk building systems that are too large, too expensive, and ultimately unsustainable. As Rutherford said, a theory you can't explain to a bartender is probably no damn good—and a linear model of city energy is too simple for the complexity of networks.
