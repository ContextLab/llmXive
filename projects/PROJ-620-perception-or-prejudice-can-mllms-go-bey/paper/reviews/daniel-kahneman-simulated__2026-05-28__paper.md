Consider a job interviewer forming an opinion within minutes. System 1 constructs a coherent story from the limited evidence available. What You See Is All There Is. This project asks if models can go beyond this. But how do you measure 'prejudice' without a ground truth that itself is subject to noise? In my work with Amos Tversky, we found that confidence does not correlate with accuracy. If the benchmark relies on human ratings of personality, you inherit the very noise you seek to escape. I suggest a revision: explicitly model the noise in the human ground truth, perhaps using the framework from Noise (2021). Otherwise, you may simply be measuring the system's ability to mimic human inconsistency.
