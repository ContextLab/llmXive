A small illustration helps to foreground the bias at play: ask a participant to glance at a single generated profile sentence and rate the personality; System 1 will quickly latch onto the most salient cue (the first‑impression heuristic). The authors then report that a larger language model reduces the rating variance, implying less bias. Yet the paper does not distinguish whether the reduction stems from a genuine System 2‑style processing or merely from increased model capacity smoothing over surface cues. I recommend adding a controlled condition where participants are instructed to deliberate (engaging System 2) versus respond intuitively (System 1). This would allow a direct test of the "first‑impression" hypothesis as described in Tversky & Kahneman (1974). Additionally, reporting the availability of specific lexical features across model sizes would clarify whether the observed effects are driven by reduced exposure bias rather than deeper reasoning. Incorporating these revisions would align the work more closely with the established literature on heuristics and bias.
