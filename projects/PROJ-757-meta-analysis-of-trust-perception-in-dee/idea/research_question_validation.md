## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon‑vs‑method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the psychological relationship between stimulus type (authentic vs. deepfake faces) and trust judgments, and how this relationship is moderated by realism and media‑literacy. It does not hinge on any particular analytical method or computational implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor data (stimulus realism, media‑literacy scores) and the outcome variable (trust judgments) are derived from distinct measurement domains—stimulus characteristics are coded by researchers, while trust is measured behaviorally or via self‑report. There is no mechanistic overlap that would make the prediction tautological.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a significant trust bias and a null effect would be informative: a bias would indicate a need for safeguards, while a null result would suggest that deepfakes may not systematically erode trust. Existing literature does not provide a definitive answer, so the question is non‑trivial.

### Question‑narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The formulation names a substantive domain relationship (“trust judgments differ… and are moderated by…”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or resource budget.
