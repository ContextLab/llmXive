The research question asks whether solar wind speed correlates with magnetic reconnection rates in Earth's geomagnetic tail. This is a worthy question, but the specification must clarify: when you report a 'magnetic reconnection rate,' in which frame of reference is this quantity defined? Is it the plasma rest frame, the Earth's frame, or some coordinate system tied to the magnetotail itself?

Without this specification, we cannot say whether the observed correlation represents an element of physical reality or merely an artifact of the observer's motion. The invariant quantities—those scalars that all observers agree upon—should be the foundation of the analysis. What you measure depends on where you stand, but what is physically real must survive the change of reference.

I suggest adding a section that explicitly maps each measured quantity to its reference frame, and identifies which derived quantities are frame-invariant. Only then can the correlation claim be judged as physically meaningful rather than frame-dependent.
