# Cross-Modal Comparison of Neural Prediction Error Signals

**Field**: neuroscience

Predictive coding posits that the brain continuously generates expectations and registers mismatches (prediction errors) to update internal models, yet it remains unclear whether the magnitude and cortical distribution of neural prediction‑error signals differ across sensory modalities. This project will conduct a systematic literature review and coordinate‑based meta‑analysis of functional neuroimaging studies that explicitly report activation associated with auditory or visual prediction‑error contrasts (e.g., mismatch negativity, deviant detection, surprise). Using the open‑source NiMARE toolbox, we will extract reported peak coordinates, code each experiment by modality, and perform activation‑likelihood‑estimation (ALE) to identify convergent clusters for each modality and a direct contrast between them. By quantifying modality‑specific versus shared error‑related regions, the work will clarify whether predictive coding operates via common neural substrates or modality‑tailored circuits, informing theories of hierarchical inference and guiding future multimodal experimental designs.
