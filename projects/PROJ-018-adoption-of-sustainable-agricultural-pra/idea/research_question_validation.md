## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about sociological and economic drivers (factors, community engagement) of agricultural behavior, independent of any specific machine learning method. The methodology (logistic regression) is a tool for answering the question, not the subject of the inquiry itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (community engagement intensity) measures social capital and collective action, while the predicted variable (adoption of sustainable practices) measures specific behavioral outcomes. These are distinct constructs typically derived from different survey items rather than being two views of the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While social capital's role in adoption is well-established, quantifying the specific mediation effect in low-income sustainable agriculture contexts remains empirically open. A null result (engagement fails to mediate under certain constraints) would challenge prevailing development models, making both outcomes informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names domain relationships (factors influencing adoption, engagement mediating relationships) rather than implementation constraints (e.g., model accuracy, runtime, specific algorithm architecture). It seeks to explain a phenomenon, not benchmark a tool.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass: the question targets a substantive domain phenomenon, avoids circular construction between variables, offers empirical value in both positive and null outcomes, and focuses on relationships rather than implementation constraints. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
