## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive psychological relationship between emoji usage patterns (frequency, type) and human-perceived emotional intensity in digital communication. This is independent of any specific method or implementation constraint—it is about how nonverbal cues function in text-only media, not about whether a particular algorithm can detect them.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (emoji presence, frequency, and type extracted from message text) and the predicted variable (human-rated emotional intensity) are independently measured. Emoji characteristics are objective features of the message content, while intensity ratings come from human perception tasks or existing annotations, with no mechanical construction linking them.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both positive and null results would be informative and publishable. A positive result would quantify emoji's role as an intensity amplifier in computer-mediated communication, while a null result would challenge assumptions about emoji function and suggest they serve tone clarification rather than intensity modulation. Domain knowledge does not predetermine the answer.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship in psychology (emoji characteristics → perceived emotional intensity) rather than an implementation constraint. It asks "how does X affect Y in the domain" rather than "can method M handle X under constraint Y."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass without concern. The research question targets a genuine gap in understanding emoji's functional role in digital communication, uses independent predictor and outcome measurements, and would yield informative results regardless of direction. The project can proceed to initialization.
