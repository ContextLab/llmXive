## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a causal physical relationship between early-universe CMB anomalies and late-universe large-scale structure statistics. The simulations and perturbation codes are tools to trace this propagation, not the subject of the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (CMB temperature anomalies from Planck maps at z~1100) and predicted variable (large-scale structure statistics from N-body simulations at z~0) are temporally and observationally independent. The simulation models the causal propagation, not a mechanical construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (anomalies propagate to LSS) would constrain non-Gaussianity or topological defect models; a null result (no propagation) would suggest anomalies are statistical flukes or CMB-localized. Either outcome informs cosmological theory and is publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (primordial anomalies → cosmic structure) rather than implementation constraints. Runtime and memory limits appear in methodology only, not in the research question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed for a physics investigation: it asks about a substantive physical relationship, uses independent data sources across cosmic epochs, and either outcome would be scientifically informative. No reframing is needed.
