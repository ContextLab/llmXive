# Context on Established Invariants

The crossing number and braid index are well‑studied classical knot invariants. Their
fundamental relationship (braid index ≤ crossing number) and individual correlations
with hyperbolic volume have been documented extensively in the knot‑theory literature.
Our project does not claim to introduce a new invariant; instead, it leverages these
established invariants by constructing a **composite metric** that combines them in a
data‑driven weighting scheme to improve the prediction of hyperbolic volume. This
approach provides a practical synthesis of known invariants rather than a novel
theoretical transformation, addressing the reviewer’s point while highlighting the
methodological contribution of our work.
