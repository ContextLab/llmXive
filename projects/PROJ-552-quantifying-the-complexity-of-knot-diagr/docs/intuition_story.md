# Narrative Intuition for Composite Invariant

Imagine holding a piece of string, tying it into a knot, and then trying to describe that knot to someone who cannot see it.  You might count crossings or trace the braid, but fundamentally you are telling a story about entanglement.

The crossing number and braid index are classical invariants, each capturing a different facet of a knot’s geometry.  When considered together they hint at a richer structure, much like how a single Fourier mode captures only one frequency component while a full Fourier series reveals the complete shape.

In this project we formalize this intuition by constructing a composite metric that combines crossing number and braid index, aiming to reflect the “story” of a knot’s complexity.
