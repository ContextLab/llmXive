# Intuition: Tying Knots as Storytelling

Imagine holding a piece of string, tying it into a knot, and then trying to describe that knot to someone who cannot see it.  You might count the number of crossings, trace the braid, or note the over‑under pattern, but what you are really doing is weaving a story about entanglement.

This project seeks to formalize that intuition.  The crossing number and the braid index are both classical invariants—each captures a different facet of a knot’s geometry.  Together, however, they suggest a richer, composite picture, much like how a Fourier series combines sine and cosine components to describe a complex signal.

By treating the pair (crossing number, braid index) as a vector‑valued invariant, we can explore whether their joint distribution reveals deeper structural insights into knot complexity.
