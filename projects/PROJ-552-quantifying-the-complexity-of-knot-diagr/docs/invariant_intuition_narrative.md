# Intuitive Narrative for Composite Knot Invariants

Imagine holding a piece of string, tying it into a knot, and then trying to describe that knot to someone who cannot see it. You might count the crossings, trace the braid, but what you are really doing is telling a story about entanglement.

The classical invariants—crossing number and braid index—each capture a different facet of a knot’s geometry. By considering them together we obtain a richer, composite picture, much like how a single Fourier series combines many sine waves to describe a signal.

This narrative guides the development of our composite metric, grounding the mathematics in a tangible, visual intuition.
