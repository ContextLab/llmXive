# Combined Invariant Intuition – A Narrative

Imagine holding a piece of string, tying it into a knot, and then trying to describe that knot to someone who cannot see it.  
Counting crossings or tracing the braid gives you quantitative descriptors, but the real act is telling a story about entanglement.  
The crossing number and braid index are classical invariants that capture different geometric facets of a knot.  
Together they hint at a richer composite invariant, analogous to how a Fourier series combines sine and cosine components to represent a signal.  
This narrative motivates our exploration of combined metrics throughout the project.
