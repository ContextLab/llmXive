## Combined Invariant Intuition

While the crossing number captures how tangled a knot appears in a planar projection and the braid index reflects the minimal number of strands needed to represent the knot as a closed braid, considering them together can reveal richer structure.
Analogous to how a single Fourier coefficient provides limited information but the full series encodes the entire shape, the pair *(crossing number, braid index)* can be thought of as a two‑dimensional “spectral fingerprint” of a knot.
This perspective motivates our composite metric that blends both invariants, offering a more nuanced view of knot complexity.
