# Research Question and Scope

The primary research question of this project is: **How can we quantitatively assess the complexity of knot diagrams using a combination of topological invariants and machine‑learning‑derived metrics?**

To address this, we aim to:
- Compile a comprehensive dataset of knot diagrams from the Knot Atlas.
- Define and compute a suite of classical invariants (crossing number, braid index, hyperbolic volume, etc.).
- Develop composite complexity metrics that integrate these invariants, including entropy‑based and novel learned components.
- Validate the metrics through statistical analysis, correlation studies, and predictive modeling of knot properties.
- Provide reproducible analysis pipelines and visualizations to enable further exploration of knot complexity.

The scope is limited to knots with up to 12 crossings present in the Knot Atlas, and to invariants that can be computed algorithmically with available software libraries.
