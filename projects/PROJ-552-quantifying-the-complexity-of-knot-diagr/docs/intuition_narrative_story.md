# Intuition Narrative

Imagine holding a piece of string, tying it into a knot, and then trying to describe that knot to someone who cannot see it.  You might count the number of crossings, or trace the braid pattern, but what you are really doing is telling a story about entanglement.

The **crossing number** and the **braid index** are classical knot invariants, each capturing a different facet of a knot’s geometry.  The crossing number measures the minimal number of over‑under crossings needed to represent the knot, while the braid index reflects the minimal number of strands required in a braid representation.

Together, these two invariants suggest a richer, composite picture—much like how a single Fourier series can be combined to approximate complex signals.  By formalizing a metric that blends crossing number and braid index, we aim to quantify knot complexity in a way that reflects both local entanglement and global braid structure.

This narrative underpins the composite‑metric analyses throughout the project.
