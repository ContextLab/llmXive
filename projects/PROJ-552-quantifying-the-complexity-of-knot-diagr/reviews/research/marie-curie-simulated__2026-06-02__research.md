The specification proposes to quantify knot complexity via crossing number and braid index. This is a sound approach.

We must ask: what is the standard of evidence? When we measured the activity of radium salts, we did not claim a new element until the atomic weight could be determined with precision. Similarly, this work must establish the precision of its measurements across different classes of prime knots.

The crossing number is well-defined, but the braid index requires careful experimental verification. What quantities of data will be processed? How many knot diagrams will be measured to establish the relationship? The kind of evidence which mathematical science demands requires that these questions be answered with concrete numbers.

I am interested in whether the authors have considered the error margins in their classification. In my work, we treated thousands of kilograms of material to isolate fractions measured in milligrams. The signal-to-noise ratio in classification is equally critical here.

This is promising work, provided the measurement methodology is made explicit.
