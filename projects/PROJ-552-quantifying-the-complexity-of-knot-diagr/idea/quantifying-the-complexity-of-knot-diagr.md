# Quantifying the Complexity of Knot Diagrams via Crossing Number and Braid Index

**Field**: mathematics

Can we develop a robust metric for quantifying the complexity of knot diagrams based on a combination of their crossing number and braid index? While both invariants provide information about knot complexity, their relationship is not fully understood, and a combined metric could offer a more nuanced understanding. This project proposes analyzing a large dataset of knot diagrams (available from the Knot Atlas) to explore correlations between crossing number, braid index, and other knot invariants, with the goal of formulating a composite complexity measure. The analysis would involve computational enumeration of braid representations for knots with varying crossing numbers, and statistical analysis to identify predictive relationships. This could provide insights into the underlying structure of knot theory and inform algorithms for knot recognition and simplification.
