## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive relationship between brain network topology (efficiency metrics) and cognitive ability (fluid intelligence), independent of any specific machine learning method or algorithmic constraint. The core scientific inquiry is about how information-flow properties of brain networks relate to individual differences in cognition.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (graph-theoretical efficiency metrics derived from resting-state fMRI functional connectivity) and the predicted variable (fluid intelligence scores from the NIH Toolbox behavioral assessment) are sourced from independent measurement modalities. The neural data comes from imaging, while the cognitive scores come from psychometric testing—no shared primary signal makes the relationship mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would advance mechanistic models of intelligence by linking network efficiency to cognitive capacity and could inform biomarker development for cognitive health. A null result would be equally informative, suggesting that efficiency metrics do not capture the relevant neural substrate or that other network properties (e.g., modularity, hub distribution) are more predictive. Both outcomes would contribute meaningfully to the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (network efficiency → fluid intelligence) and compares two theoretically motivated efficiency measures (frontoparietal vs. global). It does not name implementation constraints like computation time, model architecture, or hardware requirements as the core research target.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass without significant concerns. The research question is well-posed for a substantive neuroscience investigation into the neural correlates of fluid intelligence, with both positive and null outcomes being scientifically informative and no circularity or method-fixation issues present.
