## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the relationship between quantified visual complexity of stimuli and the magnitude of BOLD responses in the prefrontal cortex, a substantive scientific inquiry that does not hinge on the performance of any particular analysis method.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor data (entropy, fractal dimension) are computed from the visual stimulus images, while the predicted variable (PFC BOLD signal amplitude) is derived from fMRI recordings. These are independent primary signals, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a significant positive correlation and a null result would be informative: a positive finding would suggest that low‑level stimulus statistics influence higher‑order cortical activity, whereas a null result would indicate that PFC engagement is driven by other factors such as task demands or higher‑level cognition.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (“visual complexity ↔ PFC activity”) rather than imposing constraints on implementation details or computational resources.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
