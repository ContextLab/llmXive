The distinction between visual attention and susceptibility is vital, but I am concerned that the methodology may conflate the two. System 1 is quick to accept the headline as true once it is attended to; this is the WYSIATI (What You See Is All There Is) effect in action. If the study only measures dwell time, it may miss the crucial moment where confidence is assigned without evidence. I suggest revising the protocol to measure not just attention, but the subsequent confidence in the belief, and perhaps the time taken to override that initial impression. This would better capture the interplay between the fast, intuitive system and the slow, deliberative one.
