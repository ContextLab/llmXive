## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a relationship between baseline brain network architecture (resting-state functional connectivity) and aesthetic preference (musical genre), independent of any specific ML method or computational constraint. The fMRI and network metric approaches are measurement tools, not the focus of inquiry.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (functional connectivity from BOLD time series) and predicted variable (genre preference from self-report surveys) come from entirely independent data sources. There is no shared primary signal or construction overlap that would mechanically guarantee a relationship.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would inform theories of how individual neural variability shapes aesthetic experience; a null result would suggest musical preference is primarily shaped by cultural/experiential factors outside baseline architecture. Both outcomes are publishable and would advance understanding of neural individual differences.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (brain network architecture → aesthetic preference) rather than implementation constraints. The question asks "how do X relate to Y" in the neuroscience domain, not "can method M handle X under budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed as a substantive scientific inquiry about neural individual differences rather than a method-evaluation or circular construction. The project can proceed to initialization.
