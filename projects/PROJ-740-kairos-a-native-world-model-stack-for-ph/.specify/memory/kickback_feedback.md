## Unresolved concerns

- The plan proposes a fallback to 'synthetic MuJoCo trajectory generator' data because verified sources for Open X-Embodiment/BridgeData V2 are missing. This fundamentally breaks the study's validity: a world model trained on synthetic, rule-based physics data cannot be evaluated for 'open-world' planning fidelity or 'sample efficiency' against real-world distribution shifts. The research question becomes unanswerable as the 'open-world' variable is removed by design.
- The plan claims to measure 'sample efficiency' but restricts training to '~10k training steps' on a CPU-only runner. This sample size is statistically insufficient to train a Transformer or Hybrid Memory model to convergence, let alone compare their learning curves. The resulting metrics will reflect under-training rather than architectural superiority, invalidating the 'sample efficiency' claim.
- The plan acknowledges 'statistical power is low' with N=5 seeds but proceeds with a Wilcoxon signed-rank test. While non-parametric, this test has very low power with n=5. If the effect size is small (expected in early-stage research), the study will almost certainly fail to reject the null hypothesis even if the architecture is superior, leading to a false negative conclusion.
- The assumption that a 'stratified subset' of robotics data is sufficient to train a world model exhibiting 'general trends' of the full dataset is unverified. In world modeling, data diversity (scene, object, lighting) is critical for generalization. A small, stratified subset likely lacks the variance required to test 'open-world' robustness, rendering the 'Long-Horizon Success Rate' metric a measure of overfitting to the subset rather than true planning capability.
- FR-001 requires downloading a stratified subset of Open X-Embodiment/BridgeData V2. The plan (research.md) explicitly states 'NO verified source found' and proposes a fallback to 'synthetic MuJoCo trajectory generator'. This unbacked FR means the plan does not satisfy the spec's requirement to use the specified real-world datasets, risking a failure to validate the hypothesis against the intended data distribution.
- SC-001 requires measuring Long-Horizon Success Rate against a baseline with a specific threshold (+5pp, p<0.05). The plan's fallback to synthetic data (research.md) may not support the 'open-world, safety-critical' conditions required to validly measure this specific success rate metric as defined in the spec, leaving the success criterion untestable in the intended context.

The plan's synthetic data strategy (research.md) generates data with 'physics_error' flags but does not guarantee the complex collision/momentum dynamics of the real Open X/BridgeData datasets required to validly test the 'Physics Consistency' hypothesis, leaving the SC unbacked by a realistic test environment.

Training a 'World Model' on synthetic data generated by the *same* physics engine (MuJoCo) used for evaluation creates a circular validation loop. A valid PCS requires an independent physical check (e.g., energy conservation calculation) not provided in the schema.

The plan does not account for the computational cost of the simulation step itself in the latency budget, potentially making the 'real-time' claim (SC-003) empirically impossible to achieve regardless of model architecture.
