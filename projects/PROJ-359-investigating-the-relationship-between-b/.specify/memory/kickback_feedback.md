## Unresolved concerns

- The plan assumes ds000277 contains 'cognitive training' data to test the hypothesis that baseline connectivity *predicts* training response. However, the research.md explicitly notes this dataset is the 'HCP-1200' release, which typically contains baseline rs-fMRI and behavioral tasks but NOT a longitudinal training intervention with pre/post scores. If the dataset lacks the 'Post-training score' variable, the proposed regression model (WM_Gain ~ Metrics) cannot be computed, rendering the causal claim (prediction of change) untestable with this data source.
- The plan proposes a linear regression to claim that baseline connectivity 'predicts' response to training. This implies a causal or predictive relationship. However, if the dataset is cross-sectional (baseline only) as suspected, the analysis can only test correlation between baseline connectivity and baseline cognition, not 'response to training'. Using a correlational design to support a 'prediction of change' conclusion is a validity flaw.
- The research.md identifies a 'Fatal variable fit flaw' risk: if ds000277 lacks training scores, the hypothesis must change. The plan's mitigation (pivoting to 'Baseline vs Baseline') fundamentally alters the research question from 'predicting response' to 'associating baseline states'. The methodology section must explicitly state that the primary hypothesis (prediction of change) is contingent on data availability, and if unavailable, the study is limited to cross-sectional association, which does not support the original causal/predictive claims.
- The plan sets a motion exclusion threshold of 'mean FD > 3mm'. In rs-fMRI literature, a mean FD > 0.2-0.3mm is often considered high motion; 3.0mm is an extremely lenient threshold that likely includes severe motion artifacts, potentially introducing spurious correlations (motion artifacts often correlate with network topology). This threshold may invalidate the 'Construct Validity' of the connectivity metrics, as they may reflect motion rather than neural dynamics.
