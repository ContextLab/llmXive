## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive relationship between brain network organization and aging, and between network organization and cognitive function. The graph-theoretical metrics are measurement tools, not the subject of the question itself—the phenomenon of interest is how brain network efficiency changes with age and relates to cognition.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (network efficiency derived from EEG functional connectivity) is computed from electrophysiological signals. The predicted variables (age and cognitive performance scores) are independent demographic and behavioral measures collected separately from the EEG recordings. No circular construction exists between these data sources.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would validate network efficiency as a scalable EEG biomarker for cognitive aging. A null result would be equally informative, suggesting either that network efficiency is preserved in normal aging, or that EEG-derived metrics lack the sensitivity to capture age-related cognitive decline. Either outcome advances the field's understanding of brain aging biomarkers.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (network efficiency ↔ age, network efficiency ↔ cognitive performance) rather than implementation constraints. It asks "How does X behave under Y?" which is a substantive neuroscience question, not "Can method M handle X within budget B?"

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-posed as a substantive scientific inquiry about brain aging rather than a method-evaluation question. The predictor and outcome variables are independently measured, and both positive and null findings would be informative to the field. This idea is ready to advance to project initialization.
