## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether a biological relationship exists between pre‑stress metabolite abundances and subsequent recovery performance across species, independent of any specific modeling technique or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictor data are metabolite concentrations measured before stress exposure; the outcome variable is a recovery metric (e.g., biomass, chlorophyll, survival) measured after stress. These come from distinct experimental measurements, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a positive finding (metabolomics forecasts resilience) and a null finding (no predictive signal) would provide novel insight: the former would support a new biomarker avenue, the latter would caution against relying on metabolomics alone.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question names a substantive domain relationship—pre‑stress metabolomic state → post‑stress recovery—rather than imposing constraints on a particular algorithm or resource budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
