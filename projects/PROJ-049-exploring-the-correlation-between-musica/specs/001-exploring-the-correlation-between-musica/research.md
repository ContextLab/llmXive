# Research: Exploring the Correlation Between Musical Preference and Personality Traits

## 1. Research Question & Hypotheses

**Primary Question**: Do individuals with specific Big Five personality traits show statistically significant preferences for particular musical genres, and how strong are these correlations after controlling for demographic variables (age, gender, country)?

**Hypotheses**:
*   **H1 (Openness)**: Higher Openness scores will be positively correlated with preferences for complex/innovative genres (e.g., Classical, Jazz, Electronic) and negatively correlated with conventional genres (e.g., Country, Pop).
*   **H2 (Extraversion)**: Higher Extraversion scores will be positively correlated with high-energy genres (e.g., Hip-Hop, Electronic, Rock).
*   **H3 (Conscientiousness)**: Higher Conscientiousness scores will correlate with traditional/structured genres (e.g., Country, Folk).
*   **H4 (Neuroticism)**: Higher Neuroticism scores will correlate with emotionally intense genres (e.g., Metal, Alternative).
*   **H5 (Agreeableness)**: Higher Agreeableness scores will correlate with soft/relaxing genres (e.g., Pop, Folk).

## 3. Statistical Methodology

### 3.1 Correlation Analysis
*   **Method**: Spearman Rank Correlation ($\rho$).
*   **Rationale**: Personality scores and listening minutes are not necessarily normally distributed; Spearman is robust to outliers and non-linearity.
*   **Execution**: Compute $\rho$ for each of the 5 traits against each of the $N$ genres (10).
*   **Multiple Comparison Correction**: Apply **Benjamini-Hochberg (BH) FDR** correction to the resulting $5 \times 10 = 50$ p-values (FR-005). Significance threshold $q < 0.05$.
*   **Effect Sizes**: Report Spearman's $\rho$ with bootstrapped 95% Confidence Intervals. Additionally, calculate Pearson's $r$ on **log-transformed** minutes (where normality is improved) and Fisher's $z$ for these secondary metrics, clearly labeled as `effect_size_r_log`.

### 3.2 Regression Analysis
*   **Method**: Linear Mixed Models (LMM) with random intercepts for `user_id`.
*   **Rationale**: The data is long-format (one row per user-genre pair). Simple OLS violates the independence of observations assumption. LMM accounts for the non-independence.
*   **Model**: $GenreScore \sim Trait + Age + Gender + Country + (1|user\_id)$.
*   **Covariates**: Age (numeric), Gender (binary/dummy), Country (grouped).
*   **Output**: Beta coefficients, Standard Errors, p-values.
*   **Collinearity Check**: Calculate Variance Inflation Factor (VIF). If $VIF > 5$, drop the offending covariate and log a warning.
*   **Coefficient Delta (SC-003)**: Run two models: (1) Baseline (Trait only) and (2) Full (Trait + Covariates). Calculate $\Delta\beta = \beta_{full} - \beta_{baseline}$ and Cohen's $d$ for the delta.

### 3.3 Causal Framing & DAG
*   **DAG**: A Directed Acyclic Graph will be constructed to justify the selection of demographic covariates (age, gender, country) as sufficient confounders.
*   **Assumption**: These demographics are assumed to be the primary confounders. A sensitivity analysis will be performed to check for omitted variable bias.

## 5. Decision Rationale

*   **Why Synthetic Proxy?**: The primary data strategy (joining OpenML BFI-2 and Last.fm) is infeasible due to zero user overlap. A synthetic proxy allows the pipeline to be developed, tested, and demonstrated reproducibly without fabricating results from a non-existent real dataset. The synthetic data is generated using a fixed seed and realistic distributions to ensure the statistical methods are valid.
*   **Why Spearman?**: Listening data is highly skewed (power law distribution of artists). Pearson would be sensitive to outliers; Spearman provides a rank-based association.
*   **Why BH FDR?**: With 5 traits $\times$ 10 genres = 50 tests, Bonferroni is overly conservative. BH controls the False Discovery Rate while maintaining power.
*   **Why LMM?**: The data structure (multiple genres per user) violates the independence assumption of OLS. LMM is the standard method for this design.
*   **Why no conversion of Spearman to Pearson?**: The conversion is methodologically unsound. We report Spearman's $\rho$ with bootstrapped CIs as the primary metric. Pearson's $r$ is calculated only on log-transformed data as a secondary, clearly labeled metric.
*   **Dataset Strategy**: The plan strictly adheres to the verified dataset IDs (OpenML 42473, HF `lastfm/lastfm_k`) for reference, but the primary execution path uses the **Verified Synthetic Proxy** generated by `code/synthetic_data.py`.
