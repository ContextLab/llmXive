The question asks about the relationship between brain signal dynamics (neural entropy) and behavioral capacity (cognitive flexibility), independent of the specific entropy algorithm or hardware used. It focuses on the biological link rather than methodological benchmarking or computational performance constraints.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor comes from resting-state EEG recordings while the predicted variable comes from behavioral task performance (e.g., Wisconsin Card Sorting Test). These are independent measurement modalities (electrophysiology vs. psychometrics) and are not derived from the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While aging generally reduces both entropy and flexibility, the specific predictive link remains empirically unverified in this population. A positive correlation confirms biomarker utility, while a null result would challenge the 'entropic brain' framework's applicability to normal aging, making both outcomes informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question specifies a domain relationship (neural complexity predicting behavioral adaptability) rather than implementation constraints like runtime or specific hardware. It frames the inquiry around the physiological phenomenon, not the computational pipeline.

### Overall verdict

The research question is substantive, empirically testable, and avoids circular or implementation-focused framing. It successfully isolates the relationship between neural dynamics and behavior without relying on predetermined outcomes or methodological artifacts.
