Table~\ref{tab:positioning} compares \method{} with the prior methods discussed in Section~\ref{sec:related}.
For each method we report the harness surface it edits, the feedback signal that drives it, and the cost regime it incurs, and we mark whether it meets \method{}'s setting on three criteria, namely whether optimization is label-free, whether it edits the full harness, and whether it runs as a single offline retrospective pass.
Validation-feedback optimizers reach the full-harness end of the surface axis, where ADAS and Meta-Harness rewrite executable code, but every member of this family steers the search with a ground-truth metric and iterates against it.
Experience-based methods drop the labels, yet they only curate memory or skill text online and leave the executable harness untouched, while Sleep-time Compute precomputes context offline but optimizes nothing.
As a result, each prior method satisfies at most two of the three axes, and \method{} is the only entry that satisfies all three at once.
