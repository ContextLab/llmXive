## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about ecological relationships between microbial community composition and ecosystem function (nutrient removal) in constructed wetlands. The methodology (16S sequencing, network analysis, regression) is a means to investigate the phenomenon, not the question itself. The core inquiry is about biological succession patterns and functional prediction, independent of any specific algorithm or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (microbial taxa abundances from 16S rRNA sequencing) and the predicted variable (nitrogen/phosphorus removal efficiency from water chemistry measurements) are derived from independent data sources. Community composition comes from biological sequencing while nutrient removal comes from chemical analysis of influent/effluent water. There is no mechanical guarantee of the relationship by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result identifying predictive taxa would provide actionable biomarkers for wetland monitoring and management optimization. A null result would be equally informative by suggesting that community composition is less important than environmental or hydraulic factors for nutrient removal. Neither outcome is predetermined by existing domain knowledge, making both scientifically valuable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a clear domain relationship: microbial community structure → nutrient removal efficiency during wetland succession. It does not reference specific algorithms, computational budgets, or implementation constraints as the core inquiry. The framing is appropriately ecological rather than methodological.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question addresses a genuine gap in understanding microbial succession dynamics in engineered ecosystems, uses independent data sources for prediction and outcome variables, and would yield informative results regardless of direction. The question is ready to advance to project initialization.
