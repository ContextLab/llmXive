# Robustness of Confidence Intervals to Differential Privacy Noise  

**Field**: statistics  

## Research question

How does the magnitude of differential‑privacy noise (controlled by the privacy budget ε) affect the frequentist coverage probability of confidence intervals for simple means and linear‑regression coefficients?

## Motivation

Differential privacy (DP) is increasingly required when releasing statistical summaries, yet practitioners still rely on conventional confidence intervals that assume noise‑free data. If DP noise substantially distorts interval coverage, inferential conclusions may be misleading. Quantifying this effect and identifying simple adjustments would give analysts concrete guidance on the trade‑off between privacy guarantees and statistical reliability.

## Related work

- [Unbiased Statistical Estimation and Valid Confidence Intervals Under Differential Privacy (2021)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.14465) — Proposes unbiased estimators and construction of valid DP confidence intervals, providing methodological baselines for our bias‑correction experiments.  
- [Finite Sample Differentially Private Confidence Intervals (2017)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03908) — Studies DP confidence intervals for the mean under known/unknown variance, offering theoretical coverage bounds we can compare against empirical results.  
- [Differentially Private Inference for Binomial Data (2019)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.00459) — Derives DP hypothesis tests and confidence intervals for proportions, illustrating techniques for adjusting variance that may extend to linear‑regression settings.  
- [Differentially Private Federated Learning: Servers Trustworthiness, Estimation, and Statistical Inference (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.16287) — Investigates DP inference in high‑dimensional settings, highlighting challenges that motivate our focus on low‑dimensional mean and regression models.  
- [Statistical Inference for Differentially Private Stochastic Gradient Descent (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.20560) — Explores DP‑SGD inference, underscoring the broader relevance of reliable CI construction under DP noise.  
- [Differentially private ratio statistics (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.20351) — Provides DP methods for ratio‑type statistics, demonstrating the diversity of DP inference problems and motivating a systematic coverage study.

## Expected results

We anticipate that coverage will systematically decline as ε decreases (i.e., noise increases), with Laplace noise causing larger degradation than Gaussian noise for the same ε. Simple bias‑correction or variance‑inflation adjustments derived from the unbiased‑estimation literature are expected to recover nominal 95 % coverage for moderate privacy budgets (ε ≥ 1) but may fail for very stringent privacy (ε ≤ 0.5). Demonstrating these patterns will furnish quantitative guidelines for practitioners.

## Methodology sketch

- **Data acquisition**: Download UCI Adult, Iris, and Wine Quality datasets via their public URLs (CSV format).  
- **Population reference**: For each dataset, treat the full original sample as a proxy for the population; compute true means and OLS coefficients (and their exact sampling distribution via bootstrap) to serve as ground‑truth parameters.  
- **DP noise generation**: For each ε ∈ {0.1, 0.5, 1, 2, 5} and for each noise type (Laplace, Gaussian), add calibrated DP noise to every numeric attribute according to the standard Laplace/Gaussian mechanisms (sensitivity = range of attribute).  
- **Repeated sampling**: For each noisy dataset, draw 1 000 bootstrap resamples (with replacement) of size equal to the original dataset, recompute the statistic of interest (mean or regression coefficient) and its conventional (non‑DP) 95 % confidence interval.  
- **Coverage assessment**: Compute the proportion of bootstrap CIs that contain the corresponding ground‑truth parameter (empirical coverage).  
- **Adjustment evaluation**: Implement the unbiased estimator and variance‑inflation corrections from the 2021 and 2017 papers; repeat the bootstrap coverage assessment on the adjusted intervals.  
- **Statistical analysis**: Plot coverage vs. ε for each dataset, noise type, and statistic; conduct a two‑way ANOVA to test the effects of ε and noise type on coverage.  
- **Reproducibility**: All code written in Python (NumPy, pandas, statsmodels, scipy); scripts will be modular so each step runs in ≤10 minutes on a GitHub Actions runner (2 CPU, 7 GB RAM).
