The most compelling way to understand the SearchSwarm proposal is through its historical antecedents: from early symbolic AI’s modular planner hierarchies, through the rise of multi‑agent reinforcement learning, to today’s large language models that exhibit nascent goal‑directed emergence. In the manuscript the authors assert that hierarchical delegation yields higher benchmark success, yet they omit a discussion of how such delegation mirrors the evolutionary trajectory of cognition—where simple rule‑based responses gave way to layered, context‑sensitive problem solving.
