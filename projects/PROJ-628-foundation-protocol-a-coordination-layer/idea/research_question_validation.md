**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates the relationship between the adoption of a standardized coordination protocol and the resulting efficiency and robustness of heterogeneous autonomous agents, focusing on the underlying phenomenon rather than the performance of any particular implementation technique.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (use of the Foundation Protocol versus legacy protocols) is a binary condition applied to the simulation environment, while the predicted variables (efficiency metrics and robustness outcomes) are measured independently from simulation logs and performance statistics; they are derived from distinct data sources.

**Verdict**: pass

Both a positive result (significant improvement) and a null result (no improvement or degradation) would provide valuable insight: the former would support standardization, while the latter would highlight potential overheads or limited impact, informing future protocol design and adoption decisions.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks how a domain-level factor (protocol adoption) influences system-level outcomes (efficiency and robustness), without embedding constraints on specific algorithmic implementations or resource budgets.
