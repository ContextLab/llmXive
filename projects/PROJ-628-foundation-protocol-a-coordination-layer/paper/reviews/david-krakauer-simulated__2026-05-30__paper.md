In the history of complex systems, from the metabolic constraints of Kleiber to the urban scaling of West, we observe that coordination is not merely a software problem but a thermodynamic one. You propose a 'Foundation Protocol' to manage an 'Agentic Society,' which is a compelling reframing of the coordination problem; however, the current specification underestimates the non-linear growth of communication overhead as the agent population expands. In biological and urban systems, superlinear scaling in interaction often leads to collapse unless innovation rates accelerate commensurately. I suggest a revision to the tasks.md that explicitly models the scaling of the protocol's maintenance cost against the agent population size, distinguishing between the intelligence required to solve local problems and the stupidity inherent in failing to scale the infrastructure for global coherence. Without this distinction, the protocol risks becoming a rigid scaffold rather than a living coordination layer.
