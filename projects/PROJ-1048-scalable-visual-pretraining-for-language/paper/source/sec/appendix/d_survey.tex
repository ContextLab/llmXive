% \noindent\textbf{Math Continuous Pretraining in LLM.} 

% Building large language models(LLMs) that can perform sophisticated mathematical reasoning is inherently challenging, demanding a deep understanding of abstract representations, complex notations, and advanced mathematical principles~\citep{wang2025survey}. Extensive studies~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath,ying2024internlm,yang2024qwen25mathtechnicalreportmathematical,allal2025smollm2,bakouch2025smollm3} have therefore focused on strengthening their mathematical reasoning abilities and narrowing the performance gap with leading proprietary models such as GPT-5~\citep{gpt5} and Gemini~\citep{geminipro2.5} in mathematical domains. To this end, these approaches typically involve continuous pretraining on domain-specific corpora as the initial and essential step, allowing LLMs to acquire mathematical knowledge from diverse sources. 
% The raw mathematical corpora commonly used for such pretraining include arXiv papers rich in mathematical content~\citep{lewkowycz2022solving,taylor2022galactica,azerbayev2023proofnet}, textbooks and monographs~\citep{allal2025smollm2,fan2025megascience,lu2025scp}, and data crawled from mathematical web pages or code~\citep{hendrycks2021measuring,paster2023openwebmath,han2024infimm, zhou2025megamath}. In addition, synthetic datasets generated through rule-based systems, mathematical programming scripts or LLMs are often incorporated to further enrich the training data~\citep{zhang2024automathtext,zhou2025megamath,yang2024qwen25mathtechnicalreportmathematical,zhou2024jiuzhang3,lin2024rho,toshniwal2024openmath2}.
% However, these textual corpora inevitably lose critical information during extraction, such as geometric figures, visual context, layout, font style, and spatial semantics. Our method alleviates this limitation by facilitating the use of visual-modality corpora that lie beyond the scope of traditional text-based pretraining, thereby improving the diversity and representational capacity of the pretraining data.

% \noindent\textbf{Optical Language Modeling}
% Optical Language Modeling treats language as a visual modality input by converting textual data into visual modalities (such as rendered text images or font textures). It then uses visual encoders (\eg, CLIP~\citep{radford2021learning,zhai2023sigmoid}, VAE~\citep{kingma2013auto}, or ViT~\citep{dosovitskiy2020image}) to transform these images into feature representations. Unlike language models such as BERT~\citep{devlin2019bert}, GPT series~\citep{radford2019language,brown2020language}, or T5~\citep{raffel2020exploring}, which directly model sequences of textual tokens, Optical Language Modeling performs language understanding and generation through visual representations instead of text tokens. Several pioneering studies~\citep{rust2022language,tschannen2023clippo,mansimov2020towards,sun2019squared} have explored modeling language directly at the pixel level by rendering text into images and learning semantic information from these visual representations to perform various tasks (\eg, sentiment analysis~\citep{sun2019squared}).  Although these methods achieve latent cross-modal semantic alignment, they cannot perform cross-modal decoding. 
% %Nevertheless, this approach has inspired a series of studies that process not only textual data but also images, charts, and tables through a unified visual input system and build visually-situated language understanding models~\citep{lee2023pix2struct}. 
% With the rapid development of LLMs and multimodal alignment techniques, multimodal large language models (MLLMs)~\citep{ye2023ureader, chen2024far, chen2024expanding,wang2025internvl3, bai2025qwen2_5,gpt4v,lu2024deepseekvl,liu2024llavanext,liu2023llava, Qwen2-VL,li2023blip2,hong2025glm_v_thinking} have demonstrated remarkable perceptual abilities in OCR-related tasks. Trained on high-quality OCR-oriented datasets~\citep{gu2022wukong,mathew2021docvqa,sidorov2020textcaps,singh2021textocr}, these models can effectively understand not only text-centric images but also natural images containing text, charts, tables, and even long-form documents, thereby exhibiting comprehensive visually-grounded language understanding capabilities.
