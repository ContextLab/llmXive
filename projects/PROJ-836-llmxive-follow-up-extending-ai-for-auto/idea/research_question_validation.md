## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive relationship between the structural topology of AI-generated knowledge graphs (specifically cycle density and isolation) and the empirical validity of downstream research outputs (experimental failure and novelty degradation). It does not frame the inquiry around whether a specific algorithm or hardware constraint can achieve a benchmark, but rather investigates if a natural structural pattern exists as a leading indicator of failure.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (topological metrics like cycle density) is derived from the citation graph constructed from the *text* of the literature reviews, while the predicted variable (experimental failure/novelty degradation) is derived from the *empirical results* of the autonomous agent's subsequent execution phase. These are distinct data sources: one is a structural summary of the input planning phase, and the other is a ground-truth label from the output validation phase, ensuring the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would be highly publishable as it would provide a lightweight, pre-execution filter for autonomous research pipelines, solving a critical cost-efficiency problem. A null result would also be informative, suggesting that hallucinations in AI research are semantic or logical in nature rather than topological, thereby directing future efforts away from graph-based heuristics and toward semantic validation methods.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship: the correlation between specific graph anomalies (high cycle density, citation isolation) and the quality of scientific claims generated by autonomous agents. It avoids narrowing the scope to implementation constraints like "can method X run in 6 hours" and instead asks "does phenomenon Y predict outcome Z."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass: the question targets a genuine scientific relationship between graph topology and research validity, uses independent data sources for prediction and outcome, offers publishable value in both positive and null scenarios, and avoids implementation-specific narrowing. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
