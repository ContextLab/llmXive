## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates a physical relationship between atmospheric states and seismic events, independent of the statistical tools used to test it. It does not frame the inquiry around the performance of a specific algorithm or hardware constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (atmospheric pressure from reanalysis data) and the outcome (earthquake occurrence from seismic catalogs) are derived from independent physical measurement systems. There is no mechanical construction linking the two datasets that would guarantee a correlation.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would validate a controversial precursor hypothesis with implications for monitoring, while a null result would rigorously close a persistent debate in geophysics. Neither outcome is predetermined by current consensus, as existing literature reports mixed evidence.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The inquiry focuses on the existence of a geophysical phenomenon (pressure anomalies preceding quakes) rather than implementation constraints like runtime or model architecture. It asks about the behavior of the Earth system, not the limits of the analysis pipeline.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass because the research question targets a substantive scientific relationship using independent data modalities. The potential outcomes are scientifically informative regardless of direction, and the framing avoids implementation-specific constraints.
