## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether a minimal leptophilic dark‑matter model can produce a loop contribution that resolves the muon (g‑2) anomaly while satisfying independent cosmological, direct‑detection, and collider constraints. It is a substantive inquiry about the physics of the model, not about the performance of a particular computational or experimental method.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor is the set of model parameters (mediator mass, DM mass, couplings) and the predicted quantities are distinct observables: the muon anomalous magnetic moment shift, relic density, spin‑independent scattering cross‑section, and collider limits. These observables are measured or bounded by independent experiments, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (identifying a viable parameter region) would provide a concrete, testable link between dark matter and the muon (g‑2) anomaly, meriting publication. A negative result (no viable region) would be equally informative, demonstrating that leptophilic DM cannot simultaneously address both sets of constraints, which is also publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question frames a scientific inquiry about the compatibility of a class of models with multiple physical phenomena, rather than imposing a constraint on a specific implementation or computational resource.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, indicating that the research question is well‑posed, scientifically interesting, and free of methodological or circularity flaws.
