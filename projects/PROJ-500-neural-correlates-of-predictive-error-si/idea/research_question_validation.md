## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates the relationship between a neural error signal (somatosensory MMN) and behavioral learning performance. It is framed as a hypothesis about brain-behavior dynamics consistent with predictive coding theory, independent of any specific algorithm or computational method.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (MMN amplitude) is derived from EEG neural responses to deviant stimuli, while the predicted variable (behavioral accuracy) is derived from task response logs. These are distinct measurement modalities, so the relationship is empirical rather than mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While predictive coding theory suggests error attenuation with learning, the specific trajectory in the tactile modality remains empirically unquantified. Both a confirmation (domain generality) and a null result (modality-specific mechanisms) would provide informative contributions to the literature.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a relationship in the neuroscience domain (neural signal vs. learning curve) rather than imposing constraints on data acquisition or analysis pipelines. It focuses on the underlying mechanism of learning rather than the feasibility of a specific tool.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is scientifically sound and appropriately framed for empirical investigation. The project addresses a genuine gap in tactile predictive coding literature without falling into method-centric or circular pitfalls.
