## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive relationship between brain network dynamics (integration/segregation) and creative performance ability. It is framed as a domain question about neural mechanisms underlying improvisation skill, independent of any specific computational method or algorithm's performance.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (functional connectivity metrics from fMRI BOLD signal) and the predicted variable (improvisation skill from expert behavioral ratings or pre-study assessments) derive from independent measurement modalities. The network metrics summarize temporal correlations in brain activity, while skill ratings come from performance evaluation—no mechanical construction links them.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would support theories linking network flexibility to creative cognition and could inform training interventions. A null result would also be informative, suggesting either that network dynamics don't capture the relevant neural mechanisms or that improvisation skill is determined by other factors (e.g., local circuit properties, specific task representations). Both outcomes are publishable and advance domain understanding.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a clear domain relationship (brain network dynamics → improvisation skill) rather than implementation constraints. It does not fixate on specific algorithms, hardware budgets, or runtime requirements as the core question.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four validation checks pass. The research question is appropriately framed as a substantive scientific inquiry about neural mechanisms of creative performance, with independent measurements for predictor and outcome, and outcomes that would be informative in either direction.
