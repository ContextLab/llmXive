## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the biological mechanism linking genotype and environment to metabolite production. It is independent of any specific statistical method or computational budget. The focus remains on the "how" of the biological relationship rather than the "can" of a specific algorithm.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictors (genomic variants, climate metadata) and outcome (defense compound concentration) are derived from distinct biological layers and measurement modalities. There is no shared primary signal between the input features and the target variable. Genomic data comes from sequencing while compounds come from chemical assays.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both positive (identifying adaptive drivers) and null (suggesting stochasticity or unmeasured biotic factors) results would constrain ecological theory on defense allocation. A null result is not a foregone conclusion given the known plasticity of plant defenses. Either outcome provides new evidence regarding the predictability of secondary metabolite production.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a relationship in the domain (G x E interaction on metabolites) rather than a constraint on the implementation. It asks for the nature of the influence rather than the performance of a specific tool. This keeps the scientific inquiry open to whatever the data reveals.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

The research question is scientifically substantive and avoids the failure modes of method-narrowing or circular construction. It is ready for project initialization without needing reframing. The core inquiry into genotype-environment interactions remains the focus.
