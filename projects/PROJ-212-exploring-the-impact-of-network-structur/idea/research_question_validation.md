## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a relationship between network topology (degree distribution, clustering, path length) and dynamical behavior (synchronization robustness) in coupled oscillator systems. The Kuramoto dynamics is the modeling framework, not the method being evaluated—the question is about whether structural properties predict dynamical outcomes, which is a substantive scientific relationship independent of implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (topological features) is derived from the static network adjacency structure, while the predicted variable (synchronization robustness) is derived from dynamical simulations of oscillators evolving on that network. These are independent signals: structure versus emergent dynamical behavior. The relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome would be informative to the network physics community. A positive result (specific topological features predict synchronization thresholds) would enable design principles for resilient coupled systems. A null result (topology alone is insufficient) would suggest that synchronization robustness depends on dynamical parameters or heterogeneity beyond simple metrics, also advancing theoretical understanding.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (network structure → synchronization dynamics) rather than implementation constraints. It asks "does X predict Y" in the physics domain, not "can method M handle X under budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed as a substantive physics question about the structure-dynamics relationship in complex networks. The methodology (Kuramoto simulations, topological feature extraction) serves the question rather than becoming the question itself. This idea is ready to advance to project initialization.
