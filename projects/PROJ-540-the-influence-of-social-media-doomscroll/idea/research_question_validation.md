## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether a behavioral phenomenon (frequency of negative‑news consumption on social media) predicts a psychological phenomenon (elevated anticipatory anxiety), without tying the answer to any particular analytical technique or platform constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictor data come from self‑reported usage patterns, while the predicted variable is a standardized anxiety score; they are measured from distinct questionnaire items and thus are not mechanically derived from the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a positive association and a null finding would be scientifically informative: a positive result would support theories linking doomscrolling to anxiety, while a null result would suggest other factors dominate anticipatory anxiety, guiding future research and interventions.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question frames a substantive domain relationship (“does X predict Y”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method, dataset, or computational resource.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
