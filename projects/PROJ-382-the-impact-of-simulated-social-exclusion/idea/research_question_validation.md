## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a causal relationship between social exclusion and prosocial behavior, independent of any specific analytical method or algorithm. It focuses on the psychological phenomenon rather than implementation details like model architecture or computational constraints.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (experimental exclusion condition manipulated via Cyberball or similar tasks) and predicted variable (monetary donation behavior) are measured independently through separate tasks and behavioral outcomes. They do not derive from the same primary signal or computational construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

The literature shows inconsistent findings on this relationship—some studies find exclusion reduces prosociality, others find it increases prosocial compensation, and some find null effects. Either a significant negative correlation or a null result would be informative and contribute to resolving this debate in the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (exclusion → prosociality) rather than implementation constraints. The resource limits (CPU, 6 hours, 7GB RAM) appear only in the methodology section, not in the research question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed as a substantive psychological inquiry with independent predictor and outcome measures, non-trivial expected findings given the inconsistent literature, and clear domain focus without implementation narrowing.
