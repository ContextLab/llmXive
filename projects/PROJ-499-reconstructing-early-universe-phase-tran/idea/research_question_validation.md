## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive physical relationship: whether causal, non-inflationary phase transitions in the early universe produce distinct signatures in CMB B-mode polarization. The methodology (Planck/BICEP/Keck data, MCMC fitting, Bayes factors) is mentioned as the means of testing, but the question itself is about cosmological physics, not method performance.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (theoretical phase transition vs. inflationary tensor spectra) is derived from distinct physical models, while the predicted variable (observed CMB B-mode power spectra) comes from independent observational data (Planck and BICEP/Keck measurements). These are not derived from the same primary signal, so there is no mechanical guarantee of correlation.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes are scientifically informative: a positive detection would provide evidence for non-inflationary early-universe physics, while a null result would tighten constraints on alternative cosmological models and narrow the viable parameter space for phase transitions. Either result advances our understanding of the universe's first moments.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (phase transition signatures vs. inflationary signals in CMB B-modes) rather than implementation constraints. It asks "do these physical phenomena produce distinguishable signatures" instead of "can method M handle X within budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is substantive, independent of methodology, free of circularity, and non-trivial. The project can proceed to initialization without requiring reframing.
