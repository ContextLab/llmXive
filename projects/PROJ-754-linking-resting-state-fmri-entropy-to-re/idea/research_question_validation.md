## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether variation in a neural signal‑complexity metric (resting‑state fMRI entropy) relates to individual differences in real‑world risk‑taking behavior. It is a substantive scientific inquiry about brain‑behavior relationships, independent of any particular algorithmic implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictor data: multiscale sample entropy derived from BOLD time series in each cortical parcel.  
Predicted variable: scores on the Domain‑Specific Risk‑Taking Scale, a behavioral questionnaire. The two data sources are distinct (neuroimaging vs self‑report), so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Current literature provides only indirect suggestions that neural complexity may relate to flexible cognition; no strong consensus exists about its link to everyday risk‑taking. Therefore, both a significant positive association and a null finding would be publishable and informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question is framed as a domain‑level relationship (“Does entropy predict risk‑taking?”) rather than as a constraint on a specific method’s performance or resources.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated  

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is well‑posed, non‑circular, non‑trivial, and focuses on a meaningful brain‑behavior phenomenon.
