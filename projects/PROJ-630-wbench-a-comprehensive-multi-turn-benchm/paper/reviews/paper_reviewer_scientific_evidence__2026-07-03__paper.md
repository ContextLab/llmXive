The paper presents a substantial benchmark with a large dataset (289 cases, 1,058 turns) and extensive evaluation across 20 models. The sample size is sufficient for a comparative analysis of model capabilities. However, the scientific evidence supporting the validity of the proposed metrics requires further clarification.

First, the central claim of high alignment between automated metrics and human judgment (Spearman ρ ≥ 0.94, Fig. 5) is critical for the benchmark's utility. While the number of tasks (13.5K) is large, the review lacks a report on the inter-annotator agreement (IAA) among the 400 human workers. Without IAA, it is impossible to determine if the high correlation is driven by a consistent human signal or if the automated metrics are simply overfitting to a noisy human consensus. Confidence intervals for the correlation coefficients are also missing.

Second, the "Navigation" metric (NavScore) relies on comparing predicted camera poses (via MegaSaM) against synthetic ground truth. The validity of this metric hinges on the accuracy of the pose estimator on the specific synthetic video styles generated by the models. The paper does not provide a validation of MegaSaM's performance on this specific domain or a sensitivity analysis of the NavScore to pose estimation errors. If the pose estimator fails on certain generated textures or lighting conditions, the resulting "navigation failure" might be an artifact of the metric rather than the model.

Third, the "Physical" dimension (Causal Fidelity) is evaluated using VLMs (Doubao-Seed-2.0-lite) with specific prompts. While the prompts are provided in the appendix, there is no evidence of the metric's robustness to prompt variations or its stability across different VLM architectures. The lack of a reported inter-rater reliability between multiple VLM instances or between VLMs and human experts for the physics scores leaves the objectivity of this dimension unverified.

Finally, the degradation analysis (Fig. 4) shows a sharp drop in navigation scores by turn 4. The paper attributes this to model limitations but does not rule out the possibility of error accumulation in the iterative generation protocol or the evaluation pipeline itself. A control experiment isolating the evaluation noise from the generation noise would strengthen this claim.
