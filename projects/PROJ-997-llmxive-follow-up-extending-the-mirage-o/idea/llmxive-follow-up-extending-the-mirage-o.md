# llmXive follow-up: extending "The Mirage of Optimizing Training Policies: Monotonic Inference Polici"

## Summary of the prior work
This paper identifies a critical "training-inference mismatch" in LLM Reinforcement Learning where separate training and inference engines cause probability inconsistencies, leading to objective misalignment where training improvements do not guarantee deployment gains. The authors propose Monotonic Inference Policy Improvement (MIPI) as a new objective and implement it via Monotonic Inference Policy Update (MIPU), a two-step framework that uses sampler-referenced updates and an inference-side gap proxy to selectively accept policy updates. Experiments demonstrate that MIPU enhances reasoning performance and stability under high-mismatch conditions like FP8 quantization compared to standard RL baselines.

## Proposed extension
Can we replace the computationally expensive, inference-engine-dependent "inference-side gap proxy" in MIPU with a lightweight, CPU-tractable analytical estimator derived solely from the training engine's logits and the known quantization error bounds of the inference engine? This direction matters because the current MIPU Step 2 requires synchronizing the candidate model to a separate inference engine (e.g., vLLM) for every update evaluation, creating a significant bottleneck that prevents real-time or large-scale deployment; a CPU-only estimator would enable continuous, low-latency policy acceptance without the overhead of full engine synchronization.

## Methodology sketch
We will construct a synthetic dataset of token-level probability distributions and corresponding "ground truth" inference probabilities generated by simulating FP8 quantization noise on high-precision logits (using standard floating-point arithmetic on a CPU). The procedure involves training a lightweight regression model (e.g., a small MLP or kernel ridge regression) on this dataset to predict the "inference gap" (the divergence between the synchronized inference policy and the training policy) based only on the training engine's raw logits, gradient norms, and a pre-computed quantization error vector. We expect the results to show that this analytical estimator correlates strongly ($r > 0.85$) with the actual gap measured by a full inference engine, allowing the MIPU framework to operate with a 90% reduction in synchronization overhead while maintaining comparable training stability and final reasoning scores on standard math benchmarks.
