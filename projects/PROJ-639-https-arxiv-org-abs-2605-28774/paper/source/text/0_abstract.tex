\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Abstract}\\
Vision-language models with extended reasoning succeed on complex problems, but many real-world problems require external tools that internal reasoning alone often cannot resolve. Agentic reasoning therefore interleaves two behaviors with a structural asymmetry: thinking (the self-contained default) and tool use (a high-variance auxiliary acting). We refer to this asymmetry as the \emph{Thinking-Acting Gap}. Under standard RL recipes like GRPO, the gap manifests as two diagnostic symptoms during training: tool use is attempted on only $\sim$$30\%$ of rollouts, and when attempted, the tool-using rollouts within a group are all-wrong on $\sim$$40\%$ of questions, suppressing the learning signal at the tool calls that needed it. We propose \textbf{AXPO} (\textbf{A}gent e\textbf{X}plorative \textbf{P}olicy \textbf{O}ptimization): for each all-wrong tool-using subgroup, AXPO fixes the thinking prefix and resamples the tool call and its continuation, paired with uncertainty-based prefix selection.
Across nine multimodal benchmarks and three scales of Qwen3-VL-Thinking, SFT\,+\,AXPO outperforms SFT\,+\,GRPO at average (\dPassOneEightB\,pp Pass@1 and \dPassFourEightB\,pp Pass@4 at 8B on average) and 8B with SFT\,+\,AXPO surpasses the 32B Base on Pass@4 with $4\times$ fewer parameters.

\end{abstract}
