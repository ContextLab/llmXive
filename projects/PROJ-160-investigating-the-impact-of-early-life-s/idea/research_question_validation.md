## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive relationship between early life stress (ACE scores) and hippocampal subfield structure, independent of any specific ML or statistical method. The methodology (linear mixed-effects models, Freesurfer segmentation) serves as tools to answer a domain question rather than being the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (ACE scores from parent-reported questionnaire) and the predicted variable (hippocampal subfield volumes from MRI-based Freesurfer segmentation) are derived from completely independent measurement modalities. There is no shared primary signal that would mechanically guarantee the relationship.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While the general link between early life stress and hippocampal volume is well-established, the subfield-specific question (CA3, DG, subiculum) and inter-subfield ratio analysis represent a more nuanced contribution that is less settled in the literature. A positive result would map vulnerability patterns; a null result would suggest stress effects are more diffuse or absent at subfield resolution. Both outcomes would be informative for the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a clear domain relationship (ACE scores → hippocampal subfield volumes in adolescents) rather than implementation constraints. The methodological details (ABC D dataset, Freesurfer, linear models) are means to answer the question, not the question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is scientifically substantive, uses independent data modalities, offers informative results in either direction, and focuses on a domain relationship rather than implementation constraints. The project can proceed to initialization.
