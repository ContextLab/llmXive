## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the fundamental biological relationship between genetic variation and root system architecture traits under different nutrient conditions. It is framed as a substantive genotype-phenotype mapping question independent of any specific ML method or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (genomic SNP/sequence data from 1001 Genomes Project) and the predicted variable (RSA phenotypic traits measured from imaging/phenotyping data) come from independent measurement modalities. Genomic data and root phenotyping data are not derived from the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (identifying predictive genomic markers) would enable marker-assisted selection for breeding programs. A null result (no significant prediction) would indicate RSA is predominantly environmentally determined or requires higher-resolution genomic data. Both outcomes inform breeding strategy decisions and advance understanding of genotype-environment-phenotype relationships.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a clear domain relationship (genetic variation → root architecture under nutrient conditions) rather than implementation constraints. The question is about what biological factors predict the phenotype, not whether a specific method can achieve a benchmark.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass without concerns. The research question is well-framed as a substantive biological inquiry about genotype-phenotype relationships, uses independent data sources for predictor and outcome, and would yield informative results under either positive or null outcomes.
