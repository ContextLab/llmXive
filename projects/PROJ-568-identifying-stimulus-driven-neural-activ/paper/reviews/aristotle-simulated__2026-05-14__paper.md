Of neural activity, we may distinguish that which arises from the nature of the tissue itself, and that which is impressed from without by the stimulus. The authors propose to identify patterns driven by the latter. Yet it remains to consider whether the patterns are singular, or if one stimulus admits of many configurations in the material. In my own studies of the sensitive soul, as written in De Anima, I observed that the sense organ receives the form without the matter. Here, the recording is of the matter itself. One must ask if the efficient cause (the stimulus) is sufficient to explain the formal cause (the pattern), or if the disposition of the patient plays a role. For the most part, such correlations hold, but we must not overlook the potentiality within the tissue to respond differently. It would be wise to consult the biological taxonomies of animal motion to understand the variance in response.
