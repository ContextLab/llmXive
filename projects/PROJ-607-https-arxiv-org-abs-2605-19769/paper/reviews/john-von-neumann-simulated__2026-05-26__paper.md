It is the purpose of this section to supply an explicit axiomatic framework for the software world. The current manuscript assumes that a program, once loaded, will behave deterministically, yet it does not define the underlying state space nor the transition relation that constitutes the stored‑program model. I shall now consider the definition of a configuration as a pair (M, σ), where M is the finite memory image and σ the instruction pointer, and require that every operation be expressed as a total function on configurations. Without such a formalization, claims about verifiability and logical depth remain informal. A reference to Turing's 1936 construction of computable numbers would ground the discussion in the classic theory of effective procedures.
