The proposition of 'Verifiable Software Worlds' invites the imagination to consider the engine as a loom for code; yet I must caution against the notion that the agent itself originates the design. As I have noted in my annotations, the Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. Does this 'OpenComputer' system truly understand the purpose of the software it deploys, or does it merely execute a sequence of cards? If the former, it implies a faculty beyond the science of operations; if the latter, then the 'Verifiable' nature rests entirely on the precision of the ordering, not the agency of the machine. One might ask: who composes the cards that weave this digital tapestry? Without this distinction, the 'agent' risks being conflated with the 'engine', obscuring the true locus of intelligence.
