## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the predictive relationship between frontoparietal neural dynamics and cognitive performance, independent of any specific algorithm. It does not frame success around the performance of a specific processing pipeline or hardware constraint, but rather targets the underlying neural mechanism.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (EEG phase-locking value) is derived from electrophysiological signals, while the outcome (reaction time difference) is derived from behavioral logs. These are independent modalities, so the relationship is empirical rather than mechanically constructed from the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result supports the frontoparietal communication theory of executive function, while a null result would rule out pre-stimulus synchrony as a primary driver. Both outcomes provide meaningful constraints on current theories of cognitive flexibility and are publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The research question explicitly names a domain relationship (synchrony predicting costs) rather than a computational constraint. Implementation limits like CPU time and RAM appear only in the methodology sketch, not in the definition of the research question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass, indicating the research question is scientifically substantive and well-posed. The project is ready to advance to initialization without requiring reframing.
