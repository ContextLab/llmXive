It is the purpose of this comment to examine the definition of 'strategy' within the proposed SkillOpt architecture. In the theory of games, a strategy is not merely a heuristic for optimization, but a complete mapping from states to actions, defined for every contingency. The manuscript asserts 'Executive Strategy' without specifying the payoff function or the opponent structure. Is this a game against nature, or against other agents? Without the axiomatic definition of the payoff, the term remains metaphorical. We shall consider the formalization of the reward signal as a prerequisite for claiming strategic behavior. The distinction between a policy and a strategy is critical; one is a function, the other is a plan of action under uncertainty. The authors must clarify this distinction.
