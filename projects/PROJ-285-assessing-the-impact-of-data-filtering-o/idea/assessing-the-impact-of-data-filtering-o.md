# Assessing the Impact of Data Filtering on Gravitational Lens Detection Rates

**Field**: physics

## Research question

How do variations in automated filtering thresholds—specifically signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) and morphology scores—affect the reported detection rates and purity of gravitational lens candidates in large optical surveys, when purity is validated against an independent simulation-based or visually-confirmed lens catalog?

## Motivation

Current gravitational lens catalogs suffer from selection biases introduced by pipeline cuts, which can skew cosmological parameter estimates derived from lens statistics. Quantifying this impact is essential for correcting survey incompleteness and refining future search strategies for identifying these crucial cosmological probes.

## Related work

- [Strong gravitational lenses from the Vera C. Rubin Observatory (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.08919) — Establishes the scale and methodology challenges of lens detection in next-generation surveys like LSST.
- [Bayesian approach to gravitational lens model selection: constraining H_0 with a selected sample of strong lenses (2012)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1206.5801) — Demonstrates rigorous model selection frameworks for lens samples, providing a precedent for purity validation methods.
- [Strong Lensing considerations for the LSST observing strategy (2019)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.05141) — Discusses how survey design choices impact strong lensing science outcomes, relevant to understanding pipeline-induced biases.

## Expected results

We expect to find that stricter SNR cuts significantly reduce false positives but disproportionately eliminate low-mass lens detections. The measurement of detection rate variance across threshold grids, validated against independent lens catalogs, will confirm the sensitivity of current catalogs to pipeline choices and quantify incompleteness corrections.

## Methodology sketch

- Download DES Year 3 Gold catalog subset via `wget` from https://des.ncsa.illinois.edu/releases (verify size ≤12GB to fit 14GB runner storage).
- Load the catalog into Python using `astropy.io.fits` and `pandas`, filtering to lens candidate columns only.
- Obtain an independent validation catalog: either (a) simulated lens images from the `lsst-detect-lenses` public simulation suite, OR (b) visually confirmed lenses from the Space Warps / Strong Lens Finding Challenge dataset.
- Implement a filtering script that iterates through SNR thresholds (5σ to 20σ in 1σ steps) and morphology score cut-offs (0.3 to 0.9 in 0.1 steps).
- For each threshold combination, calculate detection count and compare against the independent validation catalog to estimate true purity (TP / (TP + FP)).
- Apply a Chi-squared test to compare detection distributions across different filtering regimes.
- Generate summary plots (detection rate vs. threshold, purity curves) using `matplotlib`.
- Store final analysis figures, logs, and threshold recommendations in the project directory.
