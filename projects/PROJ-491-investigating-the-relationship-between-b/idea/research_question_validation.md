## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive neurobiological relationship between two brain phenomena: resting-state network flexibility and ventral striatum reward responsivity. It does not frame the inquiry around whether a specific algorithm or computational method performs adequately, but rather asks about the relationship itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (network flexibility from resting-state fMRI) and predicted variable (ventral striatum activation from task-fMRI during reward cues) are derived from temporally separated scanning sessions measuring different brain states. While both use BOLD fMRI signals from the same subjects, the resting-state and task-evoked contexts are independent measurement conditions, not mechanically linked summaries of the same signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would support the trait-marker hypothesis and justify resting-state flexibility as a clinical biomarker for reward-processing disorders. A null result would challenge this hypothesis and suggest resting-state dynamics are functionally distinct from task-specific reward engagement. Either outcome advances understanding of brain-behavior relationships and is publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (resting-state network dynamics → anticipatory reward processing in ventral striatum) rather than implementation constraints. The methodology (sliding-window FC, Pearson correlation) is appropriate but not the focus of the inquiry.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-constructed, asking about a substantive brain-behavior relationship using independent measurement contexts. The project addresses a genuine literature gap regarding whether resting-state network flexibility serves as a predictive trait marker for reward sensitivity. The methodology appropriately tests the question without making implementation constraints the focus of the inquiry.
