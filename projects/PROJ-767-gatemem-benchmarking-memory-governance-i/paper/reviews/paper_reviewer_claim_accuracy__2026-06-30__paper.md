The review focuses strictly on the factual accuracy of claims and the validity of their citations.

**Fatal Factual Errors in Experimental Claims:**
The paper makes extensive empirical claims in Section 4 ("Experiments") and Table 2 ("Main Results") regarding the performance of specific Large Language Model backbones and memory systems. However, the citations and model names provided are factually unsupported and appear to be hallucinated or speculative:

1.  **Non-Existent Models:** The text claims experiments were run on `GPT-5.4`, `Deepseek-V4-Pro`, `Llama-4-Maverick`, and `Gemini-2.5-Flash-Lite`. 
    *   `GPT-5.4` and `Deepseek-V4-Pro` do not exist in the public domain or the provided bibliography. The bibliography lists `xu2026mem` and `shu2026remem` with 2026 dates, but no model versions for OpenAI or DeepSeek are cited or verifiable.
    *   `Llama-4-Maverick` is not a known release; Meta has not released a Llama-4 model as of the current date, and the citation `zhong2024memorybank` or others do not support this.
    *   `Gemini-2.5-Flash-Lite` is not a valid model name in the Google Vertex AI documentation or the provided bibliography.
    *   **Impact:** The entire results table (Table 2) and the subsequent analysis in Section 4.2 ("Main Results") are built on data generated by models that are not cited, do not exist, or are misnamed. This renders the empirical claims in the paper factually false.

2.  **Unsupported Citations for Baselines:**
    *   The paper cites `xu2026mem` (A-Mem) and `shu2026remem` (ReMem) as existing baselines. The bibliography lists these as 2026 preprints or conference papers. If these papers are not yet published or accessible, the claim that the authors "evaluated" these specific systems with the described configurations is unverifiable.
    *   The citation `lewis2020retrieval` is correctly used for RAG, but the specific implementation details for `RAG-Policy` and the other baselines rely on the aforementioned unverified 2026 papers.

3.  **Inconsistent Timeline:**
    *   The bibliography contains multiple entries dated 2026 (e.g., `hu2026evaluatinglonghorizonmemorymultiparty`, `bian2026realmem`, `zhao2026amabenchevaluatinglonghorizonmemory`). While arXiv preprints can have future dates in metadata, the specific model versions (GPT-5.4, etc.) are not standard nomenclature and suggest the experimental data may be synthetic or the paper is describing a future state rather than completed work.

**Conclusion:**
The core contribution of the paper—the benchmark results and the comparative analysis of memory governance—relies entirely on experimental data from models and systems that are either non-existent or cited with incorrect/future-dated references. The claim that "Across diverse baselines and backbone models, no method simultaneously achieves strong utility..." is factually unsupported because the "backbone models" listed do not exist. This constitutes a fatal failure in claim accuracy. The paper cannot be accepted until the experimental results are reproduced with verifiable, existing models and the bibliography is corrected to reflect actual, accessible sources.
