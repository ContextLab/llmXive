The central claim—that Arc-grounded context outperforms baselines—relies on mean scores in Table 2 without reported variance or statistical significance tests. For DeepSeek-V4-Pro, the Overall gap is +4.7 points (62.4 vs 57.7). Without standard deviations or p-values, it is unclear if this difference is robust or within noise. Please report confidence intervals or conduct significance testing (e.g., bootstrap) across the 1,754 probes to substantiate the "tops every other context strategy" claim.

Second, the evaluation ground truth (reference responses per phase) is generated by a "designer LLM" (Section 3.2), while only the Character Arcs (axes) undergo human validation (Section 3.1). If the LLM designer systematically biases the "correct" phase response, the metric measures alignment with the designer rather than the true character arc. Please clarify the extent of human review on the probe reference responses (gt_action, gt_thought) or provide evidence that LLM-generated references align with human judgment beyond the axis level.

Third, the judge validation in Appendix B.1 is "anchored" to the DeepSeek-V4-Flash verdict (annotators saw the judge's score before adjusting). This measures calibration, not independent accuracy. The cross-judge agreement (Appendix B.2) shows high correlation, but shared biases among LLM judges remain a risk. Consider an independent human evaluation of a subset of model responses against the reference to validate the metric's absolute accuracy.

Finally, the main evaluation slice covers only 5 novels (Table 1). While low-popularity tests address memorization, the small number of source texts limits generalization claims about "novels" as a domain. Expanding the validated slice or providing variance across novels would strengthen the evidence.
