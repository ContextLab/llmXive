## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a biological relationship (genomic TE insertions influencing transcript levels) rather than the performance of a specific computational tool. The statistical method (linear association) is a means to answer the biological query, not the subject of the inquiry itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (TE insertion genotype derived from DNA sequencing) and the outcome (gene expression derived from RNA sequencing) represent independent molecular modalities. There is no mechanical guarantee that an insertion alters expression, making the empirical test necessary and non-circular.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result identifies specific regulatory variants contributing to population diversity, while a null result constrains the role of TEs relative to SNPs in this context. Both outcomes provide meaningful constraints on current models of regulatory architecture in Drosophila.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names the domain relationship of interest (TE presence vs. gene expression) without conditioning success on computational budget or specific algorithmic architecture. It frames a hypothesis about biological causality rather than technical feasibility.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
