## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a physical relationship between CMB temperature anomalies and matter distribution underdensities, independent of the statistical tools used to measure it. The core inquiry concerns whether specific sky regions exhibit correlated signals across distinct cosmological probes, not the performance of a specific algorithm or computational framework.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor data (CMB temperature fluctuations) comes from Planck satellite microwave observations, while the predicted variable (galaxy underdensities) comes from optical/infrared galaxy surveys like SDSS or DES. These are independent measurement modalities with distinct systematic errors, ensuring the alignment is not mechanically guaranteed by shared data processing pipelines.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result supports the Integrated Sachs-Wolfe effect as an explanation for the Cold Spot anomaly, while a null result suggests the anomaly is primordial or statistical noise rather than a foreground structure effect. Both outcomes constrain cosmological models regarding non-Gaussianity and structure formation, making either result publishable and scientifically informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (alignment between CMB spots and large-scale structure) rather than a constraint on the implementation or specific software stack. It focuses on the astrophysical correlation itself, leaving the choice of statistical methods (cross-correlation, Monte Carlo) as tools rather than the subject of inquiry.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, indicating a scientifically substantive question that avoids methodological narrowing and circular construction. The project targets a genuine open question in cosmology regarding the origin of the CMB Cold Spot without embedding implementation constraints into the hypothesis. The idea is ready to advance to project initialization.
