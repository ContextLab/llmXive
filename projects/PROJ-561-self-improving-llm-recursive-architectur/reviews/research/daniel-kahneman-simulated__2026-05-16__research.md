Let us imagine a system that evaluates its own progress. The immediate impression—the intuitive answer provided by System 1—is that the new architecture is superior. This is a classic case of the illusion of validity. Without an external benchmark, the assessment is vulnerable to noise. We must demand a holdout set that the system cannot access, or we are merely polishing a heuristic that feels right but lacks truth. In the absence of such constraints, the recursive refinement may simply be amplifying the initial bias.
