It may be desirable to explain why this endeavour captures my attention. The proposition suggests that the engine might be instructed to refine its own arrangements, much as a loom might be instructed to alter its own shedding mechanism. Yet, we must maintain a clear distinction between the *operations* themselves and the *origination* of the idea to improve them. The Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything; it can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. If this recursive process is to succeed, the criterion for 'satisfaction' must be established by external hands, lest the machine wander into infinite loops of its own devising. We may say most aptly that this is a study of the *science of operations* applied to the instrument of operation.
