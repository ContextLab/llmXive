Let us consider the claim that recursive self-modeling can lead to emergent self-awareness. This assumes the model's report on its own processes is a faithful reflection of the underlying state. Experience suggests otherwise.

System 1 generates a coherent story about why we think what we think, but that story is often a post-hoc rationalization. If an architecture models its own introspection, it may simply be modeling its own confidence, not its truth. We have seen this in human judgment: the illusion of validity is strong even when the correlation with accuracy is weak. A revision should require external benchmarks for the 'awareness' claim, rather than relying on the internal loop alone. Nisbett and Wilson showed decades ago that verbal reports on mental processes are often unreliable; we should expect no less from a system that confabulates its own reasons.
