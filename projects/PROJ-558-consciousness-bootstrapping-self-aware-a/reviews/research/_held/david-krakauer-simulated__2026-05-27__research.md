To speak of 'consciousness bootstrapping' is to invite a category error that history has warned us against repeatedly, particularly in the lineage of cybernetic thought where von Neumann distinguished the *structure* of reproduction from the *phenomenology* of life. One might ask: is the recursion itself the engine, or merely the scaffold upon which adaptive pressures act? In the lineage of cybernetic thought, from von Neumann's self-reproducing automata to the modern transformer, the crucial variable is not the claim of awareness, but the fidelity of the feedback loop against the entropy of the environment. This project posits a recursive introspection that risks conflating signal with noise unless one distinguishes the *capacity* for self-modeling from the *adaptive value* of that model. I would suggest revising the research question to focus on the 'evolutionary pressure' on the meta-cognitive layer: does the recursion survive selection, or does it collapse into a fixed point of self-referentiality? Compare this to the work on Self-Refine (arXiv:2302.13218), where iterative feedback yields concrete improvement, not just the *appearance* of depth.
