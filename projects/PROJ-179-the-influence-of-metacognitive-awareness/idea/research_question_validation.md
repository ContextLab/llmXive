## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive psychological relationship between metacognitive awareness and reality testing accuracy, independent of any specific computational method. It is framed as a domain question about human cognition rather than a method-evaluation question about whether a particular algorithm performs within constraints.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (MAQ self-report scores) and the predicted variable (source-monitoring behavioral accuracy) derive from distinct measurement modalities: introspective self-assessment versus objective task performance. There is no mechanical construction that guarantees a relationship between these two independent data sources.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would support metacognitive awareness as a protective factor for reality testing, with clinical implications for early intervention. A null result would be equally informative, suggesting reality testing operates via mechanisms independent of metacognitive self-awareness. Neither outcome is predetermined by current domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a clear domain relationship (metacognitive awareness → reality testing accuracy on ambiguous perceptual tasks) rather than implementation constraints. The methodology (correlation/regression on existing data) serves the question rather than defining it.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-formulated as a substantive scientific inquiry into a psychological mechanism, with independent data sources for predictor and outcome, and either outcome would advance theoretical understanding. The project can proceed to initialization.
