# Trust Region On-Policy Distillation  

**Field**: computer science  

## Research question  

How does imposing a trust‑region constraint on the KL divergence between teacher and student policies during on‑policy distillation influence (i) the final performance of the student LLM on downstream benchmarks and (ii) the sample‑efficiency (tokens required) to reach a given performance target?  

## Motivation  

On‑policy distillation (OPD) mitigates the prefix‑mismatch problem of offline distillation but often suffers from unstable updates when the student diverges from the teacher. Trust‑region techniques, successful in reinforcement learning, promise monotonic improvement by limiting policy updates, yet their effect on OPD for large language models remains unexplored. Understanding this trade‑off could enable more reliable, data‑efficient LLM compression.  

## Related work  

- **[A Game-Theoretic Approach to Multi-Agent Trust Region Optimization (2021)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.06828)** — Shows that trust‑region constraints guarantee monotonic performance improvement in multi‑agent RL, motivating their adaptation to OPD.  
- **[Trust‑Region Behavior Blending for On‑Policy Distillation (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.31159)** — Introduces behavior‑blending to address prefix mismatch in OPD; our work differs by enforcing a hard KL trust‑region rather than blending.  
- **[Proximal Policy Distillation (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.15134)** — Proposes a PPO‑style clipping objective for policy distillation; serves as a baseline that limits KL indirectly, whereas we enforce an explicit radius.  
- **[Model‑Free DRL Control for Power Inverters: From Policy Learning to Real‑Time Implementation via Knowledge Distillation (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.07964)** — Demonstrates knowledge distillation in a control setting with trust‑region ideas, providing a cross‑domain precedent for our approach.  

## Expected results  

We anticipate that a trust‑region‑constrained OPD will (a) achieve comparable or higher benchmark scores than vanilla OPD while (b) requiring fewer teacher‑generated tokens to reach a predefined performance threshold. Confirmation will be a statistically significant reduction in token count (paired t‑test, *p* < 0.05) without loss of final accuracy; falsification will be a non‑significant difference or degraded performance.  

## Methodology sketch  

1. **Data acquisition**  
   - Download the *Pile* subset (10 k samples) from HuggingFace: `datasets.load_dataset("EleutherAI/pile", split="train[:10000]")`.  
   - Use publicly released teacher checkpoints (Llama‑2 7B) from Meta’s model hub.  

2. **Student model**  
   - Initialize a smaller Llama‑2 3B model (open‑source checkpoint).  

3. **On‑policy sampling**  
   - Generate prefixes from the current student policy (CPU‑only greedy sampling).  
   - For each prefix, obtain teacher logits on the same continuation.  

4. **Loss functions**  
   - **Vanilla OPD**: cross‑entropy between student logits and teacher logits.  
   - **Trust‑region OPD (TR‑OPD)**: same loss plus a hard KL constraint `KL(π_T‖π_S) ≤ δ`, enforced by scaling the gradient when the constraint is violated (δ = 0.5).  
   - **Baseline PPD**: PPO‑style clipped surrogate loss as described in Proximal Policy Distillation.  

5. **Training regime**  
   - Run 3 random seeds (different init states).  
   - For each seed, train all three methods for up to 200 k teacher‑token steps or until the student reaches 80 % of the teacher’s performance on a validation split.  

6. **Evaluation (independent)**  
   - Compute zero‑shot scores on:  
     - *MATH* (mathematical reasoning) via the official benchmark script.  
     - *HumanEval* (code generation) via the OpenAI evaluation harness.  
     - *TruthfulQA* (general‑domain factuality).  
   - Record the total number of teacher tokens consumed when the target performance (e.g., 75 % of teacher’s MATH score) is first achieved.  

7. **Statistical analysis**  
   - Perform paired t‑tests across seeds comparing TR‑OPD vs. vanilla OPD for (i) final benchmark scores and (ii) token‑efficiency.  
   - Report mean ± standard deviation and 95 % confidence intervals.  

8. **Ablation**  
   - Vary the trust‑region radius δ ∈ {0.2, 0.5, 1.0} to assess sensitivity.  

9. **Reproducibility**  
   - All scripts, requirements (`requirements.txt`), and random‑seed logs will be stored in a public GitHub repo.  
   - Figures and tables generated with matplotlib/ seaborn (CPU‑only).
