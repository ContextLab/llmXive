# Research: llmXive follow-up: extending "PhysisForcing: Physics Reinforced World Simulator for Robotic Manipula"

## 1. Problem Statement & Hypothesis

**Problem**: Training robotic manipulation policies with physics-informed constraints (e.g., PhysisForcing) is computationally expensive due to joint optimization of generation and simulation.
**Primary Hypothesis**: A lightweight, post-generation physics filter applied to synthetic videos can discard physically inconsistent samples such that training a distilled diffusion model on the remaining "curated" data yields physical consistency scores on R-Bench and PAI-Bench (re-implemented in MuJoCo) significantly higher than a model trained on a **Randomized Control** subset of the same size. This isolates the effect of the *filter* from the effect of *data reduction*.
**Secondary Hypothesis (Descriptive Benchmark)**: The physical consistency scores of the Filtered model are comparable (≤15% gap) to the PhysisForcing baseline. 
*   **Critical Clarification**: This comparison is **not** a test of the "filtering alone" hypothesis. It is a descriptive benchmark comparing two fundamentally different training regimes: (1) static training on curated data (our method) vs. (2) dynamic joint optimization (PhysisForcing). The "comparability" claim (≤15% gap) is defined as a measure of **generalization to unseen physical properties** (orthogonal to the filter's selection criteria), not a direct replication of the filter's score. If the gap exceeds 15%, it indicates that joint optimization provides a significant advantage for generalization that static filtering alone cannot achieve, even with high-quality data.

## 2. Methodological Rigor & Statistical Plan

### 2.1 Dataset Variable Fit
The study relies on **synthetic data generation**.
- **Predictor**: Prompt text (from verified prompt sources).
- **Outcome**: Video frames (generated by Wan2.1).
- **Covariate**: Physics consistency score (derived from PyBullet simulation).
- **Fit**: The dataset is *generated* to fit the study requirements. The plan confirms that the **Wan2.1 model** (verified source) is capable of generating the required robotic manipulation videos. The **PyBullet filter** (verified source) provides the necessary covariate (physics score).
- **Gap Mitigation**: If Wan2.1 fails to generate specific robotic manipulation scenarios, the plan falls back to a smaller set of verified prompts from `Rapidata/text-2-video-human-preferences-wan2.1` (verified source) to ensure the dataset contains the necessary "manipulation" context.

### 2.2 Statistical Rigor
- **Multiple Comparison Correction**: The study compares three models (Filtered, Randomized Control, PhysisForcing Baseline) across two benchmarks (R-Bench, PAI-Bench). A **Bonferroni correction** will be applied to the alpha level (0.05 / 6 tests = 0.0083) to control family-wise error rate.
- **Sample Size / Power**: 
  - **Target**: n ≥ 50 per group.
 - **Justification**: To detect a [deferred] gap in R-Bench scores (assuming a standard deviation of [deferred] based on prior literature) with 80% power at α=0.0083 (Bonferroni corrected), a sample size sufficient for statistical power is required.
 - **Power Limitation**: If the curated dataset yields <50 valid samples, **FR-009** (Data Augmentation) will be triggered. If augmentation fails to reach n=50, the study will report an "underpowered for [deferred] gap" conclusion and rely on effect size estimation rather than significance testing.
- **Causal Inference**: This is an **observational** study regarding the "effect" of filtering. The plan frames results as **associational**: "Training on filtered data is associated with X% improvement over random selection." No randomization of the *filtering process* is claimed; the filter is a deterministic curation step.
- **Measurement Validity & Orthogonality**: 
  - **Selection Metric**: PyBullet (trajectory continuity, contact conservation).
  - **Evaluation Metric**: MuJoCo (R-Bench/PAI-Bench).
  - **Orthogonality Check**: Before the main experiment, a subset of videos will be scored by both PyBullet and MuJoCo. The plan requires a Pearson correlation coefficient < 0.95 between the selection metric and the evaluation metric. If correlation ≥ 0.95, the evaluation metrics are considered trivially correlated with the selection criteria, and the hypothesis of "generalization" is invalidated. The plan will then adjust the evaluation metrics to focus on properties not directly optimized by the filter (e.g., force dynamics, object deformation).
- **Collinearity**: The "Physics Score" is derived from the video content. The model's "Physical Consistency" is the outcome. These are definitionally related. The plan avoids claiming "independent effects" of the score on the outcome; instead, it treats the score as a **selection criterion** and measures the resulting outcome distribution.
- **Independence**: The "Filtered" and "Control" groups are generated from **separate batches** (or distinct random seeds) to ensure statistical independence for the t-test. The "Unfiltered" set is not used as a direct comparison group to avoid subset dependency issues.

### 2.3 Compute Feasibility & Escape Hatch

| Component | Method | Feasibility | Rationale |
|-----------|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **Video Generation (Wan2.1)** | Scaled-down GPU (Kaggle) | **GPU Escape Hatch** | No faithful CPU form for high-res video generation. Plan: Use `device="cuda"`, low-bit quantization, small batch size (1-2), 500 steps max. Auto-offload to Kaggle GPU. |
| **Physics Filtering (PyBullet)** | CPU | **CPU-First** | PyBullet runs efficiently on CPU. Adequate RAM is sufficient for headless simulation of single videos. |
| **Model Training (50M Diffusion)** | CPU | **CPU-First** | A model with a moderate parameter count fits within available system memory. Training on CPU is slow but feasible (≤4h for 10 epochs) with small batch size (1) and gradient accumulation. |
| **Evaluation (R-Bench/PAI-Bench)** | CPU (MuJoCo) | **CPU-First** | Metric computation is lightweight. MuJoCo runs on CPU. |

**Decision**: The plan uses a **hybrid execution model**. Generation is offloaded to a free Kaggle GPU (scaled down). Filtering, Training, and Evaluation run on the CPU-only GitHub Actions runner. This avoids fabricating a CPU approximation for generation while ensuring the core research loop (filter → train → eval) runs on the primary CI.

## 4. Risk Analysis

1.  **Risk**: Wan2.1 generation fails or produces non-manipulation videos.
    *   **Mitigation**: Use a small, verified subset of prompts from `Rapidata` known to contain "robot" or "manipulation" keywords. If generation fails, the pipeline aborts with a clear error code.
2.  **Risk**: PyBullet filter is too slow (>2h).
    *   **Mitigation**: Process videos in parallel (where RAM allows) or in batches. If time exceeds limit, reduce the initial generation count (n=500 instead of 1000).
3.  **Risk**: Training diverges (NaN loss).
    *   **Mitigation**: Implement NaN checks in `src/training/trainer.py`. Abort and retry with lower learning rate (max a limited number of attempts).
4.  **Risk**: Sample size < 50 after filtering.
    *   **Mitigation**: Trigger FR-009 (Data Augmentation) via simple geometric transforms (rotation, flipping) on the curated set to reach n=50. Explicitly report this limitation.
5.  **Risk**: MuJoCo re-implementation of benchmarks is inconsistent with original.
    *   **Mitigation**: Validate MuJoCo implementation against a small set of known ground-truth cases. Report any discrepancies in the validation log.
6.  **Risk**: Orthogonality check fails (correlation ≥ 0.95).
    *   **Mitigation**: If the evaluation metrics are trivially correlated with the filter scores, the plan will adjust the evaluation to focus on properties not directly optimized by the filter (e.g., force dynamics, object deformation) or report that the "generalization" hypothesis cannot be tested with the current metrics.

## 5. Conclusion

The proposed methodology is rigorous, adhering to the constraints of CPU-first computation while utilizing a scaled GPU escape hatch for the only non-tractable step (generation). The dataset strategy is sound, relying on synthetic generation from verified prompts rather than non-existent pre-curated video datasets. The statistical plan addresses power, multiple comparisons, and collinearity, ensuring that the "comparability" claim is scientifically valid as a descriptive benchmark of generalization, not a causal proof of equivalence. The introduction of a **Randomized Control** group isolates the filtering effect, and the **MuJoCo re-implementation** of benchmarks with an **orthogonality check** breaks circularity with the PyBullet filter.
