### ⚠️ Critical Limitation: Associational Findings Only

**The results presented in this report are strictly associational.**

Due to the observational nature of the source data (cross-sectional surveys) and the construction of the synthetic cohort via statistical weighting rather than random assignment:
1. **No Causal Inference:** We cannot claim that increased social support *causes* reduced psychological distress, nor that harassment *causes* increased distress.
2. **Confounding:** Despite rigorous balancing (SMD ≤ 0.1) and covariate adjustment, unmeasured confounding variables (e.g., personality traits, prior trauma history, socioeconomic factors not captured in the survey) may influence both the level of social support and the outcome variables (depression, anxiety, PTSD).
3. **Reverse Causality:** The cross-sectional design precludes determining the temporal direction of effects. For instance, individuals with higher depression scores may perceive their social support as lower, rather than low support causing the depression.

Therefore, all interpretations below should be framed as **correlations**, **associations**, or **statistical relationships**, avoiding causal language (e.g., "leads to," "causes," "prevents," "mitigates").

## Limitations and Future Directions

1. **Cross-Sectional Design:** The inability to establish temporal precedence limits the ability to infer causality. Longitudinal studies are required to determine if social support interventions precede changes in mental health outcomes.
2. **Self-Report Bias:** All variables (harassment, support, mental health) were self-reported, introducing potential recall and social desirability biases.
3. **Unmeasured Confounding:** While we adjusted for key demographics, unmeasured factors (e.g., genetic predisposition, prior life events) may confound the observed associations.
4. **Synthetic Cohort Constraints:** The synthetic cohort relies on the assumption that the propensity score model captures all relevant confounders. Violations of the "no unmeasured confounding" assumption could bias the results.
