## Summary

This feature implements a CPU-tractable geometric inversion pipeline to analyze the "OmniDirector" camera grid representation. The system ingests grid-video pairs, filters for geometrically diverse sequences (dolly-ins, orbits), and solves for relative 3D camera motion and scene bounding box *aspect ratios* using classical computer vision (OpenCV `solvePnP`) on a canonical unit grid model. 

**Crucial Clarification**: The solver recovers pose *up to a scale factor* due to the planar nature of the grid. The hypothesis is refined: the grid representation encodes invertible 3D *relative* priors (aspect ratios) but discards *absolute* metric scale. The plan distinguishes between:
1.  **Internal Consistency**: Can the solver re-derive the generation parameters (relative dimensions) from the grid?
2.  **Metric Validity**: Can the solver recover *absolute* metric dimensions when the grid is generated with randomized depth (Synthetic Control)?

The pipeline strictly adheres to free-tier CI constraints (CPU-only, <7GB RAM, <6h runtime).
