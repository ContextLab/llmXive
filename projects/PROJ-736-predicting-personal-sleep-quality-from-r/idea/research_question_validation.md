## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates whether resting‑state functional connectivity (a brain signal) contains information predictive of self‑reported sleep quality, a substantive neurobiological relationship, and does not hinge on any particular algorithmic performance or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor data come from rs‑fMRI connectivity matrices; the predicted variable is the PSQI score, a behavioral questionnaire. These are independent measurement modalities, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a significant positive prediction and a null result would be informative: a positive finding would support rs‑fMRI as a non‑invasive biomarker for sleep quality, while a null would indicate that connectivity alone may be insufficient, guiding future multimodal work.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The research question asks about a domain relationship (“how accurately can connectivity predict sleep quality?”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method, hardware, or runtime budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
