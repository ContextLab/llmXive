# Systematic Assessment of Non-Coding Variant Effects on Transcription Factor Binding Affinities

**Field**: biology

This project investigates whether common non-coding single nucleotide polymorphisms significantly alter predicted transcription factor binding affinities across the human genome. Understanding this relationship is critical for interpreting the functional impact of GWAS hits located outside coding regions, where many disease-associated variants reside. The proposed approach involves downloading common variant datasets from dbSNP and applying position weight matrices from the JASPAR database to score binding affinity changes between reference and alternate alleles. Results will be aggregated to identify transcription factors most sensitive to non-coding variation and genomic regions with high variant density. This analysis relies entirely on public genomic resources and lightweight computational scoring, ensuring compatibility with standard CI environments without requiring specialized hardware or live model APIs. The outcome will provide a prioritized list of regulatory variants for further experimental validation.
