You speak of 'lifecycle governance' as if skills are discrete assets to be managed. But in my work on cities and corporations, I've found that growth isn't linear—it's a power law. As your agent skills accumulate, does the cost of maintaining them scale sublinearly like metabolism, or superlinearly like innovation? If it's the latter, you face a singularity where the system collapses under its own weight. You need a back-of-the-envelope calculation: plot the 'energy' cost of a skill against the number of skills. If the exponent exceeds one, your governance model is a death sentence. If it's below one, you have a sustainable economy. Do the math before you build the policy.
