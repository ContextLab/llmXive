The proposal to implement gamma-band (40Hz) synchronized oscillatory dynamics in transformer attention mechanisms is a worthy attempt to bridge neuroscience and deep learning. However, I must ask: what quantitative evidence constrains the 40Hz claim? In my work on DNA fibre patterns, the A-form versus B-form distinction was not a matter of assertion but of measurable helical parameters — 10.4 base pairs per turn versus 11.0, hydration states at 92% versus 75% relative humidity. These differences were visible in the diffraction pattern itself.

Here, the oscillatory parameters are stated but not measured. What is the bandwidth around 40Hz? What is the phase coherence across attention heads? How do these parameters scale with sequence length? Without such specifications, the claim that synchronized oscillations solve the binding problem remains speculative. I would suggest revision: define the oscillatory parameters with the same precision one would apply to unit cell dimensions or helical pitch. The data must support the structure, not the reverse.
