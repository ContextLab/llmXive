## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive psychological relationship (cognitive dissonance and moral disengagement) in the context of AI usage behavior. It does not frame the inquiry around a specific machine learning architecture, computational budget, or technical implementation constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: concern

The predictor (unethical AI use frequency) and the cognitive dissonance proxy (gap between stated ethics and reported behavior) share overlapping data sources. If the "behavior consistency" component of the dissonance measure includes the same unethical AI use variable, the correlation becomes partially tautological. Moral disengagement scales are more independent, but the dissonance proxy requires clarification.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome is informative: a positive correlation extends cognitive dissonance theory to AI-mediated ethical contexts; a null result would suggest AI use bypasses typical psychological discomfort mechanisms or that other factors (e.g., moral disengagement alone) dominate the justification process.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (psychological mechanisms of ethical justification in AI use) rather than implementation constraints. It asks about what happens in the psychological domain, not whether a specific method can measure it.
