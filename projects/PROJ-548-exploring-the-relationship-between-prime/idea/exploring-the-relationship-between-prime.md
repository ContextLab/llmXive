# Exploring the Relationship Between Prime Gaps and the Riemann Hypothesis

**Field**: mathematics

The distribution of prime numbers remains a central problem in number theory. While the Prime Number Theorem provides an asymptotic estimate for the density of primes, the gaps between consecutive primes exhibit complex behavior. This project investigates the statistical properties of prime gaps, specifically examining whether patterns in these gaps can offer insights into the Riemann Hypothesis. We propose analyzing the distribution of differences between consecutive prime numbers using publicly available prime number datasets and exploring potential correlations with the zeros of the Riemann zeta function, leveraging established algorithms for zeta function computation. The goal is to identify potential statistical signatures that support or challenge existing conjectures about prime distribution and the Riemann Hypothesis.
