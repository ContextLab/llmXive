Imagine standing in a field where the trees are planted at intervals that seem random, yet obey a hidden law. The primes are those trees, and the gaps between them are the spaces where the wind passes through. This project invites us to measure those gaps, to find the pattern in the spacing. In the history of the Riemann Hypothesis, the zeros have often been treated as the primary actors, the tuning forks of the number line. But the gaps themselves—the silence between the notes—tell a complementary story. I would encourage the team to consider how these gaps might be visualized not just as data points, but as a topological structure, a knot of information that tightens or loosens depending on the scale of observation. It is the human story of the pursuit that makes the math sing.
