## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a relationship between conversational dynamics (turn-taking, prosody) and the internal trajectory of a generative model (state-space displacement), seeking to identify a "low-information manifold." While the motivation mentions specific solvers, the core inquiry is about the empirical correlation between human behavior and latent smoothness, which is a substantive question about the nature of real-time interaction rather than a benchmark of a specific architecture's speed.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variables (semantic and prosodic signals derived from audio/text input) are causally distinct from the predicted variable (the magnitude of displacement in the video/audio latent space generated by the model). Although the latent space is conditioned on the input, the *magnitude of change* in the latent trajectory is not mechanically identical to the input features; the hypothesis that specific input patterns correlate with small latent deltas is an empirical claim, not a tautology.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (strong correlation) would provide a theoretical basis for adaptive skipping, validating the "low-information manifold" hypothesis and enabling new compression strategies. A null result (no correlation) would be equally informative, suggesting that even during "boring" conversational turns, the generative model undergoes complex, unpredictable latent shifts, thereby ruling out simple heuristic skipping and forcing a re-evaluation of current optimization assumptions.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (correlation between human turn-taking structure and latent state displacement) and frames the goal as discovering a fundamental property ("low-information manifold") independent of specific solver architectures. It avoids framing the research as merely "Can method X run faster on hardware Y?" by focusing on the underlying phenomenon that would *enable* such methods.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass; the research question successfully identifies a substantive, non-circular, and non-trivial relationship between human interaction patterns and generative model dynamics. The framing is independent of specific implementation constraints, focusing instead on the discovery of a structural property of the data generation process.
