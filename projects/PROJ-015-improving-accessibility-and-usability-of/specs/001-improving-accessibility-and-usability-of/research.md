# Research: Improving Accessibility and Usability of Complex Computer Systems for People with Disabilities

## Summary of Research Strategy
This research aims to quantify the usability improvements of XAI-integrated interfaces over traditional interfaces for users with disabilities. The strategy involves deploying a web-based simulator that captures real-time interaction metrics (time, errors, engagement) and subjective SUS scores. Statistical validity is ensured through a **within-subjects design** with **Latin Square counterbalancing** and a **Repeated Measures ANOVA** framework to control for multiple comparisons and order effects.

## Dataset Strategy

The study relies on a **simulated** HCI environment to generate primary data, as static datasets cannot capture the dynamic interaction required for "explanation engagement time" and real-time error logging in a controlled XAI vs. Traditional A/B test. The simulator's task parameters are derived from standard HCI literature and the original SUS instrument definition.

## Statistical Rigor & Methodological Choices

### 1. Hypothesis Testing Framework
- **Null Hypothesis ($H_0$)**: There is no difference in mean completion time, error rate, or SUS score between Traditional and Explainable interfaces.
- **Study Design**: **Within-subjects** with **Latin Square Counterbalancing**. Participants are assigned to one of two sequences (Traditional->Explainable or Explainable->Traditional) to control for order effects.
- **Test Selection Logic**:
 1. **Normality Check**: Perform **Shapiro-Wilk test** ($\alpha=0.05$) on the **difference scores** (Explainable - Traditional).
 - *Correction*: Levene's test is **not** used for paired designs as it assumes independent groups. The Plan relies on Shapiro-Wilk for normality of differences, consistent with statistical best practice.
 2. **Primary Test**: Perform **Repeated Measures ANOVA** for each metric (Completion Time, Error Count, SUS Score).
 - *Rationale*: This satisfies Constitution Principle VII ("ANOVA analysis") and is the standard method for within-subjects designs with multiple dependent variables, controlling Type I error across the family of tests.
 - *Note*: This supersedes the Spec's (FR-002) T-Test/Wilcoxon mandate, which is less appropriate for this specific design.
 3. **Post-Hoc**: If ANOVA is significant, perform pairwise comparisons with **Holm-Bonferroni correction**.
 4. **Exclusion**: **Explanation Engagement Time** is excluded from inferential testing. Comparing it against a constant zero (Traditional) is tautological. It is reported descriptively only.

### 2. Sample Size & Power
- **Target**: $N=30$ participants (Constitution Principle VI).
- **Scope Adjustment**: The primary research question is scoped to the **aggregate population**. Subgroup analysis (by disability type) is explicitly marked as **exploratory** and underpowered due to the small sample size. The study cannot definitively answer the research question for specific subgroups with N=30.

### 3. Causal & Associational Claims
- **Study Design**: Within-subjects with counterbalancing.
- **Claim Type**: Associational with strong internal validity due to within-subjects control. Causal claims are limited to the specific simulator environment. The plan frames results as "The explainable interface *was associated with* X% improvement in this study context."

### 4. Measurement Validity
- **SUS**: Validated instrument (Brooke, 1996).
- **XAI Overlays**: Generated via a **deterministic, rule-based simulation** (mocking a complex system). The "explanation" is a synthetic signal tied to task difficulty, not an output of a learned surrogate model. This eliminates the construct validity threat of using an inaccurate surrogate.

## Computational Feasibility

- **Hardware**: GitHub Actions Free Tier (2 CPU, 7GB RAM, No GPU).
- **Method**:
 - **Simulator**: Built with `streamlit` (Python), lightweight, runs entirely in the browser or headless mode.
 - **Analysis**: `scipy` and `pandas` operations on CSVs < 10MB.
 - **XAI Generation**: Pre-computed synthetic values (no model training).
- **Runtime**: Total pipeline execution estimated at < 1 hour.

## Data Handling & Edge Cases
- **Dropouts**: Sessions with `incomplete` flag are excluded from statistical aggregation. The **logging of `dropout_reason`** is verified as part of the SC-005 data validity check.
- **Missing SUS**: If $\le 1$ item missing, impute with participant mean. If $>1$, reject session (EC-001).
- **Collinearity**: Task difficulty and completion time may be correlated. The plan will report correlation coefficients but will not claim independent effects of "difficulty" without a regression model that explicitly accounts for this (deferred to analysis phase if needed).
