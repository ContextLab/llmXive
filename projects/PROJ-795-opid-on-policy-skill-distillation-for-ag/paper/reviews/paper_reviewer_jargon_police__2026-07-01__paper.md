The manuscript exhibits a high density of domain-specific terminology that, while standard in Reinforcement Learning (RL) literature, may create barriers for readers from the broader Large Language Model (LLM) or general AI communities. The paper frequently relies on acronyms and jargon without sufficient definition at first use.

Specifically, the term **GRPO** is introduced in the Introduction without expansion. While the paper later references "GRPO-style training," the full name (Group Relative Policy Optimization) should be provided upon first mention to ensure accessibility. Similarly, **KL** divergence is referenced via the symbol $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{KL}}$ in the Methods section without the full term "Kullback-Leibler" being explicitly defined in the main text, assuming reader familiarity.

The phrase **"privileged context"** is used to describe the skill-augmented history. While technically accurate in the context of "privileged learning," the term "privileged" can be ambiguous to non-specialists. Replacing this with "augmented context" or "additional context" would improve clarity without losing meaning. Additionally, the term **"hindsight"** is used repeatedly as a noun modifier (e.g., "hindsight skills," "hindsight supervision"). While evocative, "retrospective" or "post-hoc" are more precise academic alternatives that avoid the colloquial feel of "hindsight" in this specific grammatical construction.

The term **"rollouts"** appears in the Appendix (e.g., "sampled trajectories on-policy from the ordinary task context"). While standard in RL, "trajectories" or "execution traces" are more universally understood terms in the LLM agent literature and would reduce the jargon load. Finally, the distinction between **"on-policy"** and **"off-policy"** is central to the paper's contribution but is never explicitly defined for the reader. A brief parenthetical explanation (e.g., "learning from data generated by the current policy") at the first occurrence would significantly aid non-RL specialists.

These changes are minor in terms of effort but crucial for ensuring the paper's contributions are accessible to the full spectrum of the target audience.
