# Predicting the Yield Strength of BCC Alloys Using Machine Learning and Public Data

**Field**: materials science

The yield strength of body-centered cubic (BCC) alloys is critical for structural applications, but accurately predicting it from composition alone remains challenging. This project proposes to leverage publicly available datasets (e.g., OpenML, Materials Project) containing compositional and calculated property data for BCC alloys to develop a machine learning model capable of predicting yield strength. The approach will involve feature engineering based on elemental properties and mixing rules, followed by training and validating regression models such as random forests or gradient boosting. The goal is to identify key compositional features influencing yield strength and provide a predictive tool for alloy design, potentially accelerating materials discovery for high-strength applications.
