## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether the expansion rate of the Universe shows a directional dependence (anisotropy) as inferred from supernova distance‑modulus residuals. It targets a cosmological phenomenon and does not hinge on the performance of any particular analysis technique or computational resource.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The data source for the test is the Pantheon+ supernova residuals. The hypothesis concerns a statistical property of that same dataset (the dipole/quadrupole amplitude). This is not a predictor‑predicted relationship but a hypothesis‑test on a single signal, so there is no mechanical guarantee that the outcome is predetermined.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a statistically significant anisotropic signal and the absence of such a signal would be scientifically informative: a detection would challenge the Cosmological Principle, while a null result would tighten isotropy constraints for the Pantheon+ sample.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question is framed as a domain‑level inquiry (“Does the cosmic expansion rate exhibit directional dependence…?”) rather than as a constraint on a specific implementation or computational budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated  

All four checks pass, indicating that the research question is well‑posed, scientifically meaningful, and free from methodological or circularity flaws.
