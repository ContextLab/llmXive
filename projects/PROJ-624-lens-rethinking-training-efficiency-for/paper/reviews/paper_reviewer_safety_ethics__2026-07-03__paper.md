The manuscript addresses safety and ethics primarily in the "Broader Impacts and Limitations" section (Section 7), acknowledging the risk of misuse for misleading content and the mitigation strategy of a "reasoner" module. However, the review identifies three critical gaps in the safety and ethics disclosure that require expansion before publication.

First, the claim that a reasoner "can identify and reject inappropriate user requests" (Section 1, Section 7) is a significant safety assertion that lacks empirical support or technical specification. The paper does not define the scope of "inappropriate" (e.g., hate speech, non-consensual deepfakes, violence), nor does it provide metrics on the reasoner's false positive/negative rates. Without this, the claim is unverifiable and potentially misleading regarding the model's actual safety posture. The authors must detail the safety taxonomy, the specific prompts used for the reasoner, and any evaluation of its effectiveness against adversarial inputs.

Second, the data provenance for the "Lens-800M" dataset is insufficiently transparent. The authors state the dataset includes "private" sources (Section 2.1) alongside public and synthetic data. The use of private data in foundational model training raises serious privacy and consent concerns. The manuscript must clarify the nature of this private data, the legal basis for its inclusion, and whether consent was obtained from the subjects depicted. Additionally, the reliance on GPT-4.1 for generating 800M dense captions introduces a layer of synthetic data whose own safety and bias properties are not discussed.

Third, the paper does not address the safety alignment of the large language models (GPT-4.1, GPT-5.5) used extensively for captioning, rubric generation, and reward modeling. If these upstream models contain biases or safety gaps, they are directly propagated into the Lens model's training signal. A discussion on how these upstream risks are mitigated or monitored is necessary.

While the authors acknowledge limitations regarding multilingual performance and artifacts, the lack of concrete details on safety mechanisms and data ethics constitutes a significant omission for a foundational model paper.
