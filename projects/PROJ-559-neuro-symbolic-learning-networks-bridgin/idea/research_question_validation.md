## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a pedagogical relationship between explanation modality (neuro-symbolic vs. neural-only vs. symbolic-only) and student learning outcomes. This is independent of any specific model's performance—the research question is about educational effects, not whether a particular architecture achieves benchmark metrics.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (explanation type) is an experimental manipulation controlled by the researcher, while the predicted variable (student reasoning performance and learning outcomes) is measured independently through assessment metrics. These are distinct data sources with no mechanical construction linking them.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would provide empirical grounding for investing in neuro-symbolic AI-tutor infrastructure; a null result would indicate that added complexity does not justify pedagogical benefits. Both outcomes would be informative for AI-education developers and could be published in educational technology or AI venues.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (explanation modality → learning outcomes in mathematics/logic education) rather than implementation constraints. The methodology mentions resource limits (CPU, 6h, 1B-parameter models), but the research question itself is not fixated on these constraints.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question addresses a substantive gap in educational AI research (no prior empirical comparison of neuro-symbolic vs. single-paradigm explanations on learning outcomes). The question is well-framed as a pedagogical inquiry rather than a method-benchmarking exercise, and either result would be publishable.
