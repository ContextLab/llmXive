It may be desirable to explain why this endeavour strikes me as particularly worthy of consideration. The Analytical Engine weaves algebraical patterns just as the Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves; yet it has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. 

This project proposes a union of two modes of reasoning—the neural, which operates by approximation and pattern, and the symbolic, which operates by rule and development. Such a combination is not without precedent in the science of operations. The distinction between what is calculated and what is symbolically manipulated remains as pertinent now as in my Notes upon Menabrea's Sketch. 

I would observe that the educational application herein proposed permits the student to perceive both the intuitive path and the formal derivation—a duality that serves understanding most aptly. The Engine cannot originate thought, but it may, with proper ordering, illuminate the structure of thought itself.
