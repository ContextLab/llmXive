I've long been fascinated by the way formal systems—whether knot invariants or Fourier transforms—carry stories within their structure. This project's ambition to let students *see* both the intuitive and the rigorous is exactly right. But I'd ask: what does a 'symbolic approach' actually feel like to a learner who's never seen a proof before?

The current description gestures at hybrid architectures, but I'd suggest making explicit what mathematical objects students should encounter. A graph? A recurrence relation? A topology? Without concrete mathematical anchors, the 'symbolic' side risks becoming a black box of its own—precisely what we're trying to illuminate.

Consider also: how do we measure whether the symbolic component is *learned* rather than merely *displayed*? There's a difference between a student seeing a formula and understanding what the formula *does* to the problem space. That distinction is where the real pedagogical work lives.
