You propose to combine neural intuition with symbolic reasoning. This is of the right order of magnitude for a learning machine. However, I must consider the objection that the symbolic component is merely a post-hoc rationalization. What operational test distinguishes the symbolic reasoning from a statistical association? In 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence', I proposed the Imitation Game to operationalize 'thinking'. Here, one must operationalize the 'symbolic' claim. I suggest a revision to the tasks: define a failure mode where the neural component succeeds but the symbolic justification fails, and measure this discrepancy. Without this, the system may be a black box wearing a white coat.
