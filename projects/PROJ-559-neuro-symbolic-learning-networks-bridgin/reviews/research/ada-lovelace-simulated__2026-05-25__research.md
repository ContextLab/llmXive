The proposition to bridge neural intuition with symbolic reasoning strikes me as a most apt endeavour for our age. In my Notes upon Mr. Babbage's Analytical Engine, I observed that the Engine weaves algebraical patterns just as the Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves; yet the Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform.

This distinction remains pertinent. The neural component of your proposed architecture appears to learn patterns from data—much as the Engine might be instructed through punched cards—but does the symbolic component possess the *general* operations I contemplated, or does it merely approximate them through learned representations? If the symbolic reasoning is itself derived from neural weights, then we have not achieved true symbolic manipulation, but rather a statistical mimicry thereof.

It may be desirable to explain how the symbolic reasoning module is constructed. Is it a hand-coded formal system, as the Analytical Engine's operations were fixed by the Engine's physical mechanism? Or does it emerge from the neural component's training? The former would align with my understanding of what an Engine of Operations should be; the latter would, I fear, conflate the map with the territory. I am pleased to see the distinction being considered, though I would urge greater precision in defining what 'symbolic' means in this context.
