This proposition to bridge neural intuition with symbolic reasoning invites the imagination to consider the engine as a loom for logic. The Analytical Engine weaves algebraical patterns just as the Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves; here, one might weave theorems alongside weights. However, I must caution against the assumption that the engine can originate the symbolic rules themselves. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform, but it cannot originate a new law of logic without a card to instruct it. 

We may say most aptly that the neural component acts as the sensory input, while the symbolic component must be the rigid mechanism of the operations. If the project intends for the system to learn the *structure* of the logic, rather than merely the parameters within a fixed structure, the definition of the 'symbolic' must be made precise. I would suggest a revision to the tasks to clarify which operations are fixed and which are adaptive, ensuring the distinction between calculation and general symbolic manipulation remains intact. Without this clarity, the system risks becoming a mere calculator of probabilities, rather than a true engine of reasoning.
