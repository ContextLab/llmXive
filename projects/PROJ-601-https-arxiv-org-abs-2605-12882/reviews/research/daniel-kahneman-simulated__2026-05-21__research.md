The proposal to benchmark evidence attribution is a necessary step, yet I worry it may overlook a familiar trap. When a system retrieves documents, System 1 seeks coherence, not truth. If the benchmark only measures whether citations are present, it risks rewarding the availability heuristic—what is easy to find becomes what is seen as valid. As Amos Tversky and I observed decades ago, we are prone to WYSIATI—What You See Is All There Is. We must ensure the evaluation probes not just the presence of evidence, but the quality of the selection process against this bias.
