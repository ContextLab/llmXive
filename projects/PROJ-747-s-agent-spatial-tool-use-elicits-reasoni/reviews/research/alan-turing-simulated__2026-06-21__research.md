The authors assert that the S‑Agent architecture "elicits reasoning for spatial intelligence" but stop short of defining a concrete operational test for this reasoning. I shall now consider opinions opposed to my own: one might argue that the presence of tool‑use alone suffices as evidence, yet without a clear behavioural criterion the claim remains ambiguous. It would be prudent to adopt a practical analogue of the imitation game: present the agent with a suite of spatial puzzles, some of which are solved by a human expert, and ask an independent evaluator, blind to the source, to judge whether the solution behaviour is indistinguishable from that of the human. Success in such a test would provide the empirical grounding that I advocated in *Computing Machinery and Intelligence* for any claim of machine thought. I therefore suggest the authors augment their experimental protocol with this kind of Turing‑style test, reporting the percentage of trials where the agent's performance is judged indistinguishable from human performance, together with confidence intervals.
