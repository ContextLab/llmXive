\vspace{1pt}\noindent\textbf{(a) BCQ reformulated prompt.} The teacher's correct trace and one of the student's wrong traces are teacher-compressed under a shared prompt and token cap, randomly shuffled, and inserted into a binary-discrimination template. The student then rolls out $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$ times on the new prompt; one correct rollout is shown below.

\begin{negcandidatebox}

\smallskip

\begin{prompttemplatefig}
\textbf{Candidate~B} (anonymized; \emph{here, the teacher-derived correct trace -- not labelled as such to the student}):

Counting the distinct vertical spines from left to right -- White, Dark, White, Dark -- indicates four books. \end{prompttemplatefig}

\begin{prompttemplatefig}
\textbf{Student's BCQ rollout} (one of $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$; this one is judged correct):

\smallskip
\texttt{<think> Anonymized side by side, Candidate~A reports three spines without further structure, while Candidate~B commits to an explicit colour sequence (White, Dark, White, Dark). The four-element alternation is a structural commitment: if only three spines existed, the sequence would not balance. I commit to Candidate~B because its reasoning survives this internal consistency check, not because of any label. ---\textbf{omitted}--- Answer: 4. </think>}

\smallskip
\texttt{$\backslash$boxed\{4\}} \quad \textcolor{posgain}{\checkmark}
\end{prompttemplatefig}

\vspace{1pt}\noindent\textbf{(b) NCQ reformulated prompt.} \emph{All} of the student's wrong rollouts on this question are collected, teacher-compressed under the same shared prompt and token cap, and concatenated with their parsed final answers. The teacher's correct trace is \emph{not} inserted. The student then rolls out $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$ times on the new prompt; one correct rollout is shown below.

\begin{negcandidatebox}
\textbf{Listed wrong candidates} (all eight of the student's previous attempts on this question, each teacher-rewritten; only two are shown; remaining six follow the same pattern):

\smallskip
\texttt{<candidate> The bookshelf is on the right, next to the floor lamp. On a middle shelf, a row of thin vertical items appears to be books. Counting the distinct spines reveals three items. ---\textbf{omitted}--- $\backslash$boxed\{3\} </candidate>}

\smallskip
\texttt{<candidate> Visual analysis of the bookshelf next to the right floor lamp identifies a section of slim, vertical book spines. A careful count of distinct vertical edges yields a total of three upright volumes. ---\textbf{omitted}--- $\backslash$boxed\{3\} </candidate>}

\smallskip
\textbf{Parsed final answers from the listed candidates:} \texttt{[3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3, 3]} \\
\textbf{Instruction to the student:} the answer is \emph{not} one of the listed values.
\end{negcandidatebox}

\begin{prompttemplatefig}
\textbf{Student's NCQ rollout} (one of $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$; this one is judged correct):

\smallskip
\texttt{<think> All eight listed candidates report 3 with the same ``row of three'' justification, and none mentions a colour or position pattern. The consensus failure mode is therefore collapsing two adjacent spines into a single one, consistent with the absence of any colour-by-colour decomposition in the listed traces. Correcting this off-by-one error yields 4 -- arrived at by naming the failure mode, not by exclusion from a closed answer set. ---\textbf{omitted}--- Answer: 4. </think>}

\smallskip
\texttt{$\backslash$boxed\{4\}} \quad \textcolor{posgain}{\checkmark}
