\texttt{The graph shows the relationship between the distance an airplane has covered (y-axis, in miles) and the amount of time it has been flying (x-axis, in hours). What is the speed of the airplane in miles per hour?}

\smallskip
\textbf{Reference answer:} \texttt{500} \quad (\textbf{Student's plain rollouts:} all eight answer \texttt{400}, misreading the line at $x\!=\!1$ as $y\!=\!400$ rather than $y\!=\!500$.)
\end{questionbox}

\vspace{1pt}\noindent\textbf{(a) BCQ reformulated prompt.} The teacher's correct trace and one of the student's wrong traces are teacher-compressed under a shared prompt and token cap, randomly shuffled, and inserted into a binary-discrimination template. The student then rolls out $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$ times on the new prompt; one correct rollout is shown below.

\begin{negcandidatebox}
\textbf{Candidate~A} (anonymized; \emph{here, the student-derived wrong trace -- not labelled as such to the student}):

\smallskip
\texttt{The speed equals the slope of the distance-time line. Identifying clear grid points, the line passes through $(0,0)$ and $(4, 1600)$. Dividing $1600$ by $4$ gives a constant speed of $400$ mph, consistent with $(1, 400)$. ---\textbf{omitted}--- $\backslash$boxed\{400\}}
\end{negcandidatebox}

\begin{prompttemplatefig}
\textbf{Candidate~B} (anonymized; \emph{here, the teacher-derived correct trace -- not labelled as such to the student}):

\smallskip
\texttt{The speed corresponds to the slope of the line on the distance-time graph. Clear points on the line are $(0,0)$ and $(2, 1000)$. The speed is $(1000-0)/(2-0) = 500$ mph, consistent with $(1, 500)$ and the endpoint near $(4, 2000)$. ---\textbf{omitted}--- $\backslash$boxed\{500\}}
\end{prompttemplatefig}

\begin{prompttemplatefig}
\textbf{Student's BCQ rollout} (one of $G_{\rm S}\!=\!8$; this one is judged correct):

\smallskip
\texttt{<think> Both candidates assert specific grid readings, so the choice cannot be made by length or order. Candidate~A's endpoint $(4, 1600)$ would leave the line two labelled rows below the top of the $y$-axis. Candidate~B's chain $(2, 1000)$ and $(4, 2000)$ lies on exact labelled intersections, with $(4, 2000)$ coinciding with the arrowhead's terminating position. Only Candidate~B's chain is consistent on falsifiability grounds. ---\textbf{omitted}--- Answer: 500. </think>}

\smallskip
\texttt{$\backslash$boxed\{500\}} \quad \textcolor{posgain}{\checkmark}
\end{prompttemplatefig}

\vspace{1pt}\noindent\textbf{(b) NCQ reformulated prompt.} \emph{All} of the student's wrong rollouts on this question are collected, teacher-compressed under the same shared prompt and token cap, and concatenated with their parsed final answers. \begin{negcandidatebox}
\textbf{Listed wrong candidates} (all eight of the student's previous attempts on this question, each teacher-rewritten; only two are shown; remaining six follow the same pattern):

\smallskip

\smallskip
\texttt{<candidate> The line is linear through the origin. At $1$ hour the distance reads $400$ miles, and at $4$ hours the distance reads $1600$ miles. Dividing gives a rate of $400$ miles per hour. ---\textbf{omitted}--- $\backslash$boxed\{400\} </candidate>}

\smallskip
