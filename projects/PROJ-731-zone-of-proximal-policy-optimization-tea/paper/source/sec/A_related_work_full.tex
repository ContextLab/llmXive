\section{Full Related Work}
\label{sec:related_full}

This appendix expands the condensed discussion in Sec.~\ref{sec:related} into the three full paragraphs that motivate ZPPO.

\paragraph{Knowledge distillation for LLMs and VLMs.}
The dominant recipe for transferring a stronger model's competence to a smaller one is knowledge distillation, which trains the student to imitate the teacher's logits, hidden states, or sampled outputs~\citep{gou2021knowledge}. In \emph{off-policy} distillation, the target sequence is fixed -- a ground-truth response~\citep{hinton2015distilling,sanh2019distilbert} or a teacher rollout -- and the student is trained to match the teacher's per-token logits, with recent variants adaptively scheduling student-generated outputs to balance the training-inference mismatch~\citep{ko2024distillm}. In \emph{on-policy} distillation, the student instead samples its own response during training, that sequence is fed back into the teacher to obtain target logits, and the student is trained to match them~\citep{gu2024minillm,agarwal2024policy,lu2025onpolicydistillation,fu2026revisiting,bousselham2025vold,yang2026learning,zhang2026fast,wu2026lightning}. \emph{Self-distillation} removes the external teacher, instead using the student itself as the imitation target -- either via best-of-$N$ rejection sampling on its own rollouts~\citep{zelikman2022star,singh2023beyond}, or by conditioning the same model on privileged information (e.g., demonstrations, verified traces, or environment feedback) to act as an in-context self-teacher~\citep{shenfeld2026self,zhao2026self,hubotter2026reinforcement,yang2026self,kim2026does}. All three families lean on the same training signal, a logit distribution the student must match, and a recent line of analyses shows that this signal turns brittle once the student is much smaller than the teacher: the student memorizes, mode-seeks, or loses general capability beyond the training data~\citep{kim2026does,li2026rethinking,song2026survey}. ZPPO leaves this regime altogether. The teacher never enters the student's policy gradient and shows up only as a candidate inside the prompt.

\paragraph{Reinforcement learning post-training for reasoning.}
RL post-training built on PPO~\citep{schulman2017proximal} and group-relative variants such as GRPO~\citep{shao2024deepseekmath} and DAPO~\citep{yu2025dapo} has, with rule-based rewards, pushed reasoning on math, science, and broad knowledge tasks~\citep{guo2025deepseek,team2025kimi,liu2025understanding,he2025justrl,khatri2025art,liu2025prorl,hu2025brorl,hu2025reinforce++,liu2025dler,liu2026gdpo}, and the same recipe extends to multimodal reasoning on image--question pairs~\citep{chen2025r1v,yang2025r1,deng2025openvlthinker,zhou2025r1,liu2025visual,chen2025sft,zhou2025reinforced}. A complementary line scales RL compute either by prolonging training steps or by broadening rollouts per prompt to extend exploration~\citep{liu2025prorl,hu2025brorl}, regulates entropy to prevent the policy from collapsing onto its current peaks~\citep{cui2025entropy,wang2025arbitrary}, and probes whether RL extends a model's reasoning frontier or merely sharpens what the model already knows~\citep{yue2025does}. However, these advances do not solve the small student failure mode on questions where the student's every rollout still fails, the group advantage stays zero, and the question is silently filtered out as a zero-advantage sample~\citep{yu2025dapo}. The natural fix -- replacing a failed student rollout with a teacher's correct response inside the policy gradient -- breaks the on-policy assumption and induces severe policy drift; recent hybrid RL$+$distillation lines~\citep{xu2025kdrl,xu2026rlkd} keep the teacher in the policy gradient and therefore inherit the same brittleness. ZPPO instead \emph{moves teacher knowledge from the gradient to the prompt}: BCQ and NCQ inject teacher and self-generated candidates as reformulated prompts on those same hard questions, so every response token that enters the policy gradient is student-generated (on-policy at the response level).

\paragraph{Prompt replay, curriculum, and hint scaffolding for RL.}
A growing body of work re-introduces previously seen questions or rollouts in the spirit of classical experience replay~\citep{zhan2025exgrpo,zhang2025rlep,li2025repo,li2026dyjr,baroian2026prompt}, or arranges questions by difficulty from easy to hard~\citep{zhang2025clpo,mahrooghi2026goldilocks}. Both lines re-prioritize hard questions but neither provides the \emph{guidance} that distinguishes Vygotsky's zone of proximal development~\citep{vygotsky1978mind} from mere difficulty ordering. A complementary line scaffolds the student through difficult problems by injecting hints: \emph{prefix-based} methods seed a partial reasoning trace into the response so the rollout is part teacher and the policy gradient is no longer strictly on-policy~\citep{zhang2025stephint,zhang2025bread,liu2026palign}, while \emph{prompt-injected} hints add pre-authored guidance to the prompt only, keeping the response on-policy and optionally correcting the resulting off-policy bias by importance sampling~\citep{li2025staying,nath2025adaptive}. ZPPO is also a prompt-side method on the same hard questions, but its in-prompt content is BCQ and NCQ candidates assembled at training time from the current rollout batch -- not pre-authored hint text -- so candidates change dynamically as fresh rollouts are sampled throughout training, with the prompt replay buffer existing solely to amplify these reformulated prompts on questions inside the student's current zone of proximal development.
