\section{Teacher capability on the same benchmarks}
\label{sec:appendix:teacher_capability}

This appendix focuses solely on the $27$B teacher used throughout the paper. We report its per-benchmark accuracy on the exact $10$ LLM, $16$ VLM, and $5$ Video benchmarks used throughout the paper (Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix:benchmarks}), evaluated with the same decoding settings, judges, and benchmark harness as the main-paper tables (Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:llm}--Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:video}). The cross-teacher comparison ($4$B/$9$B/$27$B teachers in Fig.~\ref{fig:teacher_scale}) is presented in the body (Sec.~\ref{sec:experiments:discussion}); this appendix does not expand it. The teacher-size sweep in Fig.~\ref{fig:teacher_scale} keeps the student initialization, the ZPPO-$77$K training data, the rollout-step budget, the candidate-compression prompt, and every hyperparameter in Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:hyperparameters} identical to the $27$B-teacher headline run; only the teacher checkpoint changes between the $4$B/$9$B/$27$B variants.

\subsection{Discussion}
\label{sec:appendix:teacher_capability:disc}

Two observations on the $27$B teacher link Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:vs_teacher} and Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:llm}--Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:video} back to the per-benchmark structure of ZPPO's gains.

\textbf{(1)~The $27$B teacher's accuracy varies sharply across benchmarks.} Even at $27$B, the teacher is far from saturated on the hard math/reasoning slices: HLE ($16.0$), OCR$^\text{ZH}$ ($33.5$), BabyV ($30.4$), and OCR$^\text{EN}$ ($55.7$) all sit well below the macro-averages. BCQ candidates can therefore exist on hard benchmarks (HLE, BabyV, $\dots$) because the teacher still occasionally solves what the student cannot, but the candidate pool is intrinsically thinner there than on saturated benchmarks (AI2D, MMLU-Rd, $\dots$), and ZPPO's gains correspondingly correlate with how often the teacher--student gap on a benchmark is non-trivial but non-empty.

\textbf{(2)~A ZPPO-trained $9$B student approaches the $27$B teacher on several benchmarks.} Per-benchmark, the $9$B$+$ZPPO row of Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:llm}--Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:video} sits within ${\le}1.0$~pp of the $27$B teacher on AIME25 ($70.0$ vs.\ $70.0$), OCR$^\text{EN}$ ($56.7$ vs.\ $55.7$), InfoVQA, MultiCh, MVerse, MVista, MM-Vet, and WeMath. We do not interpret these proximate values as the student strictly surpassing the teacher: the per-run noise band on single ZPPO runs is comparable to the residual gaps. The largest remaining gaps (HLE, VBlind, VMME) line up with benchmarks where the $27$B teacher itself is least saturated -- exactly the benchmarks on which BCQ has the least leverage because the teacher cannot supply many extra solved instances the student is missing.

Putting the two together, the BCQ-pool argument can be read straight off Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:vs_teacher}--Tab.~\ref{tab:appendix:teacher_capability:video}. Under the $27$B teacher we use throughout, ZPPO's per-benchmark gains follow wherever the teacher--student capability gap is non-trivial but non-empty, and by $9$B the student has closed in on the teacher across a good part of the suite.
