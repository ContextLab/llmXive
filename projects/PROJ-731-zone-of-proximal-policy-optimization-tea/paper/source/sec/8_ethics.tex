\section*{Ethical Considerations}
\label{sec:ethics}

ZPPO is a post-training recipe that builds on the publicly released Qwen3.5 family (open-weight VLMs at $0.8$B--$27$B scale)~\citep{qwen3.5}. We do not modify or filter the base models' pretraining data; any social, demographic, linguistic, or modality biases present in the upstream pretraining of Qwen3.5 are inherited by the post-trained students. Our reward signal (rule-based exact-match grader plus LLM-as-a-judge for free-form questions) targets answer correctness on reasoning benchmarks, not safety, fairness, or harm reduction, so ZPPO is not a substitute for dedicated safety alignment such as RLHF, Constitutional AI, or red-teaming. Practitioners deploying ZPPO-trained checkpoints should pair them with an upstream safety alignment stage and downstream content moderation appropriate to the deployment domain. Beyond inherited pretraining bias, dataset-level bias and spurious visual--linguistic correlations are a distinct concern for multimodal models, addressed by debiasing and causal approaches~\citep{10222502,KIM2026112173}; ZPPO's correctness-only reward neither introduces nor corrects such biases, so these directions remain complementary to our recipe.
