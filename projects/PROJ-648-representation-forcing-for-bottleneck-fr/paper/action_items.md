## paper_reviewer_scientific_evidence — verdict: minor_revision

- **[science]** Table 1 (line 235) reports GenEval scores for 'RF-Pixel' but lacks standard deviations or confidence intervals. Given the stochastic nature of diffusion sampling and the small number of prompts typically used in GenEval, effect sizes are difficult to assess without variance metrics. Please report mean ± std over multiple seeds or runs.
- **[science]** The ablation in Table 3a (line 365) shows a massive jump from 0.25 to 0.76 for Pixel+RF. However, the text does not specify the number of training iterations or compute budget for the 'w/o RF' baseline. If the baseline was under-trained compared to the RF variant, the effect size is confounded. Confirm equal training steps and compute.
- **[science]** The claim that Pixel+RF outperforms VAE+RF on understanding (Table 2, line 310) relies on a single run per configuration. With 8 benchmarks, the probability of random fluctuation favoring one model is non-negligible. Please provide statistical significance tests (e.g., paired t-tests) or results from multiple random seeds to support the 'outperforms' claim.
- **[science]** The online vector quantization algorithm (Algorithm 1, line 520) uses a momentum update with decay 0.9999. The stability of this codebook update is critical for the 'Representation Forcing' signal. The paper lacks an analysis of codebook collapse or utilization rates over training. Please include a plot or table showing codebook utilization to prove the representations are not degenerate.

## paper_reviewer_statistical_analysis — verdict: minor_revision

- **[science]** Table 1 (GenEval) and Table 2 (Understanding) report single-point performance metrics without any measure of statistical uncertainty (e.g., standard deviation, confidence intervals) or significance testing. Given the controlled ablation study design (Pixel vs. Pixel+RF), the authors must report variance across multiple seeds or runs to substantiate claims of 'significant' improvement (e.g., the +4.3 MMMU gain) and rule out random fluctuation.
- **[science]** The ablation study in Table 3 (specifically 3a and 3b) compares models with large performance gaps (0.25 vs 0.76). While the magnitude suggests robustness, the text lacks a formal statistical test (e.g., paired t-test or bootstrap) to confirm these differences are statistically significant rather than artifacts of a single random seed or specific data split.
- **[science]** The paper claims Pixel+RF outperforms VAE+RF on '6 out of 8 benchmarks' (Sec 4.2). This is a count of wins, not a statistical comparison. The authors should provide a statistical analysis (e.g., Wilcoxon signed-rank test) across the benchmarks to determine if the observed superiority is statistically significant, rather than relying on a simple majority count.
