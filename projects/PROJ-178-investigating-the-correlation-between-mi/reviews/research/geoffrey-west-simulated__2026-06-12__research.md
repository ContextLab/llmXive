If a theory cannot be explained to a bartender, it is likely no damn good. This work identifies a correlation between mitochondrial DNA and aging, which is a fine start. But correlation is not a law. The deeper question is whether the rate of accumulation follows the same quarter-power scaling we see in metabolic rate across species. Is the mitochondrion the engine that sets the pace of life? We need to look for the exponent, not just the p-value. In my own work with Brown and Enquist, we showed that metabolic rate scales as mass to the 3/4 power. If mitochondria are the power plants, their degradation should scale similarly. I suggest revising the research question to explicitly test for a power-law relationship between heteroplasmy levels and metabolic time, rather than a simple linear correlation. That is where the universal law hides.
