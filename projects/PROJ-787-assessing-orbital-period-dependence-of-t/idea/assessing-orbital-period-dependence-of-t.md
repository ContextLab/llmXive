# Assessing Orbital Period Dependence of the Exoplanet Radius Gap

**Field**: physics

This project investigates whether the exoplanet radius gap, a deficit of planets between 1.5 and 2.0 Earth radii, exhibits a systematic shift across different orbital periods in the Kepler public catalog. Understanding this trend is critical for distinguishing between competing atmospheric loss mechanisms, such as photoevaporation versus core-powered mass loss, which predict different scaling behaviors. The proposed approach involves downloading the Kepler DR25 dataset, filtering for confirmed planets with precise radius and period measurements, and applying Gaussian mixture modeling to identify the gap location within discrete period bins. This analysis relies entirely on archived data accessible via HTTPS, ensuring compatibility with standard compute environments. We anticipate that statistical deviations in the gap position may provide evidence favoring one theoretical framework over the other, offering insights into planetary evolution without requiring new observational data.
