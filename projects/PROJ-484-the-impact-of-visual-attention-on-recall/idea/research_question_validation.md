## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the relationship between attentional allocation (gaze) and memory consolidation (recall) under emotional valence, which is a substantive psychological phenomenon. The methodology (mixed-effects regression) is a tool to answer the question, not the subject of the inquiry itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (gaze fixation duration) is measured during stimulus presentation, while the predicted variable (recall accuracy) is measured after a delay. These are temporally distinct and methodologically independent measures, avoiding mechanical construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: fail

The input explicitly flags this idea as a duplicate of an existing project ("Verdict: duplicate of RSVP Emotional Memory Recall"), which undermines publishability regardless of the result. Additionally, the emotional enhancement effect is well-documented; without a novel moderator or condition, the positive result is predictable and the null result is easily dismissed as methodological noise.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a specific domain relationship (attentional allocation predicting recall accuracy) rather than a constraint on the implementation (e.g., "Can we collect 500MB of data?"). It focuses on the mechanism of encoding rather than the feasibility of data acquisition.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validator_revise

The core question is scientifically sound but currently redundant due to the duplicate flag and established literature. To salvage novelty, the scope must be narrowed to a specific population or condition where the mechanism is less certain, such as clinical anxiety.
