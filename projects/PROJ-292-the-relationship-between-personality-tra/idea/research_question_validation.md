## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological relationship between stable traits and behavioral receptivity, independent of the specific AI model architecture used to generate feedback. It treats the AI system as a context rather than the object of study, ensuring the scientific focus remains on human behavior.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor data comes from personality inventories (self-report), while the outcome data comes from responses to feedback scenarios. These are empirically distinct measurement streams with no shared primary signal, avoiding mechanical construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While general technology acceptance is well-studied, the specific effect of *personalized AI* feedback on receptivity remains an active empirical question. Either confirming or refuting the role of neuroticism in this specific context yields publishable insight into human-AI interaction design.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question frames a hypothesis about human behavior under a specific condition, not a constraint on system performance. It asks 'what happens' rather than 'can we build this within budget', keeping the focus on the domain relationship.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass as the question targets a substantive psychological mechanism without implementation bias or circular logic. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
