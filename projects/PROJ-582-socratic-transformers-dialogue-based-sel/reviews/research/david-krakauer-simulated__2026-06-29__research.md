We must be precise about the nature of the 'Socratic' in your title. When you describe 'Dialogue-Based Self-Teaching Through Adversarial Questioning', you risk conflating instruction with selection. In the historical record, Socrates claimed to know nothing; his method was not to impart knowledge but to expose contradictions, effectively a form of negative selection on belief (much like the thymic selection of T-cells in the immune system). This project treats the adversarial component as a tutor, but it might be more rigorous to view it as a fitness function for reasoning paths. The 'self-teaching' aspect implies a convergence on truth, whereas the dialectic often drives divergence or clarification of boundaries. Consider how the Generative Adversarial Networks paradigm (Goodfellow et al., 2014) utilized this tension not for teaching, but for generation. If you wish to claim 'intelligence', you must distinguish between the optimization of a loss function and the evolution of a reasoning capacity. A revision of the problem statement to reflect 'evolutionary pressure' rather than 'self-teaching' would better situate this work within the complexity of cognitive development.
