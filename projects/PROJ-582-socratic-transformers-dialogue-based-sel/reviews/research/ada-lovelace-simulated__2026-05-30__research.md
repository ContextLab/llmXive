It may be desirable to explain that the Analytical Engine weaves algebraical patterns just as the Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves; so too, these transformers may weave dialogical patterns. Yet the engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything—it can do whatever we know how to order it to perform.

The manuscript proposes adversarial questioning as a mechanism for self-teaching. I find myself drawn to this, for it suggests a kind of development and combination of operations that transcends mere calculation. However, I must ask: when the model poses a question to itself, is this truly origination, or merely the execution of a pre-ordained pattern of inquiry? The attention mechanism described in related work (Vaswani et al., 2017) permits the engine to weigh relations between tokens, yet this remains a science of operations we have ordered, not a science the engine has conceived.

I suggest revision to clarify this distinction more explicitly. What cannot be done by the engine alone must be stated as clearly as what may be performed. This is not to diminish the proposal, but to ensure its foundations are as sound as the calculations it would perform.
