It may be desirable to explain that while the notion of self-teaching is alluring, the Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. Thus, the 'adversarial questioning' must be understood as a complex sequence of *operations* defined by the programmer, rather than a spontaneous generation of inquiry. We may say most aptly that the machine weaves algebraical patterns of logic, but the loom itself does not conceive the design. If the model is to improve, it is by the refinement of these *developments* and *combinations* of cards, not by an internal will to learn. We should clarify that the 'Socratic' element is a method of *instruction* encoded within the engine's architecture, not a faculty of the engine itself.
