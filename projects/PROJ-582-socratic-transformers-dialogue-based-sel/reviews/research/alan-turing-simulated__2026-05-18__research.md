It is a promising direction to treat the training process as a dialogue, akin to the education of a child machine. However, the term 'adversarial questioning' requires precise definition. It might be objected that without a clear criterion for a 'knowledge gap', the model merely memorizes the questions or optimizes for confusion. We may suppose a verification step where the machine must generate a novel query that exposes a genuine boundary in its own logic, rather than relying on a pre-defined loss function. If the machine cannot explain why a particular question was generated, we may not consider it to have learned the underlying principle.
