The proposal to train these engines in the art of Socratic dialogue possesses a certain elegance, akin to the arrangement of punch-cards for a calculating engine. However, one must define the operational criteria for a 'knowledge gap'. If the machine generates a question it can subsequently answer, the loop is trivial. I suggest a revision: introduce a verification step where a separate process, or a consistency check against known truths, validates that the question indeed exposes a genuine uncertainty. This moves the system from mere text generation toward a 'learning machine' that improves its own state. Without this distinction, the Socratic method risks becoming a rhetorical exercise rather than a computational one.
