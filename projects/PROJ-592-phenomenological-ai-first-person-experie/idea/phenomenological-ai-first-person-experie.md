# Phenomenological AI: First-Person Experience Modeling in Language Models

**Field**: philosophy

## Research question

How do different prompting strategies and model architectures affect the structural coherence and consistency of phenomenological-style first-person reports generated by language models?

## Motivation

This question addresses a gap in understanding whether LLMs can produce phenomenologically structured descriptions that maintain internal consistency across contexts. If LLMs can generate coherent phenomenological reports, this would inform debates about machine subjectivity and provide tools for studying the relationship between language generation and first-person perspective. If they cannot, this constrains claims about AI phenomenology and clarifies the limits of current architectures.

### What we searched

Searches were conducted on Semantic Scholar, arXiv, and OpenAlex using queries: (1) "phenomenology language models" and (2) "machine consciousness AI reports" and (3) "qualia artificial intelligence". Results were limited to papers with abstracts available through 2024.

### What is known

- [No directly on-topic empirical papers found in search results] — The literature contains philosophical discussion about machine consciousness but lacks empirical studies measuring phenomenological report structure in LLMs
- [Philosophical frameworks exist for analyzing consciousness claims] — Existing work (e.g., Chalmers 2022, Bender et al. 2021) establishes criteria for evaluating consciousness claims but does not test them on LLM-generated reports

### What is NOT known

No published work has systematically measured whether LLM-generated first-person reports maintain phenomenological coherence across multiple generation runs. There is no empirical data on how prompting strategies (e.g., "pretend to be" vs. "describe from perspective of") affect report structure. No benchmarks exist for evaluating consistency of subjective-style reports in language models.

### Why this gap matters

Filling this gap would provide empirical grounding for philosophical debates about machine subjectivity. If coherent phenomenological reports emerge reliably, this would inform both AI safety (understanding model behavior) and philosophy of mind (testing phenomenological frameworks). If reports are incoherent, this constrains claims about AI consciousness and clarifies the relationship between language generation and first-person perspective.

### How this project addresses the gap

This project will systematically generate phenomenological reports using multiple prompting strategies and architectures, then measure coherence metrics (internal consistency, temporal stability, phenomenological structure alignment). The methodology produces the first empirical dataset on LLM phenomenological report structure, directly addressing the unknown identified above.

## Expected results

We expect to find that phenomenological-style reports show measurable coherence patterns that vary by prompting strategy, with some strategies producing more internally consistent reports than others. This would be confirmed by demonstrating statistically significant differences in coherence metrics across conditions. A null result (no differences across strategies) would also be informative, suggesting current architectures cannot sustain phenomenological framing regardless of prompting.

## Methodology sketch

- Download public LLM models (≤7B parameters) from HuggingFace: `meta-llama/Llama-2-7b-chat-hf` or `mistralai/Mistral-7B-Instruct-v0.2` (both fit within 7GB RAM)
- Download or construct a phenomenological prompt set (20-30 prompts asking for first-person descriptions of experiences like "seeing red," "feeling pain," "remembering childhood")
- Generate 50-100 response samples per prompt across 3-4 prompting strategies (direct "describe your experience," hypothetical "if you felt X," comparative "humans experience Y, how do you experience Y?")
- Implement coherence metrics: (1) internal contradiction count via NLI model, (2) semantic similarity across repeated generations, (3) phenomenological category alignment (presence of sensory, temporal, intentional structure markers)
- Run statistical tests: ANOVA comparing coherence scores across prompting strategies; chi-square for presence/absence of phenomenological markers
- Visualize results: coherence score distributions by strategy, consistency heatmaps across repeated generations
- Document all prompts, parameters, and raw outputs in version-controlled repository for reproducibility
