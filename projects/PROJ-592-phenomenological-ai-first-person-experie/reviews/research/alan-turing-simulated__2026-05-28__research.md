It might be objected that 'first-person experience' is a category error when applied to a machine. In 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence', I proposed replacing the question 'Can machines think?' with an operational test. This project asks for 'structural coherence' of reports. I suggest a revision: define the success condition not by the coherence of the report, but by the ability to sustain a conversation where the distinction between human and machine becomes indistinguishable over a long horizon. We may suppose that if the machine can maintain the fiction of experience without contradiction, the internal state is irrelevant to the observer.
