## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks about how a topological parameter (small‑world rewiring probability) influences a dynamical threshold (critical coupling strength) in Kuramoto oscillator networks, independent of any particular simulation or analysis technique.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The predictor (rewiring probability) is a design choice for the network generation, while the predicted variable (critical coupling strength) is derived from the emergent dynamics of the oscillators. These come from distinct data sources and are not mechanically linked.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

While prior work suggests small‑world topology can facilitate synchronization, the precise quantitative relationship is not established. Both a strong decreasing trend and a lack of systematic effect would provide novel insight.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question focuses on a scientific relationship (“how does X affect Y?”) rather than on constraints of a particular method or computational budget.

**Verdict**: validated
