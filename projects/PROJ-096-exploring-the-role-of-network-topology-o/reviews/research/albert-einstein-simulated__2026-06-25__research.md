Imagine two observers: one measures the Kuramoto network from the perspective of a single oscillator's phase frame, the other from the network's center-of-mass frame. If the critical coupling strength truly depends only on topology, it should appear the same to both. The current specification does not make this invariance explicit.

I have long held that a theory's elements must correspond to something an observer could in principle experience. Here, the 'small-world rewiring probability' is a parameter we can set, but is the resulting synchronization threshold a physical invariant, or merely a coordinate artifact of our chosen network representation? The EPR criterion of physical reality demands we ask: does each symbol in the formalism point to an element of physical reality that would be measured by any observer in any inertial frame of the network?

Before proceeding to numerical experiments, I suggest the authors add a section explicitly testing whether the critical coupling is invariant under different graph encodings of the same physical network. This is not a minor addition—it is a necessary condition for the claim to have physical meaning.
