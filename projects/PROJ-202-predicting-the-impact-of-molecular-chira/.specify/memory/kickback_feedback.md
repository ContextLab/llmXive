## Unresolved concerns

- The plan explicitly states 'Claims are strictly associational' and 'observational design', yet the spec title and FR-003/FR-005 imply predicting the 'impact' of chirality. Correlating binding affinity differences with compound-level sensory ratings (FlavorDB) cannot establish that chirality *causes* the flavor difference, as sensory ratings are aggregate human perceptions, not receptor-specific binding events. The analysis design cannot support a causal claim of 'impact'.
- The plan proposes correlating 'ΔAffinity' (computed from docking 20 pairs × 5 receptors) with 'sensory rating differences'. However, FlavorDB provides compound-level ratings, not enantiomer-specific ratings. If the dataset lacks distinct sensory ratings for R vs L enantiomers (e.g., only 'Carvone' rating exists), the 'sensory rating difference' variable is undefined or zero, rendering the correlation analysis mathematically impossible or trivial.
- The plan reduces the sample to 10 pairs and 3 receptors to meet time limits. With N=10 pairs, the statistical power to detect a correlation (Spearman's ρ) between computational metrics and sensory ratings is extremely low. A non-significant result will be uninterpretable (could be no effect or underpowered), and the small N makes the Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction overly conservative, likely masking any true signal.
- The plan claims 'Multiple Scoring Functions' are required for robustness (FR-009), but the methodology section only details running SMINA/PLANTS on 'top 5 pairs'. This is a post-hoc selection bias. To validate robustness, the scoring function comparison must be performed on the *entire* dataset or a random subset, not just the 'best' docking results, otherwise the validation is circular.
- FR-009 requires validation using SMINA and PLANTS scoring functions. The plan.md 'Complexity Tracking' table explicitly rejects this: 'Relying on a single scoring function (Vina) risks method-specific bias; cross-validation with SMINA/PLANTS is essential...' yet the 'Methodology' section in research.md and the 'Running the Pipeline' steps in quickstart.md do not include a task to execute SMINA or PLANTS. The plan fails to implement the required robustness check.
- FR-010 mandates cross-referencing significant computational differences with known experimental binding constants from literature. The plan.md and research.md mention 'observational nature' and 'FlavorDB API fallback' but contain no task, data source, or logic to fetch or compare against external experimental binding constants (e.g., from PDBbind or literature) to mitigate circular validation.
- FR-007 requires a sensitivity analysis sweeping thresholds {0.4, 0.5, 0.6} kcal/mol. The plan.md 'Methodology' section mentions 'Sensitivity: Sweep threshold...' but the 'Running the Pipeline' steps in quickstart.md and the 'Statistical Analysis' script description in research.md do not explicitly define a task to generate the required CSV mapping threshold to significance count. The output schema in data-model.md lacks a 'threshold_kcal_mol' column in the statistical_analysis.csv (it exists in the contract but the plan steps don't confirm generation).
- SC-001 requires the total pipeline to run within 6 hours. The plan.md 'Compute Feasibility' section admits the original 20/5 target exceeds the 4-hour US-1 limit and decides to 'Reduce dataset to 10 pairs / 3 receptors'. While this addresses the 4-hour US-1 constraint, the plan does not explicitly confirm that the *entire* pipeline (including the reduced MD and analysis steps) meets the 6-hour SC-001 limit with the new reduced dataset. The 100ps MD reduction is noted, but the total time budget for the full 10-pair/3-receptor run is not explicitly validated against the 6-hour SC-001.
- FR-004 mandates 1ns MD duration. The plan.md 'Methodology' and 'Compute Feasibility' sections explicitly state a decision to reduce this to '100ps' to meet time constraints. While a feasibility compromise, the plan does not include a corresponding amendment to the spec or a clear 'addressed by' annotation explaining how the 1ns requirement is satisfied or if the spec itself needs updating. The plan currently violates the explicit 1ns duration requirement of FR-004 without a formal spec change or explicit 'deferred' marker in the plan text.
- The plan assumes '200 docking jobs' (20 pairs × 2 enantiomers × 5 receptors) and '10 MD trajectories' can run within 4-6 hours on GitHub Actions free-tier (2 CPU, 7GB RAM). This is computationally infeasible. AutoDock Vina on CPU typically takes 2-10 minutes per ligand-receptor pair; 200 jobs would require 6-33 hours alone, exceeding the 4-hour US-1 limit. MD simulation of 1ns for 10 complexes on 2 CPU cores is physically impossible within 1 hour (typically requires hours per complex). The dataset size (20 pairs/5 receptors) must be reduced significantly (e.g., to 5 pairs/2 receptors) or the runtime constraints relaxed to match the hardware reality.
- The plan cites 'FlavorDB' for sensory ratings but admits 'No verified URL for FlavorDB is provided' and relies on an 'API (if reachable)'. FlavorDB is a static database, not a live API, and does not provide enantiomer-specific sensory ratings in a machine-readable format accessible via a simple fetch. The correlation step (FR-005) depends on this data. If the dataset cannot be programmatically retrieved with enantiomer-level granularity, the core research question (correlating chirality differences with perception) cannot be answered. A verified, accessible dataset with enantiomer-specific sensory data must be identified or the research question reframed.
- The plan selects '20 enantiomeric pairs' from a generic SMILES dataset (maykcaldas/smiles-transformers) without verifying that these specific pairs have known flavor properties or sensory ratings. The dataset is a general chemical library, not a curated flavor database. Without pre-verified flavor annotations and sensory ratings for the selected enantiomers, the 'correlation with human sensory ratings' step is impossible. The dataset selection strategy is inappropriate for the research question.
- Plan claims 'Multiple Scoring Functions' (SMINA/PLANTS) are required by FR-009, but the 'Compute Feasibility' section in research.md explicitly reduces the dataset to 10 pairs/3 receptors to meet time limits. FR-009 requires validating 'at least 3 of the top 5' pairs with SMINA/PLANTS. The plan does not explicitly state if the reduced dataset (10 pairs) still supports the FR-009 requirement or if FR-009 is being dropped. This creates a conflict between the plan's stated compliance and the research feasibility adjustments.
- Plan states MD step is 'Required by FR-004 to validate docking poses dynamically.' However, FR-004 in spec.md mandates a '1ns duration'. The research.md explicitly states '1ns... is physically impossible' and reduces it to '100ps'. The plan.md 'Complexity Tracking' justifies the MD step but fails to acknowledge the deviation from the spec's 1ns requirement, creating a direct contradiction between the plan's justification and the spec's functional requirement.
- Plan claims 'COMPLIANT' for Principle VI (Computational Chemistry Methodology) which mandates 'simulation length (10 ns)'. The research.md and plan.md both explicitly reduce this to 100ps (or 1ns in the plan summary) due to hardware constraints. The plan cannot claim compliance with a constitutional principle that explicitly requires a 10ns simulation when the implementation plan explicitly rejects that duration. This is a fundamental violation of Principle VI.
- Plan states 'Scale/Scope: 200 docking jobs (20 pairs × 2 enantiomers × 5 receptors)'. However, the 'research.md' section 'Compute Feasibility & Constraints' explicitly decides to 'Reduce dataset to 10 pairs / 3 receptors' to meet time limits. The plan.md 'Technical Context' section is inconsistent with the 'research.md' decision log, presenting the original (infeasible) scope rather than the adjusted scope.
- Plan claims 'COMPLIANT' for Principle VII (Statistical Rigor) which mandates '10,000-iteration bootstrapped resampling'. The plan.md and research.md describe Shapiro-Wilk, t-test/Wilcoxon, and FDR, but do not mention the 10,000-iteration bootstrap required by the Constitution. The plan claims compliance without evidence of the required bootstrap step.
- The plan proposes correlating 'ΔAffinity' (difference in docking scores between enantiomers) with 'sensory rating differences'. However, the sensory data source (FlavorDB) is described as providing 'compound-level sensory ratings' where receptor-specific ratings are unavailable. Enantiomers (R/L) of the same compound (e.g., Carvone) often have distinct sensory profiles (e.g., spearmint vs. caraway). If the dataset only provides a single 'Sensory_Rating' per compound ID (ignoring chirality), the 'sensory rating difference' for an enantiomeric pair is definitionally zero. Correlating a non-zero ΔAffinity with a zero sensory difference yields a null result by definition, not an empirical finding. If the dataset *does* distinguish enantiomers, the plan fails to cite a verified source for enantiomer-specific sensory data, making the validation target undefined.
- The validation target (Sensory Rating) is likely not independent of the predictor construction if the sensory data is compound-averaged. The plan assumes 'Sensory_Rating' exists for 'R' and 'L' types separately in the data model, but the research.md explicitly states 'FlavorDB lacks sensory ratings for a specific enantiomer' and the system will 'skip correlation'. This creates a circular logic: the analysis can only proceed if the sensory data *already* distinguishes the enantiomers, which contradicts the stated limitation of the data source. If the data does not distinguish them, the 'difference' is zero, and the correlation is trivial.
- The plan claims to validate docking results using SMINA and PLANTS to ensure robustness. However, SMINA is a fork of AutoDock Vina using the same scoring function logic (or very similar). PLANTS uses a different scoring function (ChemPLP). If the validation target is 'binding affinity', and the predictor is 'binding affinity', comparing two docking scores (Vina vs. SMINA) is a test of scoring function agreement, not a validation of the *biological* claim (stereoselectivity). The plan lacks an independent experimental ground truth (e.g., IC50, Kd) for the specific enantiomer-receptor pairs to validate the *direction* of the stereoselectivity. Without experimental Kd data, the 'validation' is merely checking if two computational models agree, which is not a validation of the scientific hypothesis.
- The plan reduces MD simulation from 1ns to 100ps to meet the 1-hour constraint. In computational chemistry, 100ps is insufficient for conformational sampling of protein-ligand complexes to reach equilibrium or observe meaningful binding/unbinding events. The resulting 'interaction fingerprints' and 'stability' metrics will be dominated by the initial docking pose and thermal noise rather than true dynamic stability. This renders the MD step scientifically non-informative for validating the docking pose, turning it into a 'feasibility check' that cannot support the claim of 'conformational validity'.
- The plan acknowledges the observational nature but fails to address the fundamental confounding variable: the receptor models are AlphaFold predictions, not experimental structures. Olfactory receptors are notoriously difficult to model due to flexible loops and lack of homologous templates. The error in the receptor structure (pLDDT < 70 in binding pocket) will likely dwarf the subtle energy differences (0.5 kcal/mol) between enantiomers. The plan treats the receptor structure as a fixed, accurate input, ignoring that the 'signal' (stereoselectivity) is smaller than the 'noise' (modeling error). This makes the empirical result (correlation) scientifically uninterpretable.
