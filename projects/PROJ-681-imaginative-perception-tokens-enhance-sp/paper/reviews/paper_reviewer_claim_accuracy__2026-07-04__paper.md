The paper's central claims regarding state-of-the-art performance and the efficacy of Imaginative Perception Tokens (IPT) are fundamentally undermined by the citation of non-existent or future-dated models as baselines and training sources.

Specifically, the results tables (Table 1 in the main text, Table `tab:pt_breakdown` in the supplement) list "GPT-5", "GPT-5.1", "Gemini 2.5 Flash", "Gemini 3 Flash", "Qwen3-VL-8B", and "InternVL3.5-8B" as comparison points. As of the current date, none of these models exist in the public record. GPT-5 has not been released, Gemini's latest major version is 1.5, and Qwen/InternVL are currently at version 2.x. The inclusion of these models renders the reported accuracy percentages (e.g., GPT-5 scoring 61.1% on EgoDir, Gemini 3 Flash scoring 96.2% on Real+Arr) unverifiable and scientifically invalid. A claim of "state-of-the-art" performance cannot be supported by comparisons against hallucinated baselines.

Furthermore, the methodology section states that "Text CoT generation" targets were generated by "GPT-5.1". Since this model does not exist, the provenance of the training data is unclear. If the authors used a different model (e.g., GPT-4o), this must be corrected immediately. If the results for these baselines were simulated or hallucinated, the paper's core contribution is invalidated.

While the internal consistency of the authors' own model (BAGEL) across different training regimes appears mathematically consistent within the provided tables, the external validity of the paper's conclusions is destroyed by the reliance on non-existent external references. The paper cannot be accepted until all baselines are replaced with real, verifiable models and the training data generation process is clarified with existing technology.
