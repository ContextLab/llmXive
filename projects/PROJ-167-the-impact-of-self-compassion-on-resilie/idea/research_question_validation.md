## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological mechanism (moderation of feedback effects by self-compassion) independent of any specific computational method or algorithm. It focuses on the relationship between constructs rather than the performance of a specific tool.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (self-compassion scores from SCS) and the predicted variables (changes in anxiety, rumination, self-efficacy) are derived from distinct questionnaire subscales measuring different psychological constructs. While both rely on self-report, they are not mechanically derived from the same primary signal, avoiding circular construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both positive and null results are informative: confirmation supports the protective role of self-compassion in feedback contexts, while a null result would challenge the generalizability of self-compassion interventions for external evaluation threats. Domain knowledge does not predetermine the outcome to the point of triviality.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (buffering effect of self-compassion on feedback impact) rather than implementation constraints like runtime or hardware. It frames the inquiry around psychological mechanisms.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass as the question targets a substantive psychological mechanism without methodological narrowing or circularity. The project is ready for initialization.
