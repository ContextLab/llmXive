## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check
**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive psychological mechanism: whether self-compassion acts as a moderator (buffer) against the distress caused by negative feedback. The specific statistical method (moderated regression) and the dataset source are tools to test this, but the core inquiry is about the relationship between a personality trait and an emotional response to a social stressor, independent of the computational implementation.

### Circularity check
**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (Self-Compassion Scale scores) is derived from self-reported personality traits, while the predicted variable (change in state anxiety, rumination, self-efficacy) is derived from psychological state measures taken before and after an external experimental manipulation (negative feedback). These are distinct constructs measured at different times using different scales, ensuring the relationship is empirical rather than mechanically guaranteed by shared data sources.

### Triviality check
**Verdict**: concern

While self-compassion is theoretically linked to resilience, the specific buffering effect against *experimentally manipulated* negative feedback in this precise configuration (anxiety, rumination, self-efficacy) is a strong hypothesis in the literature. A null result might be attributed to the specific context or the dataset's limitations rather than falsifying the theory entirely, potentially making the outcome less definitive than a clear "yes" or "no." However, given the claim of a "gap" in direct experimental tests, a null result would still be scientifically informative by challenging the generalizability of self-compassion interventions to high-stakes feedback scenarios.

### Question-narrowing check
**Verdict**: pass

The question clearly names a domain relationship (self-compassion moderating feedback impact) rather than focusing on implementation constraints like computational budget, model architecture, or runtime. The mention of specific scales and statistical tests serves to operationalize the domain question, not to narrow the scientific inquiry to a methodological benchmark.

### Overall verdict
**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass; the research question targets a genuine psychological mechanism using independent variables and outcomes. The "concern" in the triviality check is minor and relates to the strength of the null hypothesis interpretation rather than a fundamental flaw in the question's structure. The project is ready to proceed to initialization.
