## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive psychological relationship between perspective-taking interventions and moral outrage responses in online contexts. The experimental method (prompting participants) serves as the intervention rather than being the subject of the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor is an experimental manipulation (perspective-taking instruction vs. control summarization), and the predicted variable is self-reported moral outrage scores from a validated scale. These are independent measures—the intervention is not derived from the same signal as the outcome.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would establish a low-cost, scalable intervention for reducing online polarization. A null result would be equally informative, suggesting that online contexts differ from offline settings where perspective-taking has shown promise, or that brief interventions are insufficient for affective change. Either outcome advances the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (perspective-taking → moral outrage attenuation) rather than implementation constraints. It does not fixate on specific platform features, participant numbers, or statistical thresholds as the core inquiry.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed as a substantive psychological inquiry independent of methodological artifacts, with both positive and null outcomes being theoretically informative. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
