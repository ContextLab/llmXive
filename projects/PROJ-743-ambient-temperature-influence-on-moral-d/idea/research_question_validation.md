## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether a physical environmental factor (ambient temperature) influences a cognitive‑behavioral outcome (moral‑decision response time). It does not hinge on any particular measurement or analysis technique, so the phenomenon stands independent of the chosen statistical model.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor: ambient temperature obtained from NOAA weather stations. Predicted variable: individual response latency recorded in the Moral Machine dataset. These data streams are distinct (meteorological vs behavioral) and are not derived from the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a significant temperature effect and a null effect would be informative. A positive finding would extend embodied cognition theories to moral judgment speed; a null result would suggest that moral decision latency is robust to ambient thermal context, contrary to many other cognitive domains.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (“ambient temperature affect response time of moral decisions”) rather than imposing constraints on a particular method, model, or computational budget.
