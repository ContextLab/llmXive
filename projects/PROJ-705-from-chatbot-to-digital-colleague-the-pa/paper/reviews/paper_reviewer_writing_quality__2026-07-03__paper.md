The manuscript presents a compelling narrative on the shift from chatbots to digital colleagues, but several structural and grammatical issues impede smooth reading. The most significant problem is the frequent use of sentence fragments and run-on sentences that force the reader to pause and re-parse. For instance, in Section 3.1, the standalone sentence "The shift is from querying external oracles to inhabiting a persistent host" lacks a main verb and context, breaking the flow. Similarly, Section 4.1 contains a semicolon splice that joins two independent clauses without a clear logical connector, creating a jarring transition.

Paragraph structure also needs attention. Several paragraphs, particularly in Sections 2.2 and 3.2, begin with a detail or a fragment rather than a clear topic sentence, leaving the reader to infer the paragraph's purpose. For example, the opening of Section 3.2 jumps directly into defining skills without first establishing the broader context of the "Workspace + Skill" paradigm. Additionally, some sentences are overly long and complex, such as the one in Section 5.1 that lists multiple error types without proper punctuation, making it difficult to follow the argument.

To improve readability, the authors should ensure every paragraph starts with a clear topic sentence, break up long sentences into shorter, more digestible units, and avoid sentence fragments. These changes will help the reader move through the text without unnecessary friction, allowing the paper's strong conceptual framework to shine through.
