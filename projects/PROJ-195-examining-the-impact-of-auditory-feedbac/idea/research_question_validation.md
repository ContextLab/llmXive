## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly asks about the relationship between an experimental manipulation (auditory feedback perturbation) and biological outcomes (neural activity and behavioral performance). It does not frame the inquiry around the performance limits of a specific algorithm or computational budget, but rather seeks to understand the underlying auditory-motor integration mechanism.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (feedback condition: normal vs. perturbed) is an experimental input defined by the task protocol, while the predicted variables (BOLD signal and reaction time) are measured outputs from fMRI and response logging. These are independent measurement modalities; the neural activity is not mathematically derived from the feedback condition itself.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While auditory feedback affects motor learning is established, the specific neural correlates of *error* signals (perturbed vs. normal) in motor-auditory cortices and their quantitative link to behavioral degradation remain an active empirical question. A null result (no correlation between error signals and behavior) would challenge current models of sensory prediction error processing, making either outcome informative.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a substantive domain relationship (auditory perturbation → cortical activity → behavior) rather than a constraint on implementation (e.g., "Can GLM detect this in 6 hours?"). The focus remains on the neuroscientific mechanism of adaptation.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass; the research question is well-framed as a substantive inquiry into neural mechanisms rather than a methodological benchmark or circular construction. The project is ready to advance to initialization without requiring a reframing of the core scientific question.
