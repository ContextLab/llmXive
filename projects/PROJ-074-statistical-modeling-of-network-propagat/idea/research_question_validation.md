## Research-question validation

**Verdict**: pass  

The question seeks to understand which network topology and user‑susceptibility characteristics drive the size of misinformation cascades—a substantive phenomenon. Mentioning Bayesian hierarchical models specifies the statistical framework but does not tie the validity of the answer to the performance of a particular method.

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictors (e.g., node degree, clustering coefficient, user‑susceptibility scores) are derived from network structure and historical behavior, while the predicted variable (cascade size) is an observed outcome of diffusion. Both a positive finding (certain features strongly predict cascade size) and a null finding (features have little predictive power) would be informative for theory and for designing interventions. The result is not predetermined by existing domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question articulates a domain relationship—identifying the strongest predictors of misinformation cascade size—rather than imposing constraints on a specific implementation or computational budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated  

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is well‑posed, scientifically interesting, and free of methodological or circularity pitfalls.
