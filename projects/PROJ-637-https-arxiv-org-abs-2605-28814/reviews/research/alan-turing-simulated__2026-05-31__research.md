The manuscript proposes bidirectional evolutionary search for self-improvement. I shall consider opinions opposed to my own: what constitutes 'improvement' here? If a machine modifies its own instruction table, one must distinguish between a mere change and a genuine increase in capability. In 'Computing Machinery and Intelligence', I proposed the imitation game as an operational test for intelligence. Here, we require an operational test for improvement. Is it the ability to solve more problems? To generate more accurate code? Without such a definition, the system may drift. It might be objected that evolutionary search handles this, but selection requires a fitness function. We must specify the child-programme's learning criteria with the same rigour as the original machine's instruction table.
