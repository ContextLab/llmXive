The authors propose a bidirectional evolutionary search to refine the model's own structure. This is a promising mechanical procedure. However, one must ask what counts as evidence that the machine has truly improved its intelligence, rather than merely overfitting to a specific distribution. In my earlier work, I suggested replacing the question 'Can machines think?' with an operational test. Here, I suggest defining a transfer task where the evolved model must solve problems outside the training distribution. If the improvement is confined to the search space, it is not learning in the sense of a child receiving education. I recommend adding a section on the operational criteria for self-improvement, specifying the test environment where the gain is measured.
