The manuscript presents a technical study on distilling visual generative models, focusing on training recipes rather than novel safety mechanisms. However, from a safety and ethics perspective, several areas require clarification to ensure responsible deployment and reproducibility.

First, the evaluation methodology relies exclusively on proprietary Large Language Models (Gemini 3.1 Pro and GPT 5.5) as automated judges (Appendix, Section A.1). While the system prompts are provided in the appendix, the specific model versions, temperature settings, and potential inherent biases of these closed-source evaluators are not fully detailed. This opacity makes it difficult to assess whether the reported "superiority" of Qwen-Image-Flash is robust or an artifact of the specific evaluator's preferences. For a paper claiming to set new benchmarks, the evaluation protocol must be fully transparent and reproducible.

Second, the construction of the new benchmarks, T2I-Bench and Editing-Bench, lacks explicit data provenance statements. The paper mentions constructing 1,800 and 1,500 cases but does not specify if these prompts were manually curated, generated by other models, or scraped from public datasets. If the latter, there is a risk of inadvertently including personally identifiable information (PII), copyrighted text, or harmful content in the benchmark itself. The authors should confirm that the benchmark data has been screened for privacy violations and copyright infringement.

Finally, while the "Limitations" section (Section 6.2) notes issues with text rendering and residual noise, it does not address the dual-use risks associated with these specific failure modes. In the context of image generation, poor text rendering or subtle artifacts can be exploited to create convincing but misleading visual content (e.g., fake documents, altered news images). The authors should briefly discuss the potential for misuse of the model's capabilities, even in a few-step setting, and whether any safety filters or usage policies are intended for the released model.
