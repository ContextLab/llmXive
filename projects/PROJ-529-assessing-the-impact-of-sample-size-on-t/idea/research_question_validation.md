## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks about a substantive statistical phenomenon: how the count of primary studies influences the stability of a meta‑analytic pooled effect size and the coverage of its confidence interval. It does not hinge on the performance of any particular computational method or software implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The predictor (the number of studies included) is a simple count derived from the meta‑analysis design, whereas the predicted variables (stability metrics and CI coverage) are computed from the effect‑size data themselves. These sources are independent, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

While theory suggests that larger numbers of studies improve reliability, the exact form of the relationship, the point of diminishing returns, and the empirical coverage behavior are not known. Demonstrating either a clear threshold or a surprising lack of improvement would both be publishable contributions.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The research question is framed as a domain‑level inquiry (“how does study count affect reliability?”) rather than a constraint on an algorithm, hardware, or runtime budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
