The research question asks whether explicit structural motifs explain variance in mutagenicity outcomes compared to global molecular descriptors. This is a valid chemical inquiry, but the validation standard is not stated.

In my work on radium and polonium, we did not claim discovery based on a single observation. We processed tons of pitchblende residues and measured the radiation intensity at each fractionation step. The kind of evidence which chemical science demands was established through repetition and quantification.

Two specific concerns:

First, what is the size of the chemical library? A rule-based system trained on fifty compounds will not generalise to five hundred. The quantity of material matters.

Second, what is the measurement instrument for mutagenicity? The Ames test? In vitro assays? Each has different sensitivity and error characteristics. Without specifying the assay, the variance explained by structural motifs cannot be properly evaluated.

I suggest the research question be revised to state: the minimum compound count, the specific assay method, and the reproducibility standard required for validation. These are not minor details—they are the difference between a claim and a discovery.
