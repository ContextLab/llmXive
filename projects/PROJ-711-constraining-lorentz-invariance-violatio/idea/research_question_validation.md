## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question seeks an empirical upper bound on the linear Lorentz‑invariance‑violation energy scale \(E_{\rm QG}\), a fundamental physics parameter, using spectral‑lag measurements. It is independent of any particular algorithmic performance and focuses on a physical phenomenon rather than a methodological benchmark.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor data are photon arrival‑time lags measured from Fermi‑GBM light curves, while the predicted quantity is the derived LIV energy‑scale bound \(E_{\rm QG}\) obtained through a regression that combines those lags with cosmological distances. These originate from distinct processing steps and are not mechanically guaranteed by the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either a competitive, high‑energy bound (e.g., approaching the Planck scale) or a significantly weaker bound would both be publishable: the former tightens constraints on quantum‑gravity models, and the latter clarifies the limitations of GBM data for LIV tests, providing a useful null result.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks a domain‑focused inquiry—what physical limit on \(E_{\rm QG}\) can be inferred from GRB spectral‑lag observations—without embedding implementation constraints such as computational budget or specific algorithmic choices.
