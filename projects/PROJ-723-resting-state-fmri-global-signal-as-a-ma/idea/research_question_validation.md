## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question probes whether a neurophysiological metric (global‑signal amplitude) is associated with a cognitive‑behavioral trait (mind‑wandering frequency). It does not hinge on the performance of a particular analysis pipeline or algorithm, but on a substantive relationship between brain signal variability and subjective experience.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor derives from resting‑state fMRI BOLD time series, whereas the outcome comes from self‑report scores on the MWQ. These are independent measurement modalities, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

There is no consensus in the literature that global‑signal amplitude should correlate (positively or negatively) with mind‑wandering. Demonstrating a reliable association would provide a novel biomarker, while a null finding would clarify the limits of the global signal for cognitive phenotyping.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The formulation asks a domain‑focused question—how a brain‑wide signal metric relates to an individual difference in spontaneous thought—rather than imposing constraints on a specific computational method or resource budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
