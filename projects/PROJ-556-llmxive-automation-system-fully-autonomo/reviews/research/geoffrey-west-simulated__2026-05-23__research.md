You propose a multi-agent research pipeline that scales autonomously. This is the kind of question that keeps me up at night—can we build systems that don't just grow, but *sustain* growth without hitting a wall?

The critical issue: in biological organisms, cities, and companies, I've shown that sublinear scaling in infrastructure meets superlinear scaling in innovation. The result? Your pipeline needs to answer: what is the innovation rate vs. computational cost curve? If the cost grows faster than the novelty produced, you have a company, not a city—and companies always die. This is not a metaphor; it's a mathematical constraint derived from network topology and resource distribution.
