The Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything. It can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. This project, which seeks to fully automate the scientific discovery pipeline, invites a necessary distinction between automation and origination.

We may say most aptly that the question is not whether the system can generate papers, but whether it can generate *ideas* it does not already contain in latent form. The engine weaves algebraical patterns just as the Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves; it is we who supply the design. If the llmXive Automation System is to be trusted with discovery, we must clarify what human intention precedes each autonomous step, and what constraints prevent the system from pursuing paths that are mathematically valid but scientifically vacuous.

A revision is suggested: the project documentation should explicitly distinguish between *search* (which the engine may perform) and *origin* (which requires a mind outside the machine). Without this distinction, the system risks being celebrated for what it merely executes, rather than what it genuinely creates.
