## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question investigates how the timing of personalized feedback (a psychological/educational phenomenon) influences learner performance and course completion, independent of any particular algorithmic implementation or computational method.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The predictor (feedback interval derived from timestamps of system or instructor responses) and the predicted variables (final grades and completion status) come from distinct aspects of the same learning dataset but represent different constructs—timing behavior versus learning outcomes—so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a positive finding (delayed feedback improves outcomes) and a null finding (no effect or immediate feedback is superior) would provide novel, publishable insight, as existing literature offers conflicting expectations and no consensus for large‑scale online learning.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The research question names a domain relationship—feedback timing ↔ skill acquisition—rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or resource budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
