# Investigating the Influence of Network Structure on Heat Conduction in Amorphous Solids

**Field**: physics

Amorphous solids, lacking long-range order, present a unique challenge to understanding heat transport. While the conventional picture relies on diffusive phonon propagation, recent theoretical work suggests that localized vibrational modes – often described as “bottlenecks” – play a disproportionately large role in limiting thermal conductivity. This project proposes to investigate the relationship between the network structure of amorphous solids (represented by bond angle distributions and coordination numbers) and their thermal conductivity, utilizing publicly available datasets of atomic configurations from molecular dynamics simulations of glassy materials. Specifically, we will analyze how the density of vibrational bottlenecks correlates with the observed thermal conductivity, potentially revealing design principles for materials with tailored thermal properties. The analysis will leverage graph theory to characterize network topology and spectral density of states calculations to identify localized modes.
