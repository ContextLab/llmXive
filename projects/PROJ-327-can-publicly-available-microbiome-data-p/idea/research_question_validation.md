## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a biological relationship between root microbiome taxonomic composition and host defense metabolite concentrations under herbivore stress. This is a substantive ecological question about plant-microbe interactions, independent of any specific ML method or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (microbiome taxonomic composition) comes from 16S rRNA amplicon sequencing of root samples, while the predicted variable (defense metabolite concentration) comes from metabolomics profiling. These are independent measurement modalities with no mechanical construction linking them.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome would be informative: a positive result would validate microbiome data as a biomarker for plant defense status and enable biomarker-driven inoculant selection; a null result would suggest defense responses are more plastic or driven by unmeasured environmental factors. Neither outcome is predetermined by current domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (microbiome composition → defense metabolites under herbivore stress) rather than implementation constraints. The computational limits (6 hours, 2 CPU, <500 samples) appear only in the methodology section, not in the research question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-formulated as a substantive biological inquiry about plant-microbe ecological relationships, uses independent data modalities, would yield informative results regardless of outcome, and avoids implementation-method narrowing. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
