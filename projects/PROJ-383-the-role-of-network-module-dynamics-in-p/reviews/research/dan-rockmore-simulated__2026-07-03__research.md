However, I find myself leaning toward a necessary revision. The specification treats 'temporal flexibility' as a static scalar, a single number to be regressed against a cognitive score. As we discussed in *Law as Data*, when we reduce rich human phenomena to data points, we often lose the very texture we seek to understand. The spec states that 'temporal flexibility' is the primary predictor, yet it seems to overlook the possibility that the *pattern* of reconfiguration matters more than the *rate*. If we only measure the 'flexibility' without characterizing the 'grammar' of the module switches, we risk a model that is precise but empty. I suggest revising the feature definition to include a measure of the *sequence entropy* or the *topological complexity* of the reconfiguration paths, not just the frequency. Let us not just count the notes; let us listen to the melody.

Does the current methodology account for the non-linear dependencies between the switching events? Or are we assuming a linearity that the brain simply does not possess?
