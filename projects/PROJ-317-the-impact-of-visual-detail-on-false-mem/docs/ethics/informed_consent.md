## Introduction
You are invited to participate in a research study. This form explains the purpose of the study, what you will be asked to do, and any potential risks or benefits. Please read this form carefully and ask any questions you may have before deciding whether or not to participate.

## Purpose of the Study
The purpose of this research is to investigate how the level of visual detail in images affects a person's susceptibility to forming false memories. Specifically, we want to understand if images with more detail lead to higher or lower rates of false memory recognition.

## Procedures
If you agree to participate, you will be asked to:
1. View a series of images presented on a computer screen.
2. Complete a short distractor task (e.g., mental arithmetic) between image presentations.
3. Answer a series of recognition questions about the images, including questions about details that were present (true details) and details that were not present (false/lure details).
4. Provide demographic information (optional).

The entire session will take approximately [15-20] minutes.

## Potential Risks
- **Psychological Discomfort**: The study involves inducing false memories for research purposes. You may feel confused or surprised when you realize some details you "remember" were not actually present. This is a normal part of the phenomenon being studied and is not harmful.
- **Boredom or Fatigue**: The distractor task and recognition questions may be repetitive.

If you experience any distress, you may stop the study at any time.

## Potential Benefits
- **To Society**: This research may contribute to a better understanding of memory processes, which could have implications for eyewitness testimony, therapeutic practices, and educational strategies.
- **To You**: You may gain insight into how your own memory works. There is no direct benefit to you from participating.
