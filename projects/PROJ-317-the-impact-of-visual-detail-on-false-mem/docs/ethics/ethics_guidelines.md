# Ethics Guidelines for Visual Detail and False Memory Research

## Overview

This document outlines the ethical considerations and compliance requirements for the "Impact of Visual Detail on False Memory Susceptibility" research project (PROJ-317).

## Principles

### 1. Informed Consent

All participants must provide informed consent before participating in any study. The consent form must clearly explain:
- The purpose of the research
- What participation involves
- Potential risks and benefits
- The right to withdraw at any time
- How data will be used and stored

**Template**: See `data/ethics/informed_consent.md` for the standardized consent form.

### 2. Privacy and Data Protection (GDPR Compliance)

- **Data Minimization**: Collect only the data necessary for the research objectives.
- **Anonymization**: Remove all personally identifiable information (PII) from response data.
- **Secure Storage**: Store data in encrypted formats with access controls.
- **Retention Policy**: Delete data after the research project is completed or as required by institutional policy.
- **Participant Rights**: Participants have the right to access, correct, and delete their data.

### 3. Risk Management

- **Psychological Risk**: False memory research may cause mild distress. Participants must be debriefed afterward to clarify that the study involved simulated scenarios.
- **Mitigation**: Provide clear instructions, allow breaks, and offer a debriefing session.
- **Exclusion Criteria**: Exclude participants with a history of severe anxiety or trauma if deemed necessary by the IRB.

### 4. Debriefing

All participants must receive a debriefing explaining:
- The true purpose of the study
- Why certain procedures were used (e.g., distractor tasks)
- That false memories were intentionally induced for research purposes
- Resources for further questions or concerns

### 5. IRB Approval

Before any data collection involving human subjects:
- Submit the research protocol to the Institutional Review Board (IRB).
- Obtain written approval.
- Adhere to all conditions specified by the IRB.
- Report any adverse events immediately.

## Current Project Status

- **Simulated Participants**: This project currently uses simulated (synthetic) participant data. No human subjects are involved at this stage.
- **Future Human Studies**: If the project transitions to human subject research, a full IRB application must be submitted using the templates in `data/ethics/`.
