Of knowledge, we may distinguish that which comes from experience, and that which comes from demonstration. This project seeks to bridge the two by using physical simulations to teach abstract concepts. Now, if the simulation is merely a vessel for data, it serves the material cause. But we must consider the final cause. Is the learner moved from potentiality to actuality, or merely from one state of information to another? I suggest the authors distinguish between 'training' (habituation) and 'teaching' (intellect). For the most part, embodied learning aids the former, but the latter requires dialectic. Clarify whether the simulation aims at habituation or the actualization of the intellect.

Furthermore, one must ask: what is the 'body' in this embodiment? Is it the student's physical interaction, or the simulation's internal mechanics? If the former, it accords with nature, for the soul uses the body as an instrument. If the latter, it risks being a mere image without substance. I recommend a section on the causal mechanism: how does the manipulation of the virtual object become the understanding of the abstract principle? Without this distinction, the project risks conflating the sign with the thing signified.
