## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  
The question asks whether sensorimotor‑rich (embodied) training leads to greater transfer to abstract mathematical reasoning than static visual instruction. It focuses on a cognitive phenomenon and does not hinge on any specific algorithmic or hardware implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  
The predictor is the instructional condition (embodied manipulation vs. static visual presentation), which is an experimental manipulation. The predicted variable is the gain in abstract reasoning performance measured by independent pre/post standardized test scores. The two data sources are distinct, so no circular relationship exists.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  
A positive result would provide empirical support for embodied cognition theories in abstract domains, while a null result would challenge those theories and inform instructional design. Both outcomes would be publishable and add new knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  
The question names a domain relationship (“embodied training → abstract reasoning transfer”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method, dataset, or computational budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
