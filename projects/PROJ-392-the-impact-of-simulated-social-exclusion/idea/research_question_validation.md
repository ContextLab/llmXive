## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates a causal relationship between a social manipulation and neural reward processing, focusing on the biological mechanism rather than a specific analytical technique. It remains independent of the specific fMRI preprocessing pipeline or statistical tools used to measure the effect.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor is a behavioral manipulation (inclusion vs. exclusion conditions in a game), while the predicted variable is hemodynamic activity measured during a separate, subsequent reward task. These are distinct signals captured at different time points, avoiding mechanical construction where the output is guaranteed by the input definition.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both positive and null findings are scientifically valuable; a positive result clarifies the link between social threat and reward sensitivity, while a null result would challenge theories of generalized reward dampening following exclusion. Neither outcome is predetermined by current domain knowledge, as the literature gap indicates uncertainty regarding downstream effects in adults.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a specific domain relationship (social exclusion modulating reward circuitry) rather than a constraint on data collection or analysis pipelines. It asks what happens in the brain under specific psychological conditions, not whether a specific method can succeed within a budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass as the core inquiry targets a genuine neural mechanism without implementation bias, circular logic, or triviality. The research question is substantive and ready to proceed to project initialization.
