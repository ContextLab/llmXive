## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether elemental composition ratios in the solar wind (a physical property) provide additional explanatory power for geomagnetic storm intensity, a natural‐world relationship. It does not hinge on any particular algorithm, hardware, or resource constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor variables (O/Fe, He/H ion ratios derived from ACE/WIND SWICS ion flux measurements) are obtained from compositional spectroscopy, while the predicted variables (Dst, Kp indices) are ground‑based geomagnetic observations. These data streams are independent, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a positive finding (composition adds predictive value) and a null finding (composition adds no value) would be scientifically informative: the former would suggest new coupling mechanisms, the latter would constrain theory and forecasting practice. Neither outcome is predetermined by existing knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question frames a domain‑level inquiry—how a specific solar‑wind property relates to geomagnetic response—rather than an implementation constraint on a particular method or computational budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
