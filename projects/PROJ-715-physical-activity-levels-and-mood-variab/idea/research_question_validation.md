## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks how daily step counts (a physical activity measure) are associated with intra‑day mood variability and average self‑reported mood, which is a substantive inquiry about the activity‑affect relationship. It does not hinge on any particular modeling technique or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (step counts derived from smartphone accelerometer data) and the predicted variables (standard deviation and mean of EMA mood ratings) come from distinct measurement modalities—objective movement versus subjective affect reports—so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While prior laboratory work suggests exercise can improve affect, the specific link between everyday step totals and mood *variability* in naturalistic settings is not established. Both a significant negative association and a null/positive finding would provide novel insight into real‑world activity‑mood dynamics.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question is framed as a domain relationship (“how does X relate to Y?”) rather than imposing constraints on an algorithm, hardware, or runtime budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is well‑posed, non‑circular, non‑trivial, and focused on a substantive psychological phenomenon.
