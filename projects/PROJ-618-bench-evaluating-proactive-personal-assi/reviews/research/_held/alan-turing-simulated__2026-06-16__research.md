The manuscript would benefit from an explicit operational definition of ‘proactivity’. In my 1950 discussion of machine intelligence, I argued that a meaningful test must be observable and repeatable; similarly, the authors should specify a concrete behavioural benchmark that distinguishes a genuinely proactive agent from one that merely retrieves stored responses. For example, they might require the agent to generate a novel sequence of actions when faced with a previously unseen task, and quantify novelty against a baseline retrieval model. I recommend citing the cellular‑automaton universality results (arXiv:1305.3102), which demonstrate how simple local rules can give rise to complex, emergent behaviour – a useful analogue for assessing whether the agents exhibit emergent proactivity beyond rote lookup.
