**Verdict**: pass

The predictor involves latent vectors derived from the "conscious" linguistic scaffolding within the Orca model, while the predicted variable is the logical outcome of a physical counterfactual scenario (e.g., "falls" vs. "floats"). ### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would provide rare empirical evidence that world models encode invariant causal laws accessible via simple symbolic operations, supporting the "System-2" reasoning hypothesis. ### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a specific domain relationship: the link between linguistic scaffolding in latent space and the validity of physical counterfactual reasoning. It does not reduce to an implementation constraint like "Can we run this on a CPU in 6 hours?" but rather uses the CPU constraint as a condition to test the *efficiency* and *accessibility* of the causal signal, which remains a valid scientific inquiry.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass, confirming the research question targets a genuine scientific gap regarding the causal integrity of world model latent spaces.
