The question asks about the causal relationship between static network topology and dynamic energy transfer efficiency in physical spin systems. It is framed as an inquiry into domain phenomena rather than the performance of a specific simulation algorithm or hardware constraint.

The predictor (network topology metrics like degree distribution) is derived from the static graph structure, while the predicted variable (energy diffusion rate) is derived from the dynamic spin-flip simulation outcomes. Both outcomes are scientifically informative given the stated gap in systematic evidence linking topology to diffusion rates.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (topology influencing energy transfer) rather than focusing on implementation constraints like budget, runtime, or specific software libraries. The methodology constraints are present in the plan but do not contaminate the core research question itself.
