## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the relationship between a psychological state (social exclusion) and a neural mechanism (DMN connectivity), independent of any specific analysis pipeline or algorithm. It seeks to characterize a biological phenomenon rather than evaluate a computational tool or benchmark a specific model's performance.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The independent variable (task condition: inclusion vs. exclusion) is determined by the experimental protocol (Cyberball ball-throwing rules), not by the fMRI signal. The dependent variable (connectivity) is derived from BOLD time-series recorded during the task. These sources are distinct, preventing mechanical guarantees that the result must follow from the construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A finding of reduced connectivity would clarify how social threat disrupts self-referential processing, while a null result would suggest DMN stability or implicate other networks. Both outcomes would contribute to understanding the state-level neural signatures of rejection beyond established activation patterns, making the question scientifically informative regardless of direction.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names the domain relationship (exclusion modulating connectivity) rather than imposing constraints on the analysis method or hardware resources. It focuses on the "how" of the neural mechanism, not the "can we compute it" aspect, keeping the scope on the scientific phenomenon.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass; the research question targets a substantive scientific relationship with independent variables and non-trivial potential outcomes. The proposed scope is appropriate for characterizing the phenomenon without being constrained by implementation details.
