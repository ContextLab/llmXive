## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological mechanism (the impact of feedback dynamics on self-perception) rather than the performance of a specific algorithm or model. It frames the inquiry around the relationship between environmental signals and internal states, which is independent of the specific NLP tools used to measure them.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (social feedback valence) is derived from community replies, while the predicted variable (self-esteem indicators) is derived from the user's own posts. These represent distinct actors and interaction directions within the data, meaning the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by shared construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would establish the specific harm of feedback volatility beyond simple valence, while a null result would challenge the assumption that temporal dynamics drive instability. Either outcome provides new empirical evidence regarding the design of digital social environments.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (feedback dynamics affecting self-esteem) rather than a constraint on implementation (e.g., model speed or architecture). It focuses on the "how" of the psychological phenomenon rather than the "can" of a technical solution.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

The research question is well-formed as a substantive inquiry into psychological dynamics within online environments. It avoids implementation-narrowing and circularity pitfalls, and the proposed investigation would yield informative results regardless of the statistical outcome.
