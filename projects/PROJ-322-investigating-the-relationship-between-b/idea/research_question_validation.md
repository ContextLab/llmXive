## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates the biological link between neural network topology changes and clinical symptom resolution, rather than evaluating a specific algorithm's performance. The graph metrics serve as measurement tools for the underlying phenomenon of network reconfiguration, not as the object of study itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (network metrics derived from resting-state fMRI BOLD signals) and the predicted variable (cognitive scores from clinical assessments like the Post-Concussion Symptom Scale) originate from independent data modalities. There is no mechanical construction guaranteeing their correlation, as neuroimaging data and behavioral survey data are collected separately.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would validate functional reconfiguration as a prognostic biomarker for mTBI recovery, while a null result would indicate that network plasticity is dissociated from behavioral improvement. Both outcomes provide actionable insight for clinical management and theoretical models of recovery, given the current heterogeneity in mTBI literature.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (network reconfiguration versus cognitive recovery) rather than imposing constraints on computational resources or specific software implementations. The focus remains on the scientific relationship between the variables, leaving the methodological choice of graph metrics as a means to an end.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass, confirming the research question targets a substantive scientific relationship with independent variables and meaningful potential outcomes. The framing is appropriate for advancing to project initialization without requiring reframing.
