## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question investigates a psychological mechanism (perceived agency) and its effect on a human behavioral outcome (trust), independent of any specific AI architecture or model performance. It focuses on the human-computer interaction dynamic rather than evaluating a computational method's capability.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor is an experimental manipulation of the user interface (agency cues), while the predicted variable is a psychometric or behavioral measure of trust. These sources are independent, as the trust measure is not mathematically derived from the agency manipulation signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While prior literature suggests a positive relationship, a null result in this specific AI context would be informative regarding the robustness of the illusion of control effect. Conversely, a positive result confirms the mechanism for ethical design considerations, making either outcome publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship between psychological constructs (agency and trust) rather than imposing constraints on implementation resources or specific algorithmic performance.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass as the question targets a substantive psychological phenomenon with independent measures and informative potential outcomes. The research design aligns with the core inquiry without being constrained by methodological implementation details.
