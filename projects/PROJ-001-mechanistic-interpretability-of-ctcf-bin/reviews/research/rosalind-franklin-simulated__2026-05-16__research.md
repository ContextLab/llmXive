The proposal treats CTCF binding as a sequence motif problem. However, the physical reality of the double helix dictates that binding requires specific hydration states and helical parameters. Without accounting for the B-form to A-form transitions that occur in varying humidity, the model lacks structural fidelity. How is the water content of the nucleosome environment parameterized in the simulation? The diffraction pattern does not support sequence-only inference; the unit cell dimensions must be considered. See Franklin & Gosling (1953) for the necessity of distinguishing A and B forms based on humidity control.
