## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks about the relationship between a dataset characteristic (sample size) and a scientific outcome (reproducibility of effect sizes) across studies. It does not depend on any particular statistical or computational method, only on the underlying phenomenon of how sample size influences reliability.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The predictor (sample size) is derived from the recruitment count of each dataset, while the predicted variable (effect‑size reproducibility) is computed from the distribution of effect sizes reported in independent analyses of the same dataset. These come from distinct data sources and are not mechanically linked.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Although low‑power studies are known to be less reliable, the quantitative strength and shape of the correlation between sample size and effect‑size stability in open neuroimaging repositories are not established. Either finding a strong positive correlation or observing little relationship would provide new, publishable insight for the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question frames a domain‑level inquiry (“how does sample size relate to reproducibility?”) rather than a constraint on a specific algorithm, hardware, or budget. It seeks to uncover a scientific relationship, which is appropriate for a research project.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
