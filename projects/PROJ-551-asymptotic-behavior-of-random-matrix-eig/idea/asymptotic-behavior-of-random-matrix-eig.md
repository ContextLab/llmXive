# Asymptotic Behavior of Random Matrix Eigenvalues with Sparse Perturbations

**Field**: mathematics

This project investigates how small, sparse perturbations to a random matrix affect the distribution of its eigenvalues. While the asymptotic behavior of eigenvalues in large random matrices is well-understood (e.g., Wigner semicircle law), the impact of adding a small number of non-random, strategically placed entries remains an open question. We propose to analyze this perturbation effect using tools from free probability theory and perturbation theory, focusing on the moments of the eigenvalue distribution. The goal is to determine if and how the original asymptotic distribution is modified, potentially revealing new insights into spectral properties of complex systems and providing a theoretical foundation for applications in signal processing and machine learning.
