## Summary

This project implements a comparative analysis of the MIGA generative pipeline under three conditions: (A) Full self-reflection baseline, (B) Naive baseline (no self-reflection), and (C) Deterministic flow-based correction applied to the naive baseline. The primary technical approach involves executing the MIGA pipeline on a sampled subset of the NarrLV/VBench datasets, computing optical flow fields using the RAFT-Small model in FP16 precision on CPU, applying non-differentiable warping/smoothing (temporal back-projection), and evaluating the results using VBench and FVD metrics. The plan strictly adheres to CPU-only constraints (cores, 7GB RAM) and ensures statistical rigor via normality-aware significance testing, power analysis based on a pilot study, and explicit acknowledgment of the 2D/3D proxy limitations.

## Complexity Tracking

| Violation | Why Needed | Simpler Alternative Rejected Because |
|-----------|------------|-------------------------------------|
| **Three distinct execution modes** | Required to isolate the effect of self-reflection (A), the baseline (B), and the flow correction (C) for valid statistical comparison (FR-002, FR-004). | A single mode would prevent the pairwise comparisons (A vs C, B vs C) required by US-3 and SC-001. |
| **Statistical Normality Check** | FR-006 mandates adaptive testing (t-test vs Wilcoxon) to ensure validity of p-values. | Hardcoding a t-test risks Type I/II errors if the metric distribution is non-normal, violating scientific rigor. |
| **RAFT-Small (FP16)** | Required to meet the "CPU-tractable" constraint while maintaining sufficient accuracy for optical flow (US-2). | Full RAFT or GPU-based models would exceed RAM limits or require unavailable hardware, causing CI failure. |
| **Pilot Study for Power Analysis** | Video metrics have high variance; a pilot (N=5) is needed to estimate empirical variance before finalizing N=50. | Assuming generic variance risks Type II error or wasted runtime on an underpowered study. |
