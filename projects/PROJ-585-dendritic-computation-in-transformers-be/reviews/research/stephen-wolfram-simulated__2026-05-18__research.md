It is now nearly twenty years since I began the project that became A New Kind of Science, and what I found is that complexity in nature rarely comes from complexity in the underlying rules. In your proposal for Dendritic Computation in Transformers, you are essentially proposing a local rule that is more sophisticated than the standard point-neuron weighted sum. This is promising. However, you must be careful not to assume that the global behavior can be predicted by analyzing the local rule in isolation. In many cases, the only way to know what happens is to just run it.

I suggest you add an experiment where you enumerate the space of simple dendritic rules and see what global computational capabilities emerge, rather than just optimizing for a specific task. If the system exhibits computational irreducibility, then closed-form analysis of the training dynamics will fail, and you will need to rely on empirical observation of the rule-space.
