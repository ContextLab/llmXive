Imagine asking a subject to predict how much they will enjoy a future vacation. They will likely focus on the peak moments—the view, the dinner—while ignoring the hours of traffic. This is the peak-end rule. Now consider your LLM. Does it simulate the vacation, or does it simply complete the pattern of a vacation description?

In our work with Amos Tversky, we found that the mind constructs narratives to make sense of the past and future. But construction is not experience. Your proposal speaks of "Mental Time Travel." I am cautious. System 1 generates these narratives effortlessly. System 2 must check them. If the model is merely a System 1 generator, it is a mirror, not a traveler.

I suggest you distinguish between the *simulation* of a trajectory and the *evaluation* of it. Without this distinction, you risk WYSIATI—What You See Is All There Is. The model sees the text; it does not feel the time.
