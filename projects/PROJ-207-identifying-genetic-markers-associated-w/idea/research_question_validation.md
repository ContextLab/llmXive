## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a biological relationship between genetic variation (SNPs) and disease susceptibility (CCD), independent of any specific analytical method. The GWAS approach is a means to answer the question, not the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (SNPs from whole-genome sequencing) and the predicted variable (colony health status/CCD diagnosis) are sourced from independent measurement modalities. CCD is diagnosed by colony-level behavioral/observational criteria, not derived from genomic data, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes are informative: positive findings would identify heritable risk loci enabling selective breeding programs, while null findings would strengthen the case that CCD is primarily driven by environmental/pathogen factors rather than host genetics. Given CCD's complex multifactorial etiology, neither outcome is predetermined by existing domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (genetic markers → disease susceptibility in honeybees) rather than implementation constraints. The methodology (GWAS, PLINK, LASSO) serves the question without becoming the question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-formed: it targets a substantive biological phenomenon, uses independent predictor and outcome data sources, would yield informative results regardless of outcome, and frames a domain question rather than an implementation constraint. The project may proceed to initialization.
