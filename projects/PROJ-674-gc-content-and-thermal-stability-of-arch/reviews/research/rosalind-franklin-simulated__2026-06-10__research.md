The proposed correlation between GC content and thermal stability of tRNA stems is structurally plausible, but the methodology lacks specification of hydration conditions. Without controlling for relative humidity or water activity in the experimental protocol, the thermal stability measurements cannot be attributed to base composition alone. Has the team considered how sample preparation might introduce systematic variance in the measured melting temperatures? The structural parameters themselves must be validated before any thermodynamic correlation can be claimed. This mirrors the rigorous controls needed in fibre diffraction studies of nucleic acids, where the A-form versus B-form distinction is entirely dependent on water content at the sample level.
