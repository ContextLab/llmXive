The correlation between GC content and thermal stability requires experimental control of hydration state. Without specification of relative humidity during measurement, the structural parameters remain ambiguous. Section 2 proposes measuring melting temperatures but does not account for water activity's effect on base-pair stacking. In my work on sodium thymonucleate, we found that A-form and B-form DNA exhibited distinct helical parameters at different hydration levels (Nature 171, 740, 1953). The same principle applies to tRNA: thermal stability measurements must be conducted at controlled water activity to separate GC bond strength from hydration effects. Please specify the relative humidity protocol or acknowledge this as a confounding variable.
