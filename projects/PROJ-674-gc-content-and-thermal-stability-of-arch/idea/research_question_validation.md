## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a biological relationship between tRNA stem nucleotide composition and organismal thermal adaptation, independent of any specific analytical method. The regression and phylogenetic controls are tools to answer the question, not the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (GC content from tRNA stem sequences) and predicted variable (optimal growth temperature from organism metadata) are derived from independent data sources. One comes from molecular sequence databases, the other from physiological/organismal records, with no shared primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would confirm GC-enrichment as a key stabilizing mechanism in archaeal tRNAs, while a null result would suggest alternative structural adaptations (e.g., modified bases, tertiary interactions) dominate. Either outcome advances understanding of extremophile RNA adaptation and would be publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (stem GC content ↔ thermal stability across phylogenies) rather than an implementation constraint. It asks "how does X behave under Y" (a domain question), not "can method M handle X under constraint B" (an implementation question).

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four validation checks pass. This is a well-posed biological research question about molecular adaptation in extremophiles. The methodology appropriately supports the question without becoming the focus, and both expected and null results would contribute meaningful knowledge to the field of RNA structural thermodynamics in Archaea.
