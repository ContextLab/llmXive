## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the comparative efficacy of two clinical interventions (VRET vs. in-person exposure) on a psychological outcome (anxiety symptom severity). It is independent of any specific data analysis method or software implementation; the meta-analytic approach is the correct tool for synthesizing evidence, not the phenomenon being studied.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variable is the treatment modality (VR vs. in-person) assigned in randomized controlled trials, while the outcome is the change in anxiety scores derived from validated psychometric scales. These are independent measurements where the treatment assignment is not mathematically derived from the outcome scores, ensuring no mechanical construction of the relationship.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While it is plausible that VRET is effective, the specific question of whether it is *comparable* (non-inferior) to the gold standard across diverse subtypes and doses is not predetermined by current domain knowledge. A finding of non-inferiority would validate a scalable treatment alternative, whereas a finding of significant inferiority (or superiority) would fundamentally alter clinical guidelines; both outcomes are highly informative for healthcare policy.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a domain relationship (comparative clinical efficacy) and investigates moderators (subtype, dose, guidance) that explain variance in that relationship. It does not frame the inquiry around computational constraints, specific software libraries, or implementation budgets.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-structured, scientifically substantive, and free from circularity or implementation-method narrowing. It targets a genuine gap in the literature regarding the comparative efficacy of VRET versus traditional therapy, making it suitable for immediate project initialization.
