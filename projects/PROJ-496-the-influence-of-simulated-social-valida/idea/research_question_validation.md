## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological/neural phenomenon—how the brain responds to different sources of social validation (simulated vs. real-world) and how this varies with social anxiety. It is independent of any specific method's performance; the neural measurement (P300) is an outcome variable, not a methodological constraint being evaluated.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (validation type: simulated text-based feedback vs. real-world interpersonal feedback) is an experimental manipulation, while the predicted variable (P300 amplitude) is measured from EEG recordings. These are independent data sources—the manipulation is not derived from the same signal as the neural measurement.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes would be informative: finding reduced neural responses to simulated validation would suggest digital platforms may not fully satisfy social needs, while comparable responses would indicate digital interactions carry equivalent neural weight. Either result advances understanding of how the brain processes online versus offline social cues.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (type of social validation → neural response, moderated by social anxiety) rather than an implementation constraint. The question is about what the brain does, not whether a particular analysis pipeline or hardware setup can handle a task.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
