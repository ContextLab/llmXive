## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about whether a fundamental statistical-mechanical principle (equipartition theorem) applies to a specific physical system (driven granular media), independent of any particular measurement or computational method. The core inquiry is about the physical behavior of granular systems far from equilibrium, not about whether a specific algorithm or dataset works.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variables (driving frequency, amplitude, particle material properties) are external input parameters controlled by the experimenter. The predicted variable (energy distribution ratios among degrees of freedom) is measured from particle kinematics. These are independent signals: driving conditions cause the system behavior rather than being derived from it.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome is scientifically informative. A positive result (equipartition holds in certain regimes) would establish quasi-thermal behavior windows for granular matter, supporting thermodynamic analogies. A null result (systematic violations) would demonstrate fundamental limitations of equilibrium statistical mechanics for non-ergodic dissipative systems, which is a central open question in granular physics.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (validity of equipartition in driven granular media) rather than implementation constraints. While methodology details (frequency bins, statistical tests) appear in the fleshed-out sections, the research question itself asks "does X hold in Y" rather than "can method M compute X within budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question poses a substantive scientific inquiry about the limits of equilibrium statistical mechanics in non-equilibrium granular systems, with independent predictors and meaningful outcomes regardless of whether equipartition holds or fails. The question is ready to advance to project initialization.
