# Comparative Analysis of Molecular Fingerprints for Pesticide Toxicity Prediction

**Field**: chemistry

## Research question

How do structural representations of organophosphate pesticides (circular Morgan fingerprints vs. fragment-based MACCS keys) influence the detection of toxicophore-activity relationships in predictive toxicity models?

## Motivation

Identifying which molecular encoding best captures the structural determinants of pesticide toxicity is critical for accelerating the design of safer agrochemicals and reducing reliance on animal testing in regulatory screening. Current models often assume one fingerprint type is universally superior, but this may not hold for specific chemical classes where discrete functional groups (captured by MACCS) versus broader topological environments (captured by Morgan) drive toxicity mechanisms differently.

## Related work

- [ADMET property prediction through combinations of molecular fingerprints (2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00174) — Establishes that random forests with extended-connectivity fingerprints (ECFP/Morgan) consistently outperform other methods for small molecule potency prediction, providing a methodological precedent for fingerprint comparison.
- [Graph-based Molecular In-context Learning Grounded on Morgan Fingerprints (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.05414) — Demonstrates Morgan fingerprints as effective embeddings for molecular property prediction tasks, validating their utility in toxicity-relevant contexts.
- [ToxicBlend: Virtual Screening of Toxic Compounds with Ensemble Predictors (2018)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1806.04449) — Addresses compound toxicity assessment challenges in pharmaceutical screening, though focused on ensemble methods rather than fingerprint comparison specifically.
- [Pesti-Gen: Unleashing a Generative Molecule Approach for Toxicity Aware Pesticide Design (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.14469) — Contextualizes pesticide toxicity as a critical constraint in agrochemical design, though does not address fingerprint-level structural representation choices.

## Expected results

We expect Morgan fingerprints to achieve higher ROC-AUC (≥0.75) than MACCS keys (≤0.70) for organophosphate toxicity prediction, reflecting their superior capture of topological toxicophore environments. This would be confirmed if cross-validated performance differences are statistically significant (paired t-test, p<0.05) across ≥3 toxicity endpoints from Tox21.

## Methodology sketch

- Download Tox21 dataset (https://tripod.nih.gov/tox21/) containing ~10,000 compounds with toxicity labels for 12 endpoints
- Filter to organophosphate pesticides using PubChem CID lookup and chemical structure matching (SMILES pattern: `OP(=O)(O)`)
- Generate Morgan fingerprints (radius=2, 1024 bits) and MACCS keys (166 bits) using RDKit (CPU-only, <2GB RAM)
- Split data 80/20 stratified by toxicity label; ensure no structural analogs in both train/test sets (Tanimoto similarity <0.85)
- Train Random Forest classifier (100 trees, max_depth=10) on each fingerprint type separately
- Evaluate using ROC-AUC, precision-recall AUC, and balanced accuracy on held-out test set
- Perform paired t-test comparing model performance across 5-fold cross-validation splits
- Analyze feature importance to identify which structural features (bits) contribute most to toxicity predictions
- All steps executable within 6-hour GHA job using scikit-learn, RDKit, and pandas (no GPU required)
