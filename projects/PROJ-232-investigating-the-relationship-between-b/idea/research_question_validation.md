## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a substantive brain-behavior relationship (whether resting-state connectivity predicts musical emotion perception differences), independent of any specific ML method's performance. The methodology (fMRI preprocessing, connectivity metrics, regression) are tools to answer the question, not the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (resting-state functional connectivity from fMRI) and predicted variable (self-reported emotional responses from behavioral questionnaires like the Barcelona Music Reward Questionnaire) are derived from independent measurement modalities. One is neural activity during rest, the other is subjective behavioral ratings collected separately.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive correlation would suggest intrinsic brain architecture predisposes individuals to musical emotional reactivity, advancing neural mechanisms of music perception. A null result would be equally informative, suggesting task-evoked connectivity or other factors better explain musical emotion perception. Either outcome advances theory.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (resting-state connectivity → musical emotion perception individual differences) rather than implementation constraints. No specific architecture, budget, or performance metric is the focus of the question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question poses a genuine scientific inquiry about brain-behavior relationships that is methodologically independent, uses non-circular data sources, and would yield informative results regardless of outcome direction. The project can proceed to initialization.
