## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a physical relationship between cosmic ray composition and solar activity cycles, independent of any specific analytical method. The heliospheric transport mechanisms mentioned are the phenomenon of interest, not the methodology used to study them.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (solar activity indices from sunspot numbers and solar wind parameters via NOAA/SWPC) and the predicted variable (cosmic ray composition ratios from AMS-02 detector data) are sourced from independent measurement modalities. One measures solar output directly, the other measures galactic particles that have traversed the heliosphere.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (species-dependent modulation) would provide empirical constraints on rigidity-dependent diffusion coefficients in transport models; a null result (uniform modulation across species) would similarly constrain drift-based models. Both outcomes advance understanding of heliospheric physics beyond total intensity measurements.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (cosmic ray composition variation across solar cycles) rather than implementation constraints. It asks "how does X behave under Y" rather than "can method M handle X under Y inside budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. This is a legitimate physics research question about heliospheric transport mechanisms, with independent predictor and outcome variables, and either positive or null results would be informative for the field. The question is appropriately framed around the phenomenon of interest rather than methodological performance.
