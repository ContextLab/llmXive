## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological mechanism (how social rejection modulates reward sensitivity), independent of any specific ML method or algorithm. The Cyberball paradigm is mentioned as the rejection induction method, but the core question is about the phenomenon relationship, not whether the paradigm or measurement approach performs adequately.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (social rejection induced via Cyberball exclusion task) and predicted variable (behavioral responses to positive feedback task) are independent measurement modalities. The rejection manipulation occurs in a separate task phase from the reward feedback task, and the behavioral outcomes (reaction times, self-reported satisfaction) are not derived from the same signal as the rejection manipulation.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes would be informative. A positive result (rejection dampens reward processing) would support social pain overlap theories and inform depression/social anxiety interventions. A null result would challenge existing literature suggesting reward processing is resilient to social stress. While the direction is somewhat theorized, the specific behavioral manifestations and effect sizes remain empirically uncertain.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a clear domain relationship (social rejection → reward sensitivity modulation) rather than implementation constraints. The question is about how a psychological variable behaves under another condition, not whether method M can handle X under budget B.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. Minor note: the project title mentions "Neural Responses" but the methodology explicitly uses behavioral proxies only (as acknowledged in the limitations). This is a title-consistency issue rather than a research-question flaw, but should be aligned in the final deliverable for clarity.
