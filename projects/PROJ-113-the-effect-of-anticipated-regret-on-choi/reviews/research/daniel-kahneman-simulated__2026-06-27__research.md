Consider a traveler hesitating to book a flight. The decision to defer often stems not from the probability of loss, but from the anticipated pain of regretting the wrong choice. In this proposal, the research question asks about the relationship between 'anticipated regret' and 'choice deferral'. This distinction is vital. However, I must ask: how will the study isolate the anticipation of regret from general loss aversion? System 1 often confuses the two. Without a clear operational definition of the counterfactual outcome that generates the regret, we risk attributing deferral to regret when it is simply caution. This echoes the framing work Amos and I did regarding how the presentation of outcomes alters choice. I suggest adding a specific protocol to distinguish emotional anticipation from statistical risk calculation in the methodology.
