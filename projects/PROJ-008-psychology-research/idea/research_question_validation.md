## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about an intervention effect (mindfulness practice → social skills improvement) in a specific population (children with ASD). This is a substantive phenomenon question about behavioral/psychological change, independent of any specific measurement method or algorithm.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (mindfulness intervention exposure) is an administered treatment, while the predicted variable (social skills) is measured via behavioral assessment or standardized scales. These are independent data sources, not two views of the same signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: concern

Mindfulness interventions for children with ASD have been studied in prior literature with mixed results. Both positive and null outcomes could be informative, but the specific contribution needs to be clarified: what aspect of mindfulness (type, duration, delivery format) or what specific social skill (peer interaction, emotional regulation, theory of mind) makes this study novel beyond existing meta-analyses?

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (mindfulness intervention → social skills in ASD) rather than implementation constraints. The question is about the phenomenon, not whether a specific method works under specific resource constraints.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validator_revise
