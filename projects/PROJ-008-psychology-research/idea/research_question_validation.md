## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the causal relationship between specific psychological intervention components (breath awareness, body scan) and clinical outcomes (social skill domains) in a defined population. It is independent of any specific machine learning algorithm or computational architecture, focusing instead on the efficacy of therapeutic mechanisms.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variables are defined by the intervention protocol (the type of mindfulness component and delivery format administered), while the predicted variables are distinct clinical outcome measures (e.g., SRS-2 scores, observed peer interaction). These are independent data sources derived from the intervention and the subsequent assessment, not two mathematical summaries of the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

If caregiver-mediated formats prove superior, it provides actionable guidance for clinical resource allocation and theoretical insight into the scaffolding needs of children with ASD. If no specific component shows superiority, it suggests that the general mechanism of mindfulness is what drives change, or that current delivery methods are insufficiently differentiated, both of which are informative for the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names domain relationships (mindfulness components vs. social skill domains) rather than implementation constraints like runtime, memory, or specific software libraries. The mention of "3-month follow-up" is a clinical study design parameter necessary for validity, not a computational budget constraint.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass; the research question targets a genuine gap in clinical psychology regarding the active ingredients of mindfulness for ASD. The formulation is a substantive scientific inquiry into therapeutic mechanisms, free from implementation-method narrowing or circular construction.
