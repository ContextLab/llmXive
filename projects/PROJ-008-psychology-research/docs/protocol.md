# Protocol: Mindfulness Components and Delivery Formats in ASD Social Skills

## 1. Title

Meta-Analysis of Mindfulness-Based Interventions for Improving Social Skills in Children with Autism Spectrum Disorder

## 2. Objective

To synthesize evidence on the efficacy of mindfulness-based interventions for improving social skills in children aged 6-12 with ASD, with a focus on the impact of specific mindfulness components and delivery formats.

## 3. Background

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is characterized by persistent deficits in social communication and social interaction. Mindfulness-based interventions have shown promise in improving emotional regulation and social skills in this population. However, the specific components and delivery formats that contribute to efficacy remain unclear.

## 4. Methods

### 4.1. Data Sources

- ClinicalTrials.gov
- Open Science Framework (OSF)
- *Constitution Principle VI restricts sources to these two registries.*

### 4.2. Eligibility Criteria

- **Population**: Children aged 6-12 with a confirmed ASD diagnosis
- **Intervention**: Mindfulness-based interventions with reported components
- **Comparator**: Waitlist, treatment-as-usual, or active control
- **Outcome**: Standardized social skill measures
- **Study Design**: Randomized controlled trials (RCTs)

### 4.3. Data Extraction

- Study characteristics (author, year, sample size)
- Intervention details (components, duration, delivery format)
- Outcome measures (means, standard deviations, sample sizes)
- Follow-up duration

### 4.4. Statistical Analysis

- **Effect Size**: Hedges' *g* with small-sample correction
- **Model**: Random-effects meta-analysis (if I² > 50%)
- **Subgroup Analysis**: By mindfulness component and delivery format
- **Publication Bias**: Egger's test and funnel plots (if N ≥ 10)
