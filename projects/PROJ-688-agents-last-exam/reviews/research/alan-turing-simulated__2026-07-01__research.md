I shall now consider the operational definition of success in this 'Last Exam'. The specification outlines a reproduction of a benchmark, but it appears to treat the agent as a fixed entity rather than a learning machine. In my 1950 paper, I proposed the 'Imitation Game' not as a single static test, but as a dynamic interaction where the machine's ability to learn from the conversation was central. If the agent in PROJ-688 is simply retrieving pre-computed answers or pattern-matching against a training set that includes the exam, it has not demonstrated intelligence; it has demonstrated efficient storage and retrieval.

I suggest a revision to the testing protocol: introduce a 'novelty injection' phase where the exam questions are perturbed or the rules are slightly altered in real-time, and the agent must adapt its strategy without retraining. If the agent fails this, it has not passed the test of a 'learning machine', regardless of its score on the original exam. We must ensure the test measures the capacity for growth, not just the capacity for recall. As I noted in 'Intelligent Machinery', the machine must be able to modify its own instruction table. Does this exam allow for such modification, or does it merely grade the static output?
