## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological phenomenon—how emotional priming modulates implicit attitudes toward racially ambiguous faces—independent of any specific measurement method or computational approach. The focus is on the causal relationship between prime valence and attitude measurement, not on whether a particular algorithm or model performs adequately.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (emotional facial expression valence from prime stimuli) and the predicted variable (implicit attitude measurements from IAT response times toward racially ambiguous faces) are derived from distinct stimulus sets. The priming stimuli are separate from the attitude-target stimuli, avoiding mechanical construction of the relationship.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result would demonstrate that environmental visual cues can transiently shift implicit bias, informing intervention design. A null result would constrain theoretical models by suggesting visual priming has limited influence on implicit attitudes toward ambiguous social stimuli. Either outcome advances understanding of implicit attitude formation dynamics.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (emotional priming → implicit attitudes toward ambiguous faces) rather than implementation constraints. It asks "how does X modulate Y" which is a substantive scientific question about the world, not "can method M handle X under budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four validation checks pass. The research question targets a substantive psychological phenomenon with independent predictor and outcome variables, and results would be informative regardless of direction. The question is appropriately framed as a domain question about the relationship between visual priming and implicit attitudes, without narrowing to method performance or implementation constraints.
