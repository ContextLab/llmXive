## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether gut microbiome diversity (alpha and beta metrics) is associated with fluid intelligence scores, a substantive biological relationship. It does not hinge on the performance of any particular analytical technique, merely on detecting a statistical association.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictor variables (microbiome diversity indices) are derived from sequencing data, while the outcome (fluid intelligence) comes from cognitive testing. These data streams are independent, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

Both a significant positive correlation and a null result would be informative: a positive finding would support the gut‑brain axis hypothesis at a population level, whereas a null result would suggest that diversity alone is insufficient as a biomarker for cognition.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question frames a domain‑level inquiry (“does diversity correlate with cognition?”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or computational resource.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
