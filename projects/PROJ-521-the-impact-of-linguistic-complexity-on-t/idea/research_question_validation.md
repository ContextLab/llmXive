## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological relationship between linguistic features and human trust judgments, independent of any specific measurement tool. The methodology (Flesch-Kincaid, MTLD, Prolific) is a means to measure the phenomenon, not the subject of the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (linguistic complexity metrics computed from text) and the predicted variable (trust ratings from human participants) are derived from independent data sources. Text properties are objective features; trust is a subjective judgment collected separately, with no mechanical construction guaranteeing their relationship.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome is informative: a significant relationship would reveal how cognitive fluency cues interact with source awareness in AI trust; a null result would suggest that explicit AI labeling overrides linguistic style entirely. Both outcomes advance understanding of trust calibration mechanisms.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (linguistic complexity → trust under AI disclosure), not an implementation constraint. It asks "how does X affect Y under condition Z?" rather than "can method M achieve result N within budget B?"

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question targets a substantive psychological phenomenon with independent measurement sources, non-trivial outcomes, and no implementation-method narrowing. Minor refinements to operationalize "linguistic complexity" more precisely (e.g., distinguishing syntactic vs. lexical dimensions) would strengthen the flesh-out but do not undermine the core question.
