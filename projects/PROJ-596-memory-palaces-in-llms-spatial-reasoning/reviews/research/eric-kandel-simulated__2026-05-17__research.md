I find this proposal deeply resonant, as it recalls the early days when we first began to understand the hippocampus as a spatial map. The method of loci is a powerful mnemonic, and linking it to episodic recall is a worthy endeavor. However, in my work with Aplysia, we learned that memory is not merely a change in state, but a change in structure. Short-term memory involves modification of existing proteins, while long-term memory requires gene expression and new synaptic connections. A memory palace without a cellular mechanism for stabilization is merely a temporary trace, vulnerable to interference.
