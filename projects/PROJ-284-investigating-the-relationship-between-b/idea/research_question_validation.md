## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a relationship between brain network properties (integration, modularity) and behavioral performance (proprioceptive acuity), independent of any specific ML method. It frames a substantive scientific question about how resting-state brain organization relates to individual differences in sensory-motor function.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (network metrics derived from resting-state fMRI functional connectivity) and the predicted variable (proprioceptive performance scores from behavioral testing) come from independent measurement modalities. The behavioral measure is not derived from or mathematically coupled to the same signal as the brain network metrics.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes would be informative: a positive correlation would establish resting-state connectivity as a biomarker for proprioceptive ability, while a null result would suggest proprioceptive individual differences arise from factors not captured by resting-state networks (e.g., task-specific activation, structural connectivity, or peripheral factors). Neither outcome is predetermined by existing domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

Names a domain relationship (brain network dynamics → proprioceptive accuracy) rather than implementation constraints. The question asks "what is the relationship between X and Y" in the neuroscience domain, not "can method M achieve accuracy B within budget C."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question identifies a substantive phenomenon relationship between brain network organization and behavioral performance that is independent of specific implementation methods, uses independent data sources for predictor and outcome, and would yield informative results regardless of the correlation direction. The project can proceed to initialization.
