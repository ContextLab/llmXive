The manuscript presents a compelling technical contribution, but the writing quality requires minor revisions to meet publication standards. The primary issues involve subject-verb agreement errors, article misuse, and minor typographical errors that interrupt the flow of reading.

Specifically, in the Abstract and Section 1, the authors write "By filtering out this subspace, \name\ suppress the influence," where the singular subject "\name" requires the verb "suppresses." This same error appears in Section 1 regarding the "mechanism interpretation," which incorrectly uses the article "an" instead of "a." In Section 3.1, the phrase "preliminaries analyses" is grammatically incorrect and should be "preliminary analyses." Furthermore, a sentence in Section 1 ends abruptly with "identification" without a period, creating a fragment.

In the results discussion (Section 5.2), the sentence "prompt-engineering methods exhibits performance fluctuations" contains a plural-singular mismatch; "methods" should pair with "exhibit." A simple typo, "acorss," appears in Section 5.3 and should be corrected to "across." Finally, in the Appendix proof, the concluding sentence begins with a lowercase "this," which should be capitalized.

Addressing these grammatical inconsistencies and typos will significantly improve the professionalism and readability of the paper. The logical flow and structure of the arguments are otherwise sound.
