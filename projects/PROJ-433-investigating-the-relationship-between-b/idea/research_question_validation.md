## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a relationship between two cognitive/neural phenomena (network reconfigurability and time perception accuracy), independent of any specific ML method. The methodology (sliding windows, Louvain community detection) is described separately and serves as tools rather than the question itself.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (dynamic functional connectivity from resting-state fMRI) and the predicted variable (time perception accuracy from behavioral temporal bisection task) come from independent measurement modalities. The brain imaging data and behavioral scores are not derived from the same primary signal, avoiding mechanical guarantees.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Either outcome would be informative given the stated literature gap: a positive correlation would link network flexibility to temporal cognition, while a null result would suggest dynamic connectivity is not a limiting factor for time perception. The absence of prior empirical tests makes both directions publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (network reconfigurability ↔ time perception accuracy) rather than implementation constraints. Budget and computational limits appear in the methodology sketch, not in the research question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-formed, asking about a genuinely unexplored relationship between brain network dynamics and behavioral time perception, with independent data sources and informative outcomes in either direction.
