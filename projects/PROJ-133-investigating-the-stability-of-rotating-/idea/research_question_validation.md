## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a physical phenomenon (dynamical stability and vortex-lattice structures) in dipolar Bose-Einstein condensates as a function of three physical parameters (rotation frequency, dipolar strength, particle number). It is framed independently of any specific numerical method or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variables (Ω, ε_dd, N) are externally specified input parameters that define the system conditions. The predicted variables (stability, vortex-lattice structure) are emergent properties arising from the time evolution of the Gross-Pitaevskii equation. These are not two views of the same primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (clear phase diagram delineating stable/metastable/unstable regimes) would provide a practical guide for experimental design. A null result (finding that no stable regimes exist beyond contact-interaction expectations, or that stability is unexpectedly robust) would constrain theoretical understanding of dipolar interaction effects on quantum turbulence. Either outcome advances the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a relationship in the domain (how physical parameters determine stability and vortex structures) rather than a constraint on implementation. The methodology (GPE solver, parameter grid) is a means to answer the question, not part of the question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-framed as a substantive physics question about the stability landscape of rotating dipolar BECs, independent of computational constraints or circular constructions. The project can proceed to initialization.
