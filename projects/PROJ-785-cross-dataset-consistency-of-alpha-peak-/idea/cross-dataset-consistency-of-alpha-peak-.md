# Cross-Dataset Consistency of Alpha Peak Frequency Estimates in Resting-State EEG

**Field**: neuroscience

This project investigates the reproducibility of Alpha Peak Frequency (APF) as a potential biomarker by analyzing multiple publicly available resting-state EEG datasets from OpenNeuro. While APF is frequently cited as a marker for cognitive state and aging, its estimation varies significantly based on preprocessing choices and hardware, undermining cross-study comparisons. The research question asks: How much variance in APF estimates is attributable to dataset source versus preprocessing pipeline? The proposed approach involves downloading three to five small resting-state EEG cohorts (n<50 each), applying a standardized minimal preprocessing workflow, and calculating APF using both time-domain and frequency-domain methods. Statistical analysis will quantify inter-dataset variability and identify robust estimation protocols. This work matters because establishing a stable baseline for APF is critical before deploying it as a clinical or cognitive marker in lightweight diagnostic tools. Results will inform best practices for public data re-analysis.
