# Implementation Plan: llmXive Follow-up: Teacher Entanglement vs. Scalar Distillation Loss

## Summary

This project validates the hypothesis that "structural entanglement" (statistical spread and dependency) in a teacher model's multi-dimensional score distribution predicts the "dimensional fidelity loss" when distilling to a scalar student model. The approach involves ingesting the Z-Reward evaluation dataset, computing valid statistical descriptors (Variance, Range, Global Eigenvalue) of teacher outputs, calculating the deviation of student outputs from human ground truth, and training a CPU-based Random Forest regressor with 5-fold cross-validation to quantify the *associational* power (R²) of entanglement on human-aligned error.
