# Research: Quantifying the Impact of Network Structure on Energy Dissipation in Driven Granular Materials

## Problem Statement

Driven Granular Materials (DGM) exhibit complex energy dissipation behaviors influenced by the topology of their internal contact networks. While it is known that force chains and network structure play a critical role, a quantitative, statistically rigorous link between specific topological metrics (coordination number, clustering coefficient) and the rate of energy dissipation remains to be fully characterized. This project seeks to extract these metrics from Discrete Element Method (DEM) simulations and establish their **statistical association** with dissipation rates, controlling for driving amplitude.

**Important Note on Causality**: The analysis is strictly observational. Network topology and dissipation are simultaneously determined by driving amplitude and packing density. Without instrumental variables or a structural causal model, regression coefficients capture **confounded associations**, not causal impacts. Findings will be framed as "associations" and "predictive power" in a statistical sense, not causal effects.

**Critical Methodological Correction**: The initial hypothesis proposed using "Force Heterogeneity" (CV of forces) as a predictor. However, since dissipation is calculated as the sum of force × velocity, and force heterogeneity is derived from the same force vector, a regression including this predictor would be **tautological** (predicting a variable from itself). The revised methodology **excludes Force Heterogeneity from the regression model** and tests only whether *geometric* topology (coordination, clustering) predicts dissipation.

## Statistical Methodology

### Correlation Analysis
- **Methods**: Pearson and Spearman correlation coefficients.
- **Variables**:
 - *Predictors*: Mean Coordination Number, Clustering Coefficient. **(Force Heterogeneity is excluded from regression due to functional dependence on the outcome).**
 - *Outcome*: Energy Dissipation Rate.
- **Correction**: Bonferroni or FDR correction applied if >5 tests are conducted to control Family-Wise Error Rate (FWER).
- **Significance Threshold**: p < 0.01 (per Constitution Principle VII).

### Regression Analysis
- **Model**: Generalized Least Squares (GLS) with AR(1) error structure OR Newey-West corrected OLS.
- **Rationale**: DEM time-series data exhibits strong temporal autocorrelation. Standard OLS standard errors are biased; GLS/Newey-West corrects for this.
- **Control Variable**: Driving Amplitude (to account for confounding effects).
- **Interaction Terms**: Include interaction terms (e.g., `Driving Amplitude * Coordination Number`) to address potential non-linearity and ensure the "network effect" is not merely a proxy for the "driving effect".
- **Collinearity Check**: Variance Inflation Factor (VIF) calculated. If Coordination Number and Clustering Coefficient are highly collinear (definitionally related), results will be reported descriptively without claiming independent causal effects.
- **Circularity Mitigation**: **Force Heterogeneity is excluded from the regression model.** It is reported only as a descriptive statistic to avoid the tautology where the predictor is derived from the same force vector as the outcome.

### Cross-Dataset Validation
- **Method**: Mixed-effects model or ANOVA to test for slope differences across datasets with varying driving amplitudes.
- **Metric**: ANOVA p-value. If p > 0.05, slopes are considered consistent (generalizable).
- **Regime Requirement**: Validation must cover **at least three independent driving parameter regimes** to satisfy Constitution Principle VII.

### Success Criteria (Revised)
- **Significance**: Focus on statistical significance (p < 0.01) and model diagnostics (residual autocorrelation) rather than a fixed R-squared threshold, acknowledging the noisy nature of physical systems.
- **Robustness**: Results are considered successful if they are robust across the three driving regimes and diagnostics indicate no severe bias.

## Limitations & Assumptions

- **Observational Nature**: The analysis identifies associations, not causal effects, as network structure is not randomized.
- **Data Availability**: As noted, the verified external datasets may not contain the specific granular variables. The pipeline relies on the user providing a valid Yade-DEM file or the synthetic generator for testing.
- **Measurement Validity**: Force heterogeneity (CV) is used as a proxy for stress distribution, citing Majmudar & Behringer, but is excluded from regression to avoid tautology.
- **Static Packings**: If driving amplitude is zero, dissipation is calculated as |ΔKE + ΔPE| to avoid division by zero.
- **Collider Bias Avoidance**: Steady-state detection uses **Kinetic Energy variance**, not dissipation rate variance, to avoid selection bias. **This overrides FR-011 of the spec.**
