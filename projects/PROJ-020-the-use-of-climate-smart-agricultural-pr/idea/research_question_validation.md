## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the real-world relationship between agricultural practices and livelihood outcomes, independent of any specific algorithm or model architecture. It focuses on the phenomenon of adoption and its impact rather than computational performance or benchmark constraints.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (CSA practice adoption) and predicted variable (food security/dietary diversity) are distinct concepts measured via separate survey modules within the LSMS dataset. They are not mathematically derived from the same primary signal, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While a positive correlation is expected, the magnitude and the specific mediation by digital technologies are not predetermined by domain knowledge. A null result would indicate significant barriers to practice effectiveness, which remains highly informative for policy.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a substantive domain relationship (CSA practices affecting food security) rather than a constraint on the implementation or computational budget. It seeks to understand the mechanism of improvement, not just the feasibility of measurement.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass as the question targets a substantive domain relationship without circularity or triviality. The proposed methodology aligns with the research question's intent to quantify real-world impacts.
