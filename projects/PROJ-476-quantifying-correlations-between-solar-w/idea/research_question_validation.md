## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a physical relationship between solar wind composition parameters (proton density, temperature, helium abundance) and geomagnetic activity, independent of any specific ML or statistical method. The framing focuses on understanding the underlying coupling mechanism between solar wind and magnetosphere rather than evaluating a particular algorithm's performance.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (solar wind composition from ACE spacecraft plasma instruments) and the predicted variable (Kp/Dst geomagnetic indices from ground-based magnetometers) are measured by independent instruments at different locations. The solar wind composition describes incoming plasma, while the geomagnetic indices describe Earth's magnetic field response—two physically distinct signals.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes would be informative: a significant correlation would add composition parameters to the predictive toolkit for space weather forecasting, while a null result would constrain theoretical models of solar wind-magnetosphere coupling by showing composition adds no value beyond dynamic pressure. Neither outcome is predetermined by established domain knowledge.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (how solar wind composition relates to geomagnetic disturbances) rather than implementation constraints. The methodology (correlation analysis with time lags) serves the question rather than being the question itself.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass without significant concerns. The research question targets a substantive physical relationship with independent measurements, both positive and null results would advance understanding, and the question is framed at the domain level rather than being implementation-constrained. This idea is ready to advance to project initialization.
