# AnyFlow: Any-Step Video Diffusion Model with On-Policy Flow Map Distillation

**Field**: Computer Science (Generative AI / Video Diffusion)

## Research question

How does on-policy flow map distillation mitigate the performance degradation observed in consistency-distilled video models when increasing the number of sampling steps beyond the training regime?

## Motivation

Consistency distillation has significantly accelerated video generation, but these models often suffer from a "sweet spot" limitation where quality degrades if users attempt to use more sampling steps than the model was optimized for. This restricts the flexibility of inference and prevents the trade-off between speed and quality that is standard in standard diffusion models. Addressing this gap is crucial for enabling adaptive, high-fidelity video generation where inference resources can be dynamically allocated without sacrificing visual coherence.

## Related work

- [AnyFlow: Any-Step Video Diffusion Model with On-Policy Flow Map Distillation (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.13724) — The primary source proposing the solution to the multi-step degradation problem via on-policy distillation.
- [Flow-OPD: On-Policy Distillation for Flow Matching Models (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.08063) — Establishes the methodological precedent for using on-policy distillation to resolve reward sparsity and gradient interference in flow matching, which AnyFlow adapts for the video domain.
- [Video Diffusion Models: A Survey (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.03150) — Provides the comprehensive background on current video diffusion architectures and highlights the specific challenge of sampling efficiency and consistency.
- [FSampler: Training Free Acceleration of Diffusion Sampling via Epsilon Extrapolation (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.09180) — Represents a competing approach to acceleration that focuses on training-free extrapolation, contrasting with AnyFlow's training-based distillation strategy.

## Expected results

We expect to observe that the proposed AnyFlow model maintains or improves Fréchet Video Distance (FVD) scores as the number of sampling steps increases from 1 to 100, whereas baseline consistency models show a peak and subsequent decline. The level of evidence required is a statistically significant difference in FVD and perceptual metrics across a grid of sampling steps compared to standard consistency distillation baselines on a public video dataset.

## Methodology sketch

- Download the public UCF-101 or DAVIS video dataset from their official repositories to serve as the training and evaluation corpus.
- Implement a base video flow matching model using PyTorch, ensuring the architecture supports variable-step integration.
- Design the on-policy distillation loop where the student model generates trajectories at varying step counts, and the teacher model (the original flow model) provides guidance specifically for those trajectories.
- Train the AnyFlow model by optimizing the flow map loss on the trajectories generated during the on-policy updates, ensuring the distillation objective aligns with the actual inference distribution.
- Evaluate the trained model by generating video samples at step counts $N \in \{1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 100\}$.
- Compute Fréchet Video Distance (FVD) and Inception Score (IS) for each step count using a pre-trained FVD encoder (e.g., I3D) downloaded from a public model zoo.
- Perform a statistical analysis (ANOVA) to compare the variance of quality metrics across step counts for AnyFlow versus a standard consistency distillation baseline.
- Visualize the "quality vs. steps" curve to demonstrate the absence of the degradation peak in the AnyFlow results.
