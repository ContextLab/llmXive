# Exploring the Convergence of Iterated Function Systems with Non-Contractive Maps

**Field**: mathematics

Iterated Function Systems (IFS) are commonly used to generate fractals, and their convergence is well-understood when the maps are contractive. However, many interesting dynamical systems involve maps that are not strictly contractive. This research proposes to investigate the conditions under which an IFS with non-contractive maps still converges to an attractor, and to characterize the properties of those attractors. The approach would involve analyzing the spectral radius of the associated matrix and exploring the impact of map parameters on convergence, potentially using numerical simulations to visualize the attractors and confirm theoretical findings. This could provide new insights into the dynamics of IFS and broaden their applicability to modeling more complex systems.
