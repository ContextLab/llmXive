## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks how elemental composition influences the shear modulus of bulk metallic glasses and which compositional descriptors are most predictive. It is framed purely in terms of a material science relationship, without tying the answer to any particular algorithmic implementation.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

Predictors are compositional descriptors derived from elemental properties (size mismatch, mixing enthalpy, VEC, etc.), while the target is experimentally measured shear modulus. These originate from independent data streams, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

While prior work hints at qualitative links, a quantitative assessment across multiple alloy families is absent. Demonstrating a strong predictive model would substantiate compositional design rules, and a weak or null result would highlight missing descriptors, making both outcomes publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question focuses on a domain-level relationship (“how does composition determine shear modulus”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific computational method or resource budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
