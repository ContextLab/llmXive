# Glossary

- **Moran's I**: A measure of spatial autocorrelation. Values range from -1 (dispersion) to 1 (clustering).
- **Spatial Autocorrelation**: The degree to which a set of values are correlated with each other over space.
- **Statistical Power**: The probability of correctly rejecting a false null hypothesis.
- **Type II Error**: Failing to reject a false null hypothesis (false negative).
- **Nearest-Neighbor Resampling**: A method for changing resolution that assigns the value of the nearest pixel, preserving categorical data.
- **Gibbs Sampler**: A Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) algorithm used to generate a sequence of observations from a probability distribution.
- **Null Distribution (H0)**: The distribution of a statistic under the assumption that the null hypothesis is true.
- **Alternative Distribution (H1)**: The distribution of a statistic under the assumption that the alternative hypothesis is true.
- **Lambda (λ)**: The spatial lag parameter in a spatial autoregressive model.
- **NLCD**: National Land Cover Database.
- **Raster**: A grid of cells (pixels) representing spatial data.
- **Resolution**: The size of a pixel in the raster (e.g., 30m, 60m).
- **Power Curve**: A plot showing statistical power as a function of resolution.
- **Threshold**: The resolution at which statistical power drops below a specified level (e.g., 0.80).
