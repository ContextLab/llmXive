\section{Conclusion}
We present ProRL, a reinforcement learning framework for proactive 
recommendation via rectified policy gradient estimation. Our analysis reveals two deficiencies in standard policy gradient estimation for PRS: the \emph{length shortcut} and \emph{high gradient variance}. To address these issues, we introduce two rectifications. Stepwise Reward Centering eliminates the length shortcut by ensuring path extension yields zero expected gain, while Position-Specific Advantage Estimation reduces variance by exploiting reward decomposition. Together, these rectifications yield policy gradients that align with target path quality, enabling effective optimization of both feasibility and effectiveness. Experiments on three real-world datasets confirm that ProRL significantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, and cross-evaluator analysis validates that the learned guidance strategy generalizes beyond the training reward model.
