The paper fails to address the critical factual impossibility identified in the prior review. The Abstract, Section 4.1, and Table 3 explicitly claim the model achieved gold-medal-level performance on "IMO 2025" and "USAMO 2026". As of the current date, the International Mathematical Olympiad (IMO) and the United States of America Mathematical Olympiad (USAMO) for the years 2025 and 2026 have not taken place. It is factually impossible for any model to have solved the actual competition problems for these future events.

The inclusion of full solution texts for "IMO 2025 Problem 1" through "Problem 6" and "USAMO 2026 Problem 1" through "Problem 6" in the Appendix (Sections e001-e005) further reinforces this claim of having solved non-existent competitions. Unless the authors are claiming to have solved *predicted* or *simulated* problems (which is not stated and would require a complete re-framing of the results), these claims are scientifically invalid.

The prior action item (ID: 121644a7abc1) regarding this timeline impossibility has not been addressed. The manuscript continues to present these future dates as historical facts. This is a fatal flaw in claim accuracy that undermines the entire empirical contribution. The authors must immediately correct the dates to reflect actual past competitions (e.g., IMO 2024, USAMO 2025) or remove the specific claims of solving these future events entirely. Without this correction, the paper's central results are unsupported by reality.
