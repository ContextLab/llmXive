## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon‑vs‑method check

**Verdict**: pass  

The question asks whether composition‑dependent differential accelerations exist for geodetic satellites, i.e., a test of the weak equivalence principle. It is a substantive physical inquiry and does not hinge on the performance of a particular analysis algorithm or hardware constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  

The predictor is the known material composition (density, bulk properties) of each satellite, an external attribute independent of the SLR measurements. The predicted variable is a composition‑dependent acceleration term inferred from the same SLR orbit data, but it is not a direct re‑expression of the composition; rather it is a dynamical parameter whose existence is being tested.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  

A null result (no detectable differential acceleration) would tighten existing limits on the Eötvös parameter, which is valuable for constraining alternative theories of gravity. A statistically significant non‑zero result would constitute a discovery of WEP violation. Both outcomes would be publishable.

### Question‑narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  

The research question names a relationship in fundamental physics (“do satellites of differing composition exhibit differential accelerations?”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or implementation.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated  

All four checks pass, indicating the research question is well‑posed, scientifically interesting, and free of methodological or circularity flaws.
