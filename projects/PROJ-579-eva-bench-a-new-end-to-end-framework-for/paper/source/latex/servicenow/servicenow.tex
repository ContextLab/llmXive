\title{\framework: A New End-to-end Framework for Evaluating Voice Agents}


\author{Tara Bogavelli}
\author{Gabrielle Gauthier Melançon}
\author{Katrina Stankiewicz}
\author{Oluwanifemi Bamgbose}
\author{Fanny Riols} 
\author{Hoang H. Nguyen}
\author{Raghav Mehndiratta}
\author{Lindsay Devon Brin}
\author{Hari Subramani}
\author{Joseph Marinier}
\author{Anil Madamala}
\author{Sridhar Krishna Nemala}
\author{Srinivas Sunkara}


\affiliation{ServiceNow}




\abstract{
Voice agents, artificial intelligence systems that conduct spoken conversations to complete tasks, are increasingly deployed across enterprise applications. However, no existing benchmark jointly addresses two core evaluation challenges: generating realistic simulated conversations, and measuring quality across the full scope of voice-specific failure modes. We present \textbf{\framework}, an end-to-end evaluation framework that addresses both. On the simulation side, \framework~orchestrates bot-to-bot audio conversations over dynamic multi-turn dialogues, with automatic simulation validation that detects user simulator error and appropriately regenerates conversations before scoring. On the measurement side, \framework~introduces two composite metrics: \textbf{EVA-A} (Accuracy), capturing task completion, faithfulness, and audio-level speech fidelity; and \textbf{EVA-X} (Experience), capturing conversation progression, spoken conciseness, and turn-taking timing. Both metrics apply to all major agent architectures, enabling direct cross-architecture comparison. \framework~includes 213 scenarios across three enterprise domains, a controlled perturbation suite for accent and noise robustness, and \passatone, \passatk, \passpowerk~measurements that distinguish peak from reliable capability. Across 12 systems spanning all three architectures, we find: (1) no system simultaneously exceeds 0.5 on both $\textbf{EVA-A}_{\textbf{pass@1}}$ and $\textbf{EVA-X}_{\textbf{pass@1}}$; (2) peak and reliable performance diverge substantially (median \passatk--\passpowerk~gap of 0.44 on EVA-A); and (3) accent and noise perturbations expose substantial robustness gaps, with effects varying across architectures, systems, and metrics (mean $\Delta$ up to 0.314). We release the full framework, evaluation suite, and benchmark data under an open-source license.}
