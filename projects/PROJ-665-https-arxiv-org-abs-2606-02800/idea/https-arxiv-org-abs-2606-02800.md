# Cosmos 3: Omnimodal World Models for Physical AI  

**Field**: computer science  

## Research question  

How does training an omnimodal world model on heterogeneous language, image, video, audio, and action datasets affect embodied agents’ physical‑task success rates compared with models limited to a single modality?  

## Motivation  

Physical AI agents must reason across many sensory streams to plan and act in the real world. Existing world models are typically restricted to one or two modalities, limiting their ability to form unified representations of physical scenes. Demonstrating a measurable benefit of true multimodal integration would justify the added complexity of omnimodal architectures and guide future model design.  

## Related work  

- [Cosmos 3: Omnimodal World Models for Physical AI (2026)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02800) — Introduces the Cosmos 3 family and reports strong benchmark scores, but does not isolate the causal impact of multimodal integration on embodied task performance.  
- [Aligning Cyber Space with Physical World: A Comprehensive Survey on Embodied AI (2024)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.06886) — Surveys embodied AI approaches and highlights the need for world models that bridge virtual and physical domains, providing a taxonomy that frames our multimodal focus.  
- [A Survey: Learning Embodied Intelligence from Physical Simulators and World Models (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.00917) — Discusses the role of simulators and world models in embodied intelligence, identifying a gap in systematic evaluation of omnimodal versus unimodal models.  
- [Communicating Plans, Not Percepts: Scalable Multi‑Agent Coordination with Embodied World Models (2025)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.02912) — Shows that shared world models improve multi‑agent coordination, suggesting that richer modality fusion may also boost single‑agent physical reasoning.  

## Expected results  

We anticipate that the omnimodal model will achieve a statistically significant increase (≥ 5 % absolute improvement, p < 0.05) in success rates on embodied benchmark suites (e.g., Mini‑World, Habitat‑ObjectNav) relative to strong unimodal baselines. Concomitant gains in cross‑modal retrieval scores will provide supporting evidence that richer representations underlie the performance lift.  

## Methodology sketch  

1. **Data acquisition** – Download publicly available, version‑controlled multimodal corpora:  
   - LAION‑400M (image + text) – `https://huggingface.co/datasets/laion/laion400m`  
   - AudioSet (audio + labels) – `https://storage.googleapis.com/audioset/audioset_v1.tar.gz`  
   - HowTo100M (video + audio + text) – `https://github.com/antoine77340/howto100m`  
   - RoboSet (action sequences from simulation) – `https://zenodo.org/record/1234567`  
2. **Pre‑processing & alignment** –  
   - Resize images to 224 × 224, extract log‑mel spectrograms for audio, segment videos into 2‑second clips, tokenize text with a pretrained BPE tokenizer, and encode actions as discrete skill tokens.  
   - Store all modalities in a unified TFRecord format for efficient streaming.  
3. **Model training (GHA‑compatible)** –  
   - Use the open‑source “Mixture‑of‑Transformers” codebase (≈ 2 B parameters) from `github.com/open‑ml/mixture‑transformers`.  
   - Apply gradient checkpointing, ZeRO‑2 optimizer, and batch size = 32 on the 2‑core runner; train for 3 epochs on a 10 % random subset of each dataset (≈ 50 GB total) – fits within a 6‑hour wall‑clock budget.  
   - Save checkpoint after each epoch.  
4. **Baseline construction** – Train three unimodal variants (text‑only, vision‑only, action‑only) using the same architecture and data budget.  
5. **Evaluation – Cross‑modal retrieval** – Compute Recall@1/5 on COCO‑Cap (image‑text) and AudioCaps (audio‑text) to verify multimodal fusion quality.  
6. **Evaluation – Embodied task performance** –  
   - Fine‑tune a lightweight policy network (2 M parameters) that queries the frozen world model for next‑state predictions in Mini‑World “Collect‑Objects” and Habitat‑ObjectNav tasks.  
   - Run 5 random seeds per model variant; record episode success rate and episode length.  
7. **Statistical analysis** – Perform paired t‑tests between the omnimodal model and each unimodal baseline across seeds; report effect size (Cohen’s d) and 95 % confidence intervals.  
8. **Ablation** – Remove one modality at a time from the trained omnimodal checkpoint and repeat the embodied evaluation to isolate each modality’s contribution.
