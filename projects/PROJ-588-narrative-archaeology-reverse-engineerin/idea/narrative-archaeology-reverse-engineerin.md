# Narrative Archaeology: Reverse-Engineering Story Memories from Brain Data

Develop methods to decode and reconstruct narrative memories from neural recordings, then use these insights to improve how LLMs encode and retrieve story information. This project would use fMRI/MEG data from people recalling stories to understand narrative representation.

Research plan:
- Collect brain data during story encoding, recall, and retelling
- Identify neural signatures of plot points, characters, and themes
- Develop decoding algorithms to reconstruct story elements
- Implement brain-inspired narrative representations in LLMs
- Test whether bio-inspired encoding improves story coherence

Combines narrative psychology, neuroscience, and AI for memory research.
