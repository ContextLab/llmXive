# Investigating the Relationship Between Molecular Topology and Reaction Selectivity

**Field**: chemistry

This project will investigate whether molecular topology, as quantified by topological indices like the Wiener index or Balaban index, can predict the regioselectivity of electrophilic aromatic substitution reactions. While electronic and steric effects are well-established determinants of reactivity, the contribution of global molecular shape remains less explored. Using a publicly available dataset of aromatic compounds and their reported substitution reaction products (e.g., from the NIST Chemistry WebBook or similar), we will calculate a range of topological indices for each reactant and correlate them with the observed product ratios. This analysis could reveal subtle but systematic relationships, potentially leading to a more holistic understanding of reaction mechanisms and enabling predictive modeling of reactivity.
