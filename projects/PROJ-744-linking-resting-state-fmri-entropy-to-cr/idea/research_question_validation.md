## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether a neurophysiological property (whole‑brain resting‑state fMRI entropy) is associated with a cognitive phenotype (divergent‑thinking performance). It does not depend on any particular analysis pipeline or hardware constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (multiscale sample entropy derived from BOLD time series) and the outcome (Alternative Uses Test scores) come from distinct data sources—neuroimaging versus behavioral testing—so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A significant positive association would identify entropy as a non‑invasive marker of creative ability; a null result would demonstrate that entropy alone does not capture creative cognition. Both outcomes would provide useful insight for the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The research question names a domain relationship (“entropy predicts creative performance”) rather than imposing a constraint on a specific computational method or resource budget.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
