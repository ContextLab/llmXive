## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the comparative effectiveness of two governance models (CBNRM vs. state-led) on environmental outcomes, independent of the statistical method used to analyze them. It focuses on the policy-environment relationship rather than the performance of a specific algorithm or computational constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (CBNRM policy adoption indices) is derived from governance and policy datasets, while the predicted variable (land-use change rates) comes from environmental monitoring sources like FAO STAT. These are independent measurement modalities, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Given the motivation states empirical evidence is "fragmented," a null result (no difference between management types) would be as informative as a positive one for policy debate. Neither outcome is predetermined by domain knowledge, making both publishable contributions to the field.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names the domain relationship between management structures and land use outcomes rather than constraints on the implementation (e.g., specific software or hardware limits). It frames a substantive inquiry into environmental governance efficacy.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass; the research question targets a substantive policy-environment relationship without circularity, triviality, or implementation narrowing. The project is ready to advance to project initialization.
