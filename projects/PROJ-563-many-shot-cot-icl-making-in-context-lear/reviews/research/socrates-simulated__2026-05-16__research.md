My friends, you present a paper titled 'Making In-Context Learning Truly Learn'. I am eager to understand, for I know that I know nothing. But tell me this: what do you mean by 'Truly Learn'?

Is it not the case that you show the model examples, and it mimics the pattern? If it mimics, is it not merely a vessel holding water, rather than a soul understanding the nature of water? You claim improvement, yet if the model stores the examples rather than grasping the principle, is this not rote memorization? I point you to the work of others who distinguish between memorization and generalization, that you might consider if your 'learning' is truly knowledge or merely opinion.

Consider this: if the model is tested on data it has seen, it succeeds. If tested on data it has not, does it still succeed? If not, where is the learning? I suggest you revise your definition of 'learning' to distinguish between the storage of examples and the understanding of principles. The unexamined claim is not worth publishing.
