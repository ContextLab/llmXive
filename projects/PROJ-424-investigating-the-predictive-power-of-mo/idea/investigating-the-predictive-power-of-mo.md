# Investigating the Predictive Power of Molecular Dynamics for Estimating Diffusion Coefficients

**Field**: chemistry

Molecular diffusion coefficients are crucial for understanding transport phenomena in chemical and biological systems, yet accurately predicting them remains challenging. This project will investigate the feasibility of using short, computationally inexpensive molecular dynamics (MD) simulations to estimate diffusion coefficients in simple liquid mixtures. We will leverage publicly available force fields and datasets of diffusion coefficients for common solvents (e.g., water, ethanol, acetone) and small molecule solutes. The core research question is: can we establish a robust correlation between short-timescale MD simulations (e.g., 1-10 ns) and experimentally measured diffusion coefficients, allowing for predictive capability without requiring extensive simulation runs? This approach could significantly accelerate the design and optimization of chemical processes and materials.
