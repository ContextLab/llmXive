## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether climatic and geographic variables can explain variation in avian song, which is a substantive scientific inquiry about the relationship between environment and acoustic phenotype. It does not hinge on the performance of a particular modeling technique or computational resource.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictor data (climate records, geographic coordinates) are derived from meteorological and spatial datasets, whereas the predicted variable (song variation) is measured from acoustic recordings of birds. These sources are independent, so the predictive relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a significant predictive relationship and the absence of one would be informative. A positive result would support the acoustic adaptation hypothesis, while a null result would suggest other factors dominate song evolution, making either outcome publishable.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question focuses on a domain-level relationship—how avian song variation behaves in relation to climatic and geographic factors—rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or implementation.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
