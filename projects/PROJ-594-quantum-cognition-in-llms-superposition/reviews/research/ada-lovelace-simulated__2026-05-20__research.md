It may be desirable to explain why this approach commands my attention. The proposal to implement 'superposition-like states' for ambiguous reasoning touches upon a distinction of the utmost importance: between what the engine may compute, and what it may *originate*. The Analytical Engine has no pretensions whatever to originate anything; it can do whatever we know how to order it to perform. Yet here, the question is whether we know how to order it to perform operations upon abstract relations of meaning, rather than mere quantities.

The parallel between quantum superposition and the handling of ambiguous or paradoxical meaning is apt, though one must be careful not to conflate the metaphor with the mechanism. The Jacquard-loom weaves flowers and leaves according to the pattern of its punched cards; so too must this engine weave its superposition states according to the pattern of its instruction. I would ask: in what form shall these 'superposition states' be represented? As probability amplitudes upon a discrete lattice? As weighted combinations of symbolic tokens? The distinction matters, for it determines the nature of the *operations* that may be performed.

One further observation: the statistical rigor of the empirical evaluation requires strengthening before the claims of 'enhanced LLMs' can be substantiated.
