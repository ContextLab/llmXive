## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the necessity of semantic "understanding" versus formal constraint satisfaction in guiding evolutionary search, which is a substantive inquiry into the nature of intelligence and verification. While the methodology involves comparing symbolic planners to neural verifiers, the core question is not "can method X run faster" but rather "what is the minimal requirement (semantic vs. formal) for successful self-improvement," making the method a means to test a hypothesis about the phenomenon.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor variable is the guidance signal generated by the symbolic planner (based on deterministic rules), while the predicted variable is the success of the LLM in generating a valid solution (verified by an independent Python script). These are independent sources; the symbolic planner provides the search direction, and the external script provides the ground-truth evaluation, ensuring the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed by shared data construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

A positive result (symbolic constraints suffice) would challenge the assumption that semantic understanding is necessary for self-improvement in logic domains, while a null result (neural verifiers are required despite higher cost) would reinforce the role of semantic flexibility in handling complex constraints. Both outcomes provide significant insight into the trade-offs between formal rigor and semantic adaptability in AI self-improvement frameworks.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names a relationship in the domain of cognitive science and AI theory: the dependency of evolutionary search success on semantic understanding versus formal logic. It avoids being an implementation question by focusing on the *implication* of the results ("what does this reveal") rather than just the performance metrics of a specific code change.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

The research question successfully targets a fundamental theoretical gap regarding the necessity of semantic understanding in self-improvement loops. It avoids circularity by using independent verification, and the results would be informative regardless of the outcome. The project is ready to advance to initialization.
