## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass  
The question asks whether variations in gut microbiome composition are associated with differences in sleep architecture (e.g., proportions of REM, NREM stages). It does not hinge on any particular analytical technique or hardware constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass  
Predictor data (microbial taxa abundances from stool metagenomic sequencing) and outcome data (sleep stage metrics from polysomnography or actigraphy) are obtained from distinct physiological measurements, so the relationship is not mechanically guaranteed.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass  
While the gut‑brain axis is a recognized concept, the specific link between microbiome composition and detailed sleep architecture remains unsettled. Either a significant correlation or a null finding would provide novel insight into how peripheral microbial communities may influence central sleep regulation.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass  
The question focuses on a biological relationship (“how does X relate to Y?”) rather than on constraints of a particular method or implementation.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
