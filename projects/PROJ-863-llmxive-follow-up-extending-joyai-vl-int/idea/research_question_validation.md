## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: fail

The research question is currently framed around the performance of a specific implementation strategy ("internal hidden states" vs. "explicit logits") for a resource-constrained scheduler, rather than a fundamental question about the nature of human-AI interaction or cognitive load. The core inquiry is whether a specific architectural feature (latent embeddings) is a better proxy than another (logits) for a specific engineering task (gating inference), which makes the answer a benchmark result rather than a scientific discovery about cognitive mechanisms.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: concern

The predictor (hidden states of the VLM) and the predicted variable (synthetic cognitive load derived from `py-sim-cognitive` based on the *same* video streams and conversational context) share a highly overlapping data source. While the physiological labels are generated by a simulator, the stimuli driving both the VLM's internal state and the simulator's "load" calculation are identical, creating a risk that the model is learning to recognize the *difficulty of the input* rather than a distinct correlation between its internal processing and human cognitive state.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: concern

If the result is positive (hidden states correlate well), it may simply confirm that the model "knows" the task is hard, which is a known property of deep networks (activation magnitude correlates with task difficulty). If the result is null, it might be dismissed as a failure of the specific synthetic simulator rather than a fundamental lack of signal. A reasonable researcher might find a negative result uninformative without a rigorous control proving the simulator's labels are distinct from the model's input difficulty, and a positive result might be viewed as a trivial engineering finding rather than a novel cognitive insight.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: fail

The question focuses on the feasibility of a specific engineering solution (using layer-wise states for a lightweight scheduler on CPU) rather than a domain relationship. It asks "Can we use X to gate Y under constraint Z?" which is an implementation question. A domain question would ask "How does the representational geometry of a VLM evolve in response to increasing environmental complexity, and does this evolution mirror human cognitive resource allocation?"

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validator_revise

The project addresses a valid engineering gap but frames it as a scientific question about alignment when it is currently a question about proxy efficacy. To validate, the question must be reframed to investigate the *mechanism* of alignment between model processing and human load, rather than the *utility* of that alignment for a scheduler.
