# Predicting Molecular Properties from Quantum Chemical Calculations with Limited Computational Resources

**Field**: chemistry

Can machine learning models trained on a relatively small set of quantum chemical calculations (e.g., DFT with a modest basis set) accurately predict properties like dipole moment, polarizability, and atomic charges when applied to larger, more complex molecules? This research would leverage publicly available datasets of quantum chemical calculations (e.g., from the NIST Computational Chemistry Comparison Key) to train and validate a graph neural network. The focus is on identifying the minimal computational cost required for generating training data that yields meaningful predictive power, potentially enabling rapid screening of molecular properties without resorting to computationally expensive high-accuracy calculations for every molecule. This could accelerate materials discovery and rational molecular design.
