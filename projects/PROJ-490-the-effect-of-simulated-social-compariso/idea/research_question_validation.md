## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about a psychological phenomenon (how virtual social comparison affects self-esteem) with an individual-difference moderator (social comparison tendency). The question is independent of any specific ML method or computational constraint; the proposed linear regression is simply a tool to answer a substantive domain question.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (avatar exposure condition) is an experimental manipulation, the predicted variable (self-esteem) is measured via the Rosenberg Self-Esteem Scale, and the moderator (social comparison tendency) is measured via the Iowa-Netherlands Comparison Orientation Measure. These are three independent measurement sources with no shared primary signal.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both outcomes would be informative: a positive result would confirm VR amplifies social comparison effects (with implications for platform design and mental health), while a null result would suggest VR differs from 2D media in ways that protect against comparison effects. Either finding advances theory about immersive environments and self-evaluation.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question names a domain relationship (avatar exposure → self-esteem, moderated by social comparison tendency) rather than an implementation constraint. It asks "how does X affect Y" in the psychological domain, not "can method M handle X under budget B."

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All four checks pass. The research question is well-formulated as a substantive scientific inquiry into a psychological phenomenon in VR contexts, with independent predictor and outcome measures, and both possible outcomes would contribute meaningful knowledge to the field.
