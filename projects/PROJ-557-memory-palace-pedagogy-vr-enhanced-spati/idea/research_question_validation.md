## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks whether moment‑by‑moment adaptation of text complexity to cognitive load influences long‑term retention of abstract concepts. This is a substantive scientific inquiry about learning processes, independent of any particular algorithmic implementation or hardware constraint.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (text‑complexity adaptation driven by a physiological Cognitive‑Load Index derived from pupil diameter) and the predicted variable (delayed recall scores) come from distinct measurement modalities. There is no mechanical linkage that makes the prediction guaranteed by construction.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

Both a positive finding (adaptation improves retention) and a null finding (no effect) would be informative for educational theory and practice. The hypothesis is not predetermined by existing consensus; the outcome would advance understanding of load‑adaptive instruction.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The research question names a relationship in the domain of cognitive psychology (“how does adaptive text complexity affect retention?”) rather than imposing constraints on a specific method or resource budget.

**Verdict**: validated
