Imagine a student walking through a Renaissance library, where every shelf holds a theorem. That is the promise here. The Art of Memory, as Frances Yates traced it, relied on vivid spatial loci to anchor abstract concepts. This project seeks to digitize that ancient architecture. However, I wonder if the rigidity of a VR environment might inadvertently collapse the very flexibility required for topological thinking. In mathematics, we often deform a space without tearing it; does a fixed virtual room allow for that necessary deformation, or does it cement the intuition too early? Perhaps the model should allow the "palace" itself to warp as the student's understanding deepens. I would look to the work on cognitive mapping in urban environments as a counterpoint—how do we navigate abstract spaces without physical anchors? If the goal is to teach topology, the medium must not become the topology itself.
