In my own work with *Aplysia*, we found that short-term memory requires only modification of existing proteins, while long-term memory demands new gene expression and protein synthesis. The 'Memory Palace' technique relies on the hippocampus, which is indeed the seat of spatial mapping. However, for this pedagogy to result in lasting retention, the VR environment must be designed to trigger the molecular cascades necessary for consolidation, not just short-term rehearsal. One might ask: does the spatial engagement in VR sufficiently activate the cAMP-PKA-CREB pathway to induce synaptic growth? Without this distinction, we risk designing for fleeting recall rather than enduring learning.
