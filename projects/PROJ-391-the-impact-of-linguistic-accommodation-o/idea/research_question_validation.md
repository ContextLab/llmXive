## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks about the psychological mechanism linking linguistic style to social perception, independent of any specific model architecture or computational budget. It frames the inquiry around human-AI interaction dynamics rather than system performance metrics.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

The predictor (linguistic metrics derived from AI text) and the predicted variable (user empathy ratings) rely on independent data sources (objective text analysis vs. subjective human annotation). There is no mechanical guarantee that text features map to empathy ratings without empirical testing.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While accommodation theory predicts a positive effect in human contexts, generalizing this to AI remains an open empirical question with significant design implications. Both confirming the effect and finding a null result would inform the boundary conditions of social response theory in non-human agents.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question explicitly names the domain relationship (accommodation -> empathy perception) rather than constraining the investigation to a specific implementation constraint or budget. It asks "Does X influence Y?" rather than "Can we do X within Y resources?".

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated

All checks pass. The question is scientifically sound, non-circular, and non-trivial. It targets a genuine gap in human-AI interaction literature.
