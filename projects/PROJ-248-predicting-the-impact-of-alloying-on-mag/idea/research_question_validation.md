## Research-question validation

### Phenomenon-vs-method check

**Verdict**: pass

The question asks how alloy composition and crystal structure influence saturation magnetization and Curie temperature, and which elemental descriptors are most informative. This is a substantive scientific inquiry about material behavior, independent of any specific modeling technique or computational budget.

### Circularity check

**Verdict**: pass

Predictors are derived from composition, elemental property averages, and crystallographic descriptors (obtained from Materials Project/OQMD). The targets (saturation magnetization, Curie temperature) are measured or calculated magnetic properties not used in constructing the predictors. Thus the predictor and predicted variables come from distinct primary data sources.

### Triviality check

**Verdict**: pass

While it is known qualitatively that composition and structure affect magnetic properties, the quantitative strength of these relationships across a broad alloy space is not established. Either a strong predictive relationship (high R²) or a weak/absent one would provide valuable insight for materials discovery and theory development.

### Question-narrowing check

**Verdict**: pass

The question focuses on a domain relationship (“how do composition and structure determine magnetic properties?”) rather than on constraints of a particular algorithm or computational resource.

### Overall verdict

**Verdict**: validated
