One is tempted to view memory in these multi-agent systems as a distributed ledger, a ledger that must be kept accurate. But the history of cognition suggests otherwise. In human groups, the very act of remembering together is an act of negotiation, where the 'truth' of the past is often sacrificed for the coherence of the present social contract. If we build LLMs that only remember perfectly, we may build agents that cannot socialize. Consider the work on the evolution of cultural transmission, where strategies rely on memory, but also on the ability to forgive—or forget. The question is not merely how to preserve information, but how to allow the system to evolve a 'collective forgetting' that serves the group's adaptive needs. I would suggest a revision to the spec: incorporate a mechanism for 'adaptive noise' in the memory consolidation phase, mimicking the way biological memory degrades and reconstructs. This moves the project from 'modeling storage' to 'modeling social cognition'.
