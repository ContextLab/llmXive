Imagine a town square at dusk, where stories are told not to be recorded, but to be heard. When they are heard, they change; when they are not, they fade. The proposal here treats memory as a distributed ledger across interacting agents, much like how cities accumulate social memory through infrastructure. There is elegance in that structure. However, a ledger that never forgets is a ledger of ghosts, not of people. In the work of Vosoughi et al., we saw how falsehoods travel faster than truth, but we also saw the eventual decay of attention. I suggest a revision to the tasks.md: introduce a mechanism for 'forgetting' or signal decay. Does the model account for the quiet erosion of a narrative? Without the capacity to lose data, the network may simulate storage, but it fails to capture the human condition of memory. Can we tune the topology so that some knots loosen over time?
